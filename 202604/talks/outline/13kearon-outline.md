### Summary

Elder Patrick Kearon teaches that sustaining and serving in Church callings—whether visible or quiet—is a sacred exercise of faith, unity, and discipleship as we join the Savior in His work.

### Haiku

Hands raised in faith’s light
Serving hearts become like Christ
About His business

### Key Points

1. Common consent is a meaningful act of agency, unity, and commitment to support those called to serve.
2. Church callings stretch, bless, and refine us as we learn charity, humility, patience, and faith through service.
3. Every calling can become the Lord’s business when accepted with a willing heart and a desire to bless His children.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean to truly sustain someone beyond raising my hand?
- How have callings helped me grow in faith, humility, or love?
- When have I seen the Lord work through imperfect servants, including myself?
- How can I better support those serving in callings around me?
- What would change in my attitude if I viewed every calling as being about the Savior’s business?
