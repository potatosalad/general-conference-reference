### Summary

Elder Stevenson teaches that because every soul is precious to Heavenly Father, disciples of Jesus Christ should minister with careful, Christlike attention—especially to those assigned to us and to the rising generation—so none are forgotten, neglected, or lost.

### Haiku

Precious souls journey
Christlike hands remember all
Homeward, safely loved

### Key Points

1. Heavenly Father knows, loves, and claims each of His children as precious and never forgotten.
2. Ministering brothers and sisters are sacred stewards who watch over, strengthen, and nourish others through small acts of Christlike care.
3. The rising generation needs intentional teaching, refuge, and encouragement to know Jesus Christ, hear the Holy Ghost, and make covenants.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [Doctrine and Covenants 20:53](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/20?lang=eng&id=p53#p53) — watching over Church members and being with and strengthening them
- [Mosiah 18:8–10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p8-p10#p8) — bearing one another’s burdens and standing as witnesses of God
- [Doctrine and Covenants 68:25–28](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/68?lang=eng&id=p25-p28#p25) — parents teaching children faith, repentance, baptism, and the Holy Ghost
- [Doctrine and Covenants 93:40](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p40#p40) — bringing up children in light and truth
- [2 Nephi 25:26](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/25?lang=eng&id=p26#p26) — teaching children to look to Christ for remission of sins
- The doctrine of divine identity: each person is a beloved child of Heavenly Parents
- Ministering as Christlike caring through love, service, comfort, and covenant support
- President Russell M. Nelson’s teachings on learning to hear the Holy Ghost
- President Dieter F. Uchtdorf’s promise about teaching, nurturing, and trusting God’s miracles

### Questions to Ponder

- Who has Heavenly Father entrusted to my care right now?
- What small improvement could make my ministering more thoughtful, consistent, and Christlike?
- How can I help someone feel remembered rather than forgotten, neglected, or lost?
- What can I do to better strengthen children, youth, or young adults in their faith in Jesus Christ?
- How am I helping the rising generation learn to recognize and follow the Holy Ghost?
