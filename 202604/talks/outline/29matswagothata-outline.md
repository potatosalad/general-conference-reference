### Summary

Elder Matswagothata teaches that Jesus Christ knows each of us personally by name, succors us in our burdens, and gives us covenant power as we take His name upon ourselves and walk with Him.

### Haiku

He calls us by name
Through storms, His healing wings rise
We walk in His light

### Key Points

1. Jesus Christ knows, remembers, and calls each of us personally, no matter our circumstances or place in the world.
2. Through His Atonement, the Savior understands our pains, sorrows, and burdens and can succor and strengthen us.
3. As we take upon ourselves the name of Christ through covenants and choose to walk with Him daily, we receive His enabling power and peace.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [Exodus 33:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/ex/33?lang=eng&id=p17#p17) — “I know thee by name”
- [Alma 7:11–13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/7?lang=eng&id=p11-p13#p11) — Christ suffered pains, afflictions, temptations, sicknesses, and sins so He can succor His people
- [Doctrine and Covenants 88:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/88?lang=eng&id=p6#p6) — Jesus Christ descended below all things
- [John 17:3](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/17?lang=eng&id=p3#p3) — Eternal life is to know God and Jesus Christ
- [John 10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/10?lang=eng) — Jesus Christ as the Good Shepherd
- [John 8:12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/8?lang=eng&id=p12#p12) — Jesus Christ as the Light of the World
- [Malachi 4:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/ot/mal/4?lang=eng&id=p2#p2) — The Savior comes with healing in His wings
- Sacrament covenant: taking upon ourselves the name of Jesus Christ, always remembering Him, and keeping His commandments
- Covenant identity through baptism, the sacrament, and temple ordinances
- President D. Todd Christofferson’s teaching to get close to God during the refiner’s fire

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I felt that the Savior knew me personally and was aware of my needs?
- What helps me remember that my pain is not evidence that God has forgotten me?
- How can I more deliberately take upon myself the name of Jesus Christ each week through the sacrament?
- Who might the Lord be placing in my path so I can help them feel seen, loved, and known?
- What would change in my daily life if I asked, “Will I walk with Him?” instead of “Will He walk with me?”
