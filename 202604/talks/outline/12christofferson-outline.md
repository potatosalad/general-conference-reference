### Summary

President D. Todd Christofferson conducted a solemn assembly in which Church members worldwide sustained President Dallin H. Oaks as prophet, seer, revelator, and President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, along with his counselors and the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles.

### Haiku

Hands rise in covenant
Saints sustain the Lord’s servants
Faith joins hearts as one

### Key Points

1. A solemn assembly is a sacred occasion for the united sustaining of Church leaders.
2. Members sustain prophets, seers, and revelators by uplifted hand and by faithful support.
3. The worldwide Church acts in order and unity under priesthood keys and prophetic leadership.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [Doctrine and Covenants 20:65–67](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/20?lang=eng&id=p65-p67#p65) — common consent and ordination in the Church
- [Doctrine and Covenants 26:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/26?lang=eng&id=p2#p2) — all things shall be done by common consent in the Church
- [Doctrine and Covenants 107:22](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/107?lang=eng&id=p22#p22) — the First Presidency sustained by the confidence, faith, and prayers of the Church
- [Doctrine and Covenants 21:4–6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/21?lang=eng&id=p4-p6#p4) — receiving the prophet’s words in patience and faith
- [Doctrine and Covenants 1:38](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/1?lang=eng&id=p38#p38) — whether by the Lord’s voice or His servants’ voice, it is the same
- The principle of sustaining leaders through faith, prayer, and covenant loyalty
- The doctrine of prophets, seers, and revelators holding priesthood keys to guide the Church
