### Summary

Elder Ulisses Soares teaches that Jesus Christ is the True Vine and that our spiritual survival depends on consciously abiding in Him amid the many distracting voices of the world.

### Haiku

True Vine gives us life
Branches bear fruit as they stay
Christ’s voice guides us home

### Key Points

1. Abiding in Christ requires daily, covenant-centered choices to let His teachings shape our thoughts, words, actions, and online interactions.
2. Many worldly voices may appear attractive or even Christlike, but only Jesus Christ can nourish, redeem, and lead us to lasting joy.
3. As we invite the Savior to abide with us and heed His voice through the Holy Ghost and His servants, we receive strength, discernment, and spiritual fruit.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [John 15:1–5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/15?lang=eng&id=p1-p5#p1) — Jesus Christ is the True Vine, and we are the branches
- [1 John 2:6](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-jn/2?lang=eng&id=p6#p6) — Those who abide in Christ should walk as He walked
- [2 Corinthians 5:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/2-cor/5?lang=eng&id=p17#p17) — In Christ we become new creatures
- [Colossians 2:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/col/2?lang=eng&id=p8#p8) — Beware of philosophies and vain deceit that are not after Christ
- [Mosiah 3:17](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3?lang=eng&id=p17#p17) — Salvation comes only in and through Jesus Christ
- [Luke 24:13–35](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/24?lang=eng&id=p13-p35#p13) — The disciples on the road to Emmaus invited the risen Lord to abide with them
- The doctrine of Christ as the only source of salvation, truth, light, and spiritual nourishment
- The role of prophets and apostles as called servants who help us hear the Savior’s voice
- Spiritual discernment through the Holy Ghost in a world of media, technology, and competing voices

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean in my daily life to abide in Jesus Christ rather than only occasionally think of Him?
- Which voices or influences most often compete with the Savior’s voice in my life?
- How can I use technology and media with greater spiritual discernment?
- What fruits of the Spirit have I noticed when I have stayed connected to Christ through covenants, prayer, scripture study, and obedience?
- How can I more intentionally invite the Savior to abide with me, as the disciples did on the road to Emmaus?
