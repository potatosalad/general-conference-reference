### Summary

Brother David J. Wunderli teaches that Jesus Christ is not an added burden in our lives but the source of relief, strength, forgiveness, and hope as we intentionally walk with Him each day.

### Haiku

Heavy packs feel light
When Christ walks the path with us
Relief in His name

### Key Points

1. Satan tempts us to think life would be easier without Christ, but Jesus is our relief, not our weight.
2. We keep Christ with us through daily prayer, scripture study, faith, repentance, covenant living, and weekly sacrament worship.
3. Walking with Christ eases burdens, shapes character, brings hope, and helps us overcome life’s challenges through His Atonement.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [Moses 6:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/moses/6?lang=eng&id=p34#p34) — The Lord’s promise to Enoch: “walk with me.”
- [John 16:33](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/16?lang=eng&id=p33#p33) — Christ has overcome the world.
- [Alma 7:11–12](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/7?lang=eng&id=p11-p12#p11) — The Savior took upon Himself pains, sicknesses, afflictions, and temptations so He can succor His people.
- Book of Mormon witness of Jesus Christ as a daily source of guidance through darkness.
- The doctrine of Christ: faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, covenants, and enduring discipleship.
- President Dallin H. Oaks’s teaching: “Jesus Christ is the way.”

### Questions to Ponder

- What “rocks” have collected in my backpack, and how can I let the Savior help me carry or remove them?
- In what ways might I be tempted to think discipleship is a burden rather than a source of relief?
- What daily habits help me keep Jesus Christ with me?
- How has walking with Christ brought relief, strength, or hope in a difficult time?
- What can I do this week to abide more intentionally with the Savior?
