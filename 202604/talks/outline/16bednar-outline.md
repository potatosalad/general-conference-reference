### Summary

Elder Bednar teaches that enduring to the end is not mere perseverance but a lifelong Christlike transformation through grace, covenants, spiritual gifts, and especially charity—the pure love of Christ.

### Haiku

Grace changes the heart
Charity endures all things
Christ makes us like Him

### Key Points

1. Enduring to the end means becoming a new creature in Christ, not simply surviving hardship.
2. Spiritual gifts, especially charity, are divine blessings that help us become more like the Savior.
3. Charity is both something we do and something that can possess and transform us through the grace of Jesus Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [Doctrine and Covenants 121:29](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/121?lang=eng&id=p29#p29) — blessings promised to those who endure valiantly for the gospel
- [3 Nephi 15:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/15?lang=eng&id=p9#p9) — look unto Christ and endure to the end
- [Doctrine and Covenants 14:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/14?lang=eng&id=p7#p7) — eternal life is the greatest gift of God
- [Mosiah 3:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/3?lang=eng&id=p19#p19) — putting off the natural man through the Atonement of Christ
- [Moroni 7:45–48](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/moro/7?lang=eng&id=p45-p48#p45) — charity is the pure love of Christ and endureth forever
- [Articles of Faith 1:13](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/pgp/a-of-f/1?lang=eng&id=p13#p13) — doing good, seeking virtue, and enduring all things
- [Doctrine and Covenants 93:12–20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/93?lang=eng&id=p12-p20#p12) — receiving grace for grace and becoming like Christ
- The doctrine of spiritual rebirth and the mighty change of heart
- The principle that grace enables gradual Christlike transformation

### Questions to Ponder

- How does this talk change my understanding of the phrase “endure to the end”?
- In what ways do I sometimes think of enduring as merely surviving rather than becoming more like Christ?
- How can I more earnestly seek the spiritual gift of charity?
- What would change in my relationships if charity began to more fully possess me?
- How have covenants, ordinances, and the gift of the Holy Ghost helped transform my desires and character?
- Where do I need to rely more fully on the Savior’s grace instead of only my own effort?
