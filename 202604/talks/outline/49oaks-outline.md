### Summary

President Oaks teaches that the literal, universal Resurrection of Jesus Christ gives eternal perspective, and that disciples should prepare to meet Him by loving all—including adversaries—and choosing to be peacemakers.

### Haiku

Christ lives; death is stilled
Enemies become God’s own
Peace blooms through our words

### Key Points

1. The Resurrection of Jesus Christ is literal and universal, giving courage, hope, and eternal perspective in mortality.
2. Christ commands His followers to love God, neighbors, and even adversaries as beloved children of Heavenly Father.
3. True discipleship requires rejecting contention, using peaceful language, serving others, and seeking fairness while remaining faithful to covenants.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [Alma 11:43–44](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/alma/11?lang=eng&id=p43-p44#p43) — the universal resurrection of all people
- [Matthew 22:37–39](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/22?lang=eng&id=p37-p39#p37) — the two great commandments to love God and neighbor
- [Luke 10:29–37](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/luke/10?lang=eng&id=p29-p37#p29) — the parable of the Good Samaritan
- [Matthew 5:43–44](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/5?lang=eng&id=p43-p44#p43) — love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you
- [1 John 4:19](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/1-jn/4?lang=eng&id=p19#p19) — we love God because He first loved us
- [Matthew 5:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/5?lang=eng&id=p9#p9) and [3 Nephi 12:9](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/3-ne/12?lang=eng&id=p9#p9) — blessed are the peacemakers
- [Mosiah 5:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/5?lang=eng&id=p2#p2) — a mighty change of heart through coming unto Christ
- President Russell M. Nelson’s invitation to choose to be a peacemaker
- The doctrine that all people are children of God

### Questions to Ponder

- How does belief in a literal and universal resurrection change the way I face trials, death, or physical limitations?
- Who in my life is difficult for me to see as a beloved child of God?
- How can I show love without compromising my covenants or values?
- What words, habits, or online behaviors could I change to become more of a peacemaker?
- Where could I help reduce suffering, heal relationships, or promote understanding this week?
