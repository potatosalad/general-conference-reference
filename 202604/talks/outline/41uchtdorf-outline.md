### Summary

President Uchtdorf teaches that as we come to know for ourselves that Jesus Christ lives, we are changed, strengthened, and called to share that witness through faithful discipleship and service.

### Haiku

Empty tomb at dawn
Living Christ lights every soul
Go, see, tell, and serve

### Key Points

1. Jesus Christ is not merely a figure of history; He lives now and actively blesses, guides, comforts, and strengthens His followers.
2. Like the women at the tomb, we can move toward Christ even in grief, darkness, or unanswered questions, trusting that light and understanding will come.
3. Those who encounter the reality of the empty tomb become witnesses of the risen Christ through testimony, missionary work, discipleship, and Christlike service.

- [Mark 16:15](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/mark/16?lang=eng&id=p15#p15) — “Go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel.”
- [John 1:5](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/1?lang=eng&id=p5#p5) — The Light shines in darkness.
- [Mosiah 5:2](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/5?lang=eng&id=p2#p2) — A mighty change of heart and a disposition to do good continually.
- [2 Nephi 31:20](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/2-ne/31?lang=eng&id=p20#p20) — Pressing forward with faith in Christ.
- The doctrine of the Resurrection of Jesus Christ.
- The principle of becoming a witness of Christ through daily discipleship and service.

### Questions to Ponder

- What have I personally seen, felt, or experienced that helps me know Jesus Christ lives?
- How can I move toward the Savior’s light when I have grief, fear, or unanswered questions?
- What does it mean for me to “come and see” the empty tomb spiritually?
- How can I “go and tell” through my words, choices, service, and example?
- What courageous and humble act of discipleship could I offer this week?
