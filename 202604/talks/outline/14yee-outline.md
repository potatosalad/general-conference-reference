### Summary

Sister Kristin M. Yee teaches that ministering is not a checklist but a covenant way of becoming like Jesus Christ by loving, seeing, and serving individuals as He does.

### Haiku

One soul seen with love
Hands of Christ lift hidden hearts
Heaven heals through us

### Key Points

1. Ministering with compassion can invite real spiritual change and bless families for generations.
2. Ministering is part of our baptismal and temple covenants, helping us become more like the Savior.
3. As we love and serve others one by one, the Savior brings healing, belonging, joy, and relief to both them and us.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- [John 13:34](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/john/13?lang=eng&id=p34#p34) — “Love one another; as I have loved you.”
- [Romans 5:8](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/rom/5?lang=eng&id=p8#p8) — Christ loved and died for us while we were yet sinners.
- [1 Nephi 3:7](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/1-ne/3?lang=eng&id=p7#p7) — The Lord provides a way to accomplish what He commands.
- [Mosiah 18:8–10](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/bofm/mosiah/18?lang=eng&id=p8-p10#p8) — Baptismal covenants to bear burdens and comfort those in need.
- [Matthew 16:25](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/nt/matt/16?lang=eng&id=p25#p25) — Losing our life for the Savior’s sake helps us find it.
- Ministering as a covenant responsibility, not merely an assignment or program.
- The Savior’s pattern of blessing “the one” through personal, individual love.
- Elder Quentin L. Cook’s teaching that love of God and others tests the condition of our spirit.
- President Jeffrey R. Holland’s teaching about helping God answer prayers, dry tears, and strengthen feeble knees.

### Questions to Ponder

- How have you seen simple, consistent ministering change someone’s life?
- What helps you move past fear or awkwardness when reaching out to minister?
- How can ministering assignments help us love beyond our usual circle of family and friends?
- In what ways has serving others helped you feel the Savior’s healing and belonging?
- Who might need to feel seen, loved, or remembered by the Lord through you this week?
