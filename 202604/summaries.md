1. Introduction - President Dallin H. Oaks

The conference we convene today is different because it begins with what we call a solemn assembly.

President Dallin H. Oaks welcomes members to a unique general conference beginning with a solemn assembly to sustain newly called Church leaders and reaffirm faith in the resurrected Jesus Christ, the head of His Church.

2. Solemn Assembly - Presented by President D. Todd Christofferson



President D. Todd Christofferson conducted a solemn assembly in which Church members worldwide sustained President Dallin H. Oaks as prophet, seer, revelator, and President of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, along with his counselors and the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles.

3. About His Business - Elder Patrick Kearon

Life is better—everything is better—when we are about His business.

Elder Patrick Kearon teaches that sustaining and serving in Church callings—whether visible or quiet—is a sacred exercise of faith, unity, and discipleship as we join the Savior in His work.

4. Ministering—“That Ye Love One Another; as I Have Loved You” - Sister Kristin M. Yee

Ministering is truly loving and caring for others as the Savior would. It is a way of being; it is the way of our Savior, Jesus Christ.

Sister Kristin M. Yee teaches that ministering is not a checklist but a covenant way of becoming like Jesus Christ by loving, seeing, and serving individuals as He does.

5. Come Home - Elder Clark G. Gilbert

The Savior loves all of us and is tenderly calling for you and for me to come home.

Elder Clark G. Gilbert teaches that Jesus Christ, our Redeemer and Repairer, lovingly calls all who feel distant—because of unbelonging, inadequacy, doubt, or tradition—to come home through His grace and covenant path.

6. All Who Have Endured Valiantly - Elder David A. Bednar

The scriptural phrase “endure to the end” is a repeated reminder of the mighty spiritual change in our hearts that should be ongoing throughout our lives.

Elder Bednar teaches that enduring to the end is not mere perseverance but a lifelong Christlike transformation through grace, covenants, spiritual gifts, and especially charity—the pure love of Christ.

7. Follow the Prophet; He Knows the Way - Elder Michael John U. Teh

When we follow the teachings and examples of prophets, seers, and revelators, we are following our Savior, Jesus Christ.

Elder Michael John U. Teh teaches that following living prophets helps us follow Jesus Christ, embrace gospel culture, receive strength through the Atonement, and reflect the Savior’s light to others.

8. Tithing—Putting God First - Elder Jorge T. Becerra

I am certain that the payment of tithes and offerings will enhance our spiritual capacity as we put God first.

Elder Jorge T. Becerra teaches that paying tithes and offerings helps us overcome double-mindedness, put God first, and receive greater spiritual power and direction.

9. Prayers for Peace - President Henry B. Eyring

As we pray continually, no matter the circumstances of life, the Lord will offer us His peace and abiding support.

President Eyring teaches that continual, heartfelt prayer invites the Savior’s promised peace and strengthening support in every circumstance.

10. Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers - Presented by President D. Todd Christofferson



President D. Todd Christofferson presented releases, new callings, and sustaining votes for General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers of the Church, inviting members to express support and gratitude.

11. Church Auditing Department Report, 2025 - Presented by Jared B. Larson

To the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

The Church Auditing Department reported that 2025 Church contributions, expenditures, and assets were audited and administered according to approved budgets, policies, and sound financial principles.

12. Lost Luggage, Redeemed Souls - Elder Gary E. Stevenson

As disciples of Jesus Christ, we rise with renewed determination to succor and care for those with whom we come in contact.

Elder Stevenson teaches that because every soul is precious to Heavenly Father, disciples of Jesus Christ should minister with careful, Christlike attention—especially to those assigned to us and to the rising generation—so none are forgotten, neglected, or lost.

13. Christ—Author and Finisher of Our Faith - Elder Eduardo F. Ortega

Becoming a lifelong disciple of Christ is a process, the sum of multiple small, daily, personal and spiritual events.

Elder Eduardo F. Ortega teaches that true discipleship requires each person—whether from a long-standing Church family or newly baptized—to seek a personal testimony of Jesus Christ and steadily press forward on the covenant path.

14. “I Will Give Away All My Sins to Know Thee” - Elder Wan-Liang Wu

We can come to know the true God the Father and gain eternal life through His Son, Jesus Christ, for the power is in us.

Elder Wan-Liang Wu teaches that through sincere faith, repentance, and willingness to give up our sins, we can come to know Heavenly Father and receive eternal life through Jesus Christ.

15. Jesus Christ Is Not Our Burden; He Is Our Relief - Brother David J. Wunderli

May we together continue to walk with Jesus Christ, even in our most difficult moments.

Brother David J. Wunderli teaches that Jesus Christ is not an added burden in our lives but the source of relief, strength, forgiveness, and hope as we intentionally walk with Him each day.

16. Love All; Love Each - Elder Gérald Caussé

May we follow the Lord’s perfect example and learn to love all and to love each, just as He does.

Elder Gérald Caussé teaches that God and Jesus Christ love both all of humanity and each individual soul, and disciples show Christlike love by widening their circle while personally caring for the one.

17. Jesus Christ Is the Way - Elder Brian J. Holmes

As we bind ourselves to Him through covenant and follow His living prophet, we walk the one path that leads us safely home.

Elder Holmes teaches that Jesus Christ is both our Guide and the only covenant path home, and we stay safely bound to Him by keeping covenants and following His living prophet.

18. He Knows You by Name - Elder Clement M. Matswagothata

The most eternity-shaping name we can receive is the sacred name of Jesus Christ.

Elder Matswagothata teaches that Jesus Christ knows each of us personally by name, succors us in our burdens, and gives us covenant power as we take His name upon ourselves and walk with Him.

19. Jesus Christ—the True Vine - Elder Ulisses Soares

In a world of many voices, remaining connected to the True Vine is not merely desirable; it is essential to our spiritual survival.

Elder Ulisses Soares teaches that Jesus Christ is the True Vine and that our spiritual survival depends on consciously abiding in Him amid the many distracting voices of the world.

20. Encounter at the Empty Tomb - President Dieter F. Uchtdorf

We must encounter the empty tomb, experience the reality of what it means, and, in turn, share that witness with others.

President Uchtdorf teaches that as we come to know for ourselves that Jesus Christ lives, we are changed, strengthened, and called to share that witness through faithful discipleship and service.

21. Best Days and Worst Days - President Emily Belle Freeman

Because of Jesus Christ, no matter how bad things are right now, your best days are ahead.

President Emily Belle Freeman teaches that as we walk with Jesus Christ through both best and worst days, eternal truths, divine invitations, and promised blessings strengthen us and assure us our best days are ahead.

22. I Feel My Savior’s Love - Elder Pedro X. Larreal

It is my prayer that each of us will feel our Savior’s love as we partake of the sacrament.

Elder Larreal teaches that as we prepare for and partake of the sacrament reverently, we can feel the Savior’s personal love and receive the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost.

23. Choose Jesus Christ as Your Guide - Elder Edward B. Rowe

I need Jesus Christ to be my guide through life’s journey amid its spiritual land mines. All of us do.

Elder Rowe teaches that because life contains unseen spiritual dangers, we must choose Jesus Christ as our perfect, personal guide and trust His power to heal us when we stumble.

24. He Is Risen - Elder Ronald A. Rasband

With His Resurrection, Jesus Christ secured salvation from physical death for us, all of God’s children throughout the ages.

Elder Ronald A. Rasband testifies that Jesus Christ’s Resurrection is central to God’s plan, conquers physical death for all, and brings hope of joyful, perfected reunions through Him.

25. Because of Jesus Christ - Elder Dale G. Renlund

Focusing on the Savior, His infinite Atonement, and what He has done for us will bring joy and clarity into our lives.

Elder Renlund teaches that focusing on Jesus Christ and His infinite Atonement brings clarity, joy, forgiveness, healing, and hope amid life’s many overwhelming concerns.

26. The Joy of a Covenant Relationship with God - Elder Thierry K. Mutombo

Wherever you are now or whatever you are going through in your life, you can feel God’s infinite love manifested through His Son, Jesus Christ.

Elder Mutombo teaches that through Jesus Christ’s Atonement and Resurrection, covenant faithfulness brings divine love, strength in sorrow, and lasting joy with God.

27. A Peculiar Treasure - Elder Alan R. Walker

The Lord declared that His people would be a peculiar treasure unto Him if they would obey His voice and keep His covenant.

Elder Walker teaches that those who obey the Savior’s voice and faithfully keep their covenants become the Lord’s “peculiar treasure,” receiving His acceptance, strength, and covenant blessings.

28. Alive in Christ - President Dallin H. Oaks

Let us follow Christ by forgoing contention and by using the language and methods of peacemakers.

President Oaks teaches that the literal, universal Resurrection of Jesus Christ gives eternal perspective, and that disciples should prepare to meet Him by loving all—including adversaries—and choosing to be peacemakers.

29. The Character of Christ - President D. Todd Christofferson

If we are to succeed in developing a Christlike character, we must possess His motivations—His thoughts, desires, and intents of the heart.

President Christofferson teaches that preparing for the Savior’s return means receiving a mighty change of heart so our motivations—and therefore our character—become like Jesus Christ’s.

30. Remember, Remember - Elder Chi Hong (Sam) Wong

Every promise of redemption, every hope of resurrection, and every assurance of eternal life flows through Jesus Christ.

Elder Chi Hong (Sam) Wong teaches that the Book of Mormon’s repeated invitation to “remember, remember” points us to obedience, agency, covenants, and salvation through Jesus Christ.

31. I Glory in My Jesus - Elder Aaron T. Hall

Nephi and Ammon inspire me to deepen my relationship with Jesus Christ and to rejoice in the many ways we can glory in Him.

Elder Aaron T. Hall invites us to deepen our personal relationship with Jesus Christ by glorying in His divine names, redeeming power, mercy, healing, and covenant love.

32. “Here Am I, Send Me” - President Susan H. Porter

Children all over the world are offering what they have to bring God’s love to their families, church, friends, and communities.

President Susan H. Porter teaches children that, because of Heavenly Father’s plan and Jesus Christ’s Atonement, they can offer their gifts and say, “Here am I, send me,” to help bring others to Christ.

33. Eternal Marriage Is an Eternal Journey - Elder Neil L. Andersen

We believe in eternal marriage, we teach of eternal marriage, and we seek an eternal marriage.

Elder Neil L. Andersen teaches that eternal marriage, made possible through Christ’s Resurrection and restored sealing power, is a sacred covenant journey of becoming more like the Savior through faith, patience, sacrifice, and enduring love.

34. Keys, Covenants, and Easter - Elder Quentin L. Cook

The keys committed by ancient prophets to the Prophet Joseph Smith in the Kirtland Temple are an essential part of the restored gospel.

Elder Cook testifies that through Jesus Christ’s Atonement and Resurrection and the restored priesthood keys of sealing, God’s children can make and keep covenants that unite families eternally and bring them back to the Father.

35. Come unto Christ—Together - Elder Taniela B. Wakolo

I invite you to make a commitment and choose to come unto Christ—together. Eternity shall be your reward.

Elder Wakolo invites all—especially part-member families and waiting loved ones—to come unto Christ together through patient faith, sacred agency, temple covenants, and trust in God’s timing.

36. ’Tis Eastertide: No One Walks Alone - Elder Gerrit W. Gong

As we walk by faith with the Lord, abiding in Him, and He in us, we come to know He lives.

Elder Gong teaches that the resurrected Jesus Christ walks with us on our personal roads to Emmaus through scripture, sacrament, covenants, the Holy Ghost, and loving fellowship.

37. Closing Remarks - President Dallin H. Oaks

Truly, Jesus Christ is the way to peace in this world and eternal life in the world to come.

President Oaks testified that Jesus Christ is the way to peace and eternal life, inviting members to study conference messages, act in faith, strengthen families, and receive personal revelation.
