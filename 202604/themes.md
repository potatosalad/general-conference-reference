### Summary

This conference centered powerfully on Jesus Christ—His Resurrection, Atonement, living reality, and role as the only way to peace, healing, covenant belonging, and eternal life. Speakers repeatedly invited Saints to come unto Christ, abide in Him, follow His living prophet, and deepen personal discipleship through daily faith, repentance, prayer, sacrament worship, ministering, and covenant keeping.

The solemn assembly emphasized unity in sustaining the Lord’s chosen servants, while many messages focused on becoming more Christlike in heart and action: loving the one, welcoming those who feel distant, serving faithfully, giving up sin, seeking peace, and walking together toward temple and family blessings made possible through the restored gospel.

- Jesus Christ lives; His Resurrection is literal, universal, and central to God’s plan of salvation.
- Jesus Christ is the only way to lasting peace, healing, forgiveness, covenant belonging, and eternal life.
- Discipleship is a daily, lifelong process of becoming like Christ through faith, repentance, charity, and enduring covenant faithfulness.
- Covenants bind us to the Savior, give spiritual power, and lead individuals and families safely home to Heavenly Father.
- Following and sustaining living prophets is an expression of faith in Jesus Christ and helps protect us spiritually.
- Ministering means loving, seeing, and serving individuals as the Savior does, not merely completing assignments.
- God knows and loves each soul personally; no one is forgotten, and all are invited to come home to Christ.
- Prayer, sacrament worship, scripture study, and the Holy Ghost help us abide in Christ amid the noise and dangers of the world.
- Christlike love includes widening our circle, caring for the one, avoiding contention, and becoming peacemakers.
- Temple covenants, priesthood keys, and sealing power bless families eternally through Jesus Christ.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I more intentionally make Jesus Christ the center of my daily thoughts, choices, and relationships?
- What does it mean for me to truly sustain the prophet and follow prophetic counsel with faith?
- Who around me may feel distant, forgotten, or unwelcome—and how can I help them feel the Savior’s love?
- What sin, habit, or attitude am I willing to give away in order to know God more fully?
- How can my sacrament worship become a deeper weekly encounter with the Savior’s love and grace?
- In what ways can I become more of a peacemaker in my home, ward, workplace, or online interactions?
- How are my covenants giving me strength, identity, and direction on the path back to Heavenly Father?
- What small daily actions are helping me develop the character and motivations of Jesus Christ?
