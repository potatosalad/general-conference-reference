## 1. Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers - Presented by President Dallin H. Oaks

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/11oaks?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/11oaks?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Dallin H. Oaks led the sustaining of the First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, General Authority Seventies, Area Seventies, and General Officers, acknowledged releases and emeritus designations, and affirmed the Church’s governance by common consent under priesthood keys.

### Haiku

Hands lift like one heart,
Common consent binds our faith—
Keys guide us in love.

### Key Points

1. Common consent and sustaining. Members express support for leaders “in the usual way,” with a respectful process for registering concerns through proper priesthood channels, reflecting agency, order, and unity in the Church.
2. Priesthood keys and orderly governance. The First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve, and quorums of the Seventy function under priesthood keys; calls, releases, and reorganizations show the Lord’s pattern of leadership by councils and continuing revelation.
3. Gratitude, continuity, and worldwide service. The Church honors released and emeritus leaders and welcomes new leaders—including General Authority and Area Seventies and a new Sunday School General Presidency—demonstrating ongoing growth and the continuity of the Lord’s work across the earth.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 26:2 — All things to be done by common consent.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:65 — Callings sustained by the vote of the Church.
- Doctrine and Covenants 1:38 — The Lord’s voice through His servants.
- Doctrine and Covenants 107:22–24, 33–35 — Quorums, councils, and priesthood keys in Church governance.
- Mosiah 18:21 — Being “of one faith and one baptism,” with hearts knit in unity.
- Articles of Faith 1:5 — Authority by priesthood and calling.
- Gospel Topics/Handbook themes: Common Consent; Priesthood Keys; Prophets, Seers, and Revelators; Councils in the Church.
- Related talks:
  - Russell M. Nelson, “Sustaining the Prophets” (Oct. 2014).
  - Dallin H. Oaks, “The Keys and Authority of the Priesthood” (Apr. 2014).
  - Russell M. Nelson, “Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives” (Apr. 2018).

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean to sustain beyond raising my hand—how can I pray for, defend, and follow the counsel of inspired leaders?
- How does common consent protect agency and promote unity in the Lord’s Church?
- In what ways do priesthood keys operating through councils bless me and my family?
- If I have questions or concerns, how can I address them faithfully and appropriately within the Lord’s established order?
- How can I show gratitude to released leaders and actively support newly called leaders in my ward and stake?
- What recent adjustments or calls invite me to seek personal revelation about my own role in building the kingdom?
- How can I better teach my family the purpose and pattern of sustaining in the Church?

## 2. Church Auditing Department Report, 2023 - Presented by Jared B. Larson

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/12larson?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/12larson?lang=eng)

### Summary

The 2023 Church Auditing Department Report affirms that sacred funds were authorized, recorded, and used according to revealed governance, approved budgets and policies, and prudent financial principles of provident living.

### Haiku

Stewards of tithes stand—  
faith, trust, and discipline keep  
covenant funds safe.

### Key Points

1) Revealed financial governance: By direction in Doctrine and Covenants 120, the Council on the Disposition of the Tithes (First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, and Presiding Bishopric) authorizes how Church funds are used.

2) Independent assurance: The Church Auditing Department—staffed by credentialed professionals and independent of other departments—provides reasonable assurance that contributions, expenditures, and assets are properly recorded and safeguarded.

3) Provident living in practice: The Church models the principles it teaches—living within a budget, avoiding debt, and saving for times of need—while administering sacred funds.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 120 — The Council on the Disposition of the Tithes.
- Doctrine and Covenants 119 — The law of tithing as a standing law.
- Malachi 3:10 — Promises and blessings associated with tithing.
- Luke 16:10–12 — Faithfulness in small things and true stewardship.
- Doctrine and Covenants 104:11–13 — Stewardship and accountability before God.
- Gospel principles: Stewardship; Accountability; Common consent and council governance; Provident living (budgeting, avoiding debt, prudent saving); Integrity and transparency in handling sacred funds.

### Questions to Ponder

- How does revealed, council-based governance (D&C 120) shape my trust in how the Church administers sacred funds?
- In what ways can I better mirror the Church’s example of provident living—budgeting, avoiding unnecessary debt, and saving for future needs?
- What does being a faithful steward over “small things” (Luke 16) look like in my personal and family finances?
- How does paying tithing strengthen my covenant relationship with the Lord and my trust in His promises?
- What systems or habits can I establish to increase accountability and transparency in my own stewardship (time, talents, resources)?
- How might family councils apply these principles to set financial priorities guided by prayer and revelation?

### Summary

President Jeffrey R. Holland bears personal witness that God hears every prayer and answers in His perfect timing, urging us—through his recent loss, illness, and renewed consecration—to pray like the Savior and live as covenant-keeping disciples preparing for eternal life.

### Haiku

A hidden fire moves,  
Morning, noon, night—pleading faith;  
His timing is love.

### Key Points

1) Prayer that changes us: God hears every prayer and answers each according to His love and timetable; persistent, sincere prayer—vocal or silent—draws heaven’s help and protection, especially amid grief and trial.

2) Pray as the Savior prayed: Jesus prayed often, in many places and postures, and “more earnestly” in Gethsemane; when we don’t know how to pray, we begin and the Holy Ghost guides our petitions—even for enemies.

3) Urgent, covenant discipleship: The Lord seeks disciples He “knows”—faithful, covenant-keeping Saints who rely on angels, priesthood power, prophets, revelation, and Christ’s grace, coming boldly to the “throne of grace” as we prepare for eternal life.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Luke 18:1–8 — The importuning widow: pray always and not faint.  
- Alma 34:17–27 — Pray continually, in homes, for “those who are around you.”  
- James 5:16 — The fervent prayer of the righteous “availeth much.”  
- D&C 10:5; 3 Nephi 18:15 — Pray always to overcome temptation.  
- Romans 8:26 — The Spirit helps when we “know not what we should pray for.”  
- Matthew 5:44 — Pray for enemies and those who despitefully use you.  
- Luke 9:29; Matthew 26:39; Luke 22:44; D&C 19:18 — The Savior’s varied, earnest prayers; His Atonement and “blood from every pore.”  
- Matthew 7:21–23 (JST Matt. 7:23) — Being truly known by Christ through covenant discipleship.  
- Hebrews 4:16; Hebrews 12:22–24 — Come boldly to the throne of grace; Mount Zion, the city of the living God.  
- Job 42:3 — “Things too wonderful” God reveals to the refined faithful.  
- Hymns: “Prayer Is the Soul’s Sincere Desire” (no. 145); “Sweet Hour of Prayer” (no. 142).  
- Principle: Gratitude as holy thought—“gratitude is happiness doubled by wonder.”

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I recognized God answering differently than I asked? How did I feel His love and timing in it?  
- What simple changes can make my prayers more frequent, specific, and sincere (morning/day/night; vocal and in my heart)?  
- How can I let the Holy Ghost guide my prayers when I don’t know what to say—or when praying for those who have hurt me?  
- In what ways can I use prayer as a shield against temptation this week?  
- What “cross” will I more earnestly take up to be known by the Savior as His covenant disciple?  
- How can I invite collective faith (family prayer, fasting, temple prayer rolls, priesthood blessings) into my current challenges?  
- Which prophetic teachings (including President Nelson’s) will I act on now to live with greater urgency and consecration?  
- How will I “come boldly to the throne of grace” this week to receive mercy, help, and spiritual power?

## 4. Put Ye On the Lord Jesus Christ - Sister J. Anette Dennis

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/14dennis?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/14dennis?lang=eng)

### Summary

Sister J. Anette Dennis teaches that making and keeping sacred covenants—symbolized in baptism, the sacrament, temple ordinances, and wearing the garment of the holy priesthood—helps us “put on” Jesus Christ, deepening our covenant (hesed) relationship with God and inviting His protection, power, and guidance.

### Haiku

Clothed in covenant,  
armor of light, Christ within—  
love’s covering stays.

### Key Points

1) Covenants deepen our relationship with God  
- Covenants invite a hesed (covenantal love) relationship, allowing God to pour out promised blessings, change our nature through Christ’s Atonement, and draw us closer to Him.

2) Ordinances and symbols help us “put on Christ”  
- Baptism symbolizes death and rebirth in Christ; the sacrament symbolizes receiving His flesh and blood; temple covenants center on the Savior, and wearing the garment daily reminds us that His Atonement “covers” us—becoming an armor of light.

3) Live the covenant path—personally and intentionally  
- God sets covenant terms; we witness our willingness through sacred actions. Wearing the garment is a personal sign to God, not to others. All are invited to prepare for temple covenants (not limited to missionaries or engaged couples) to receive increased power and capacity in Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Romans 13:12–14 — “Put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ”; the “armor of light.”
- Galatians 3:27 — “As many of you as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ.”
- Genesis 3:21 — Coats of skins as covering; pointing to Christ’s atoning “covering” (kaphar).
- Mosiah 18:8–10 — Baptismal covenant and belonging to Christ.
- 3 Nephi 18:1–12 — The sacrament as remembrance of Christ’s body and blood.
- Ephesians 6:11–13 — Whole armor of God; protection through righteousness and faith.
- John 1:29 — Jesus Christ, the Lamb of God.
- Gospel principles: The covenant path; hesed (covenant love); ordinances as teaching symbols; the Atonement as a covering; sacred nature of the garment of the holy priesthood as a personal sign to God.
- President Russell M. Nelson’s teachings on covenants and temples (e.g., Let God Prevail; The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation; Overcome the World and Find Rest; Think Celestial!).

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean for me, today, to “put on the Lord Jesus Christ” in thought, word, and action?
- How do the symbols in baptism, the sacrament, and the temple deepen my understanding of Christ and my covenants?
- In what ways have I felt God’s hesed—His covenantal love—because I have kept my covenants?
- How does wearing the garment of the holy priesthood influence my discipleship and daily choices?
- What protections, power, or guidance have I noticed as I strive to honor my covenants?
- If I have not yet received my endowment, what steps can I take with my bishop/branch president to prepare worthily for temple covenants?
- How can I better use the sacrament each week to renew my covenant relationship and invite Christ’s power into my life?

## 5. Pillars and Rays - Elder Alexander Dushku

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/15dushku?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/15dushku?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Dushku teaches that most testimonies grow through many small, consistent “rays” of revelation rather than one dramatic “pillar of light,” and as we remember and gather these rays, our faith in Jesus Christ becomes bright enough to dispel darkness.

### Haiku

Pillars start as rays;  
Line upon line, light grows bright—  
Hear Him in small peace.

### Key Points

1. Rays, not fireworks: God’s common pattern is to reveal truth “line upon line”—through quiet, continuous rays of light—so we should not be discouraged if we haven’t had spectacular spiritual experiences.
2. Notice and gather your rays: The Spirit speaks in varied, personal ways (peace to mind, impressions to mind and heart, scripture that “speaks,” love in service, remembered tender mercies); as we recognize, record, and remember these moments, “light cleaveth unto light” and our testimony strengthens.
3. Live worthy of continual light: As covenant disciples, we are “living in revelation”; with faith, repentance, and diligence, our accumulated rays grow “brighter and brighter until the perfect day,” pointing us to the Father and the Son.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Joseph Smith—History 1:15–17: The First Vision and the “pillar of light.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:22–23: “Did I not speak peace to your mind…?”
- Doctrine and Covenants 8:2–3: Revelation comes to mind and heart by the Holy Ghost.
- Doctrine and Covenants 50:24: Light grows “brighter and brighter until the perfect day.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:46; John 1:9: The Spirit/Light of Christ gives light to every person.
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:12–13, 40: Light fills the immensity of space; “light cleaveth unto light… truth embraceth truth.”
- Alma 32:28–43: Nurturing a seed of faith—experimenting, remembering, and growing.
- 1 Nephi 1:20; Elder David A. Bednar, “The Tender Mercies of the Lord” (Apr 2005): Recognizing God’s personalized mercies.
- Elder David A. Bednar, teachings on “living in revelation.”
- President Spencer W. Kimball’s counsel to avoid expecting only the spectacular and to notice the steady flow of revelation.
- President Russell M. Nelson’s invitation to “immerse [ourselves] in the glorious light of the Restoration.”

Gospel principles:
- The Light of Christ and the Gift of the Holy Ghost
- Personal revelation: patterns, patience, and discernment
- “Line upon line” growth and spiritual memory (recording sacred experiences)
- Faith, repentance, and honoring covenants as conduits for light
- Choosing Christ’s light over worldly darkness

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I recently felt a “ray” of spiritual light—however small—and how can I better remember and record it?
- Do I sometimes expect the spectacular and miss the steady flow of the Spirit? How can I adjust my expectations?
- Which channels of revelation (scripture, prayer, service, ordinances, music, teaching, quiet thoughts) most often bring me light?
- How does honoring my covenants increase my capacity to perceive and receive revelation?
- In times of anxiety, depression, or spiritual dryness, how can I recognize “tender mercies” in hindsight?
- What practices could I begin this week (journaling, regular temple worship, focused questions in prayer) to gather my rays “together in one”?
- How can I help family members or class members identify and value their own rays of testimony?
- What is one choice I can make today to “choose Him over the darkness of the world”?

## 6. Covenant Confidence through Jesus Christ - Elder Ulisses Soares

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/16soares?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/16soares?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Ulisses Soares teaches that sacred temple covenants in Jesus Christ give us covenant confidence—power, assurance, and transformation—to face today’s challenges and become higher and holier disciples.

### Haiku

Temple light within,  
Covenants kindle courage—  
Stand and be not moved.

### Key Points

1) Covenant confidence through Christ: Making and keeping temple covenants fills us with quiet, certain assurance in God’s promises, strengthening us to overcome doubt, fear, and adversity while increasing love and service.

2) Preparation changes experience, which transforms life: As we humbly center on the Savior, pursue personal worthiness, and prepare intentionally for the temple, our temple worship deepens—and that divine power flows into our daily living.

3) Live temple truths daily: Being “temple bound” means securing ourselves to Jesus Christ—scheduling regular worship, removing unkind feelings to retain the Spirit, and carrying the temple’s holiness into our homes and relationships.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Hebrews 10:35 — “Cast not away therefore your confidence.”
- 1 Nephi 3:15; 4 — Nephi’s covenant confidence to obtain the plates.
- 1 Nephi 14:14 — Saints “armed with righteousness and with the power of God.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 45:32 — Stand in holy places and be not moved.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:45 — “Then shall thy confidence wax strong in the presence of God.”
- President Russell M. Nelson, Closing Remarks (Oct. 2019) — Personal worthiness and total conversion in preparing for the temple.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation” (Oct. 2021) — The temple at the center of faith and spiritual fortitude.
- Gospel principles: Holiness to the Lord; ordinances of salvation and exaltation; discipleship that is higher and holier; continual preparation and frequent temple worship; purifying our hearts from unkindness to retain the Spirit.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does covenant confidence look like in my life this week, and how does it change how I face a specific trial?
- How can I change my preparation so my next temple visit changes my experience and transforms my life outside the temple?
- Which unkind feelings or habits do I need to discard so the Spirit can be “unrestrained” in my life and home?
- In what practical ways can my family become more “temple bound” (e.g., scheduling our next appointment, family temple preparation)?
- How am I centering my discipleship more fully on Jesus Christ and His atoning power as I make and keep covenants?
- How can I help children and youth develop joyful, confident anticipation for temple worship and lifelong covenant keeping?

## 7. Integrity: A Christlike Attribute - Elder Jack N. Gerard

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/17gerard?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/17gerard?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Jack N. Gerard teaches that integrity is a Christlike attribute modeled perfectly by the Savior and invites us to live true to God, to others, and to our divine identity—especially amid the world’s pull.

### Haiku

Choose truth, come what may  
Christ drank the bitter cup, whole—  
Walk in covenant.

### Key Points

1) True to God  
   - Integrity begins with loving God and embracing absolute truth; we resist worldliness, trust the doctrine of Christ, and “do what is right” even when it sets us apart.

2) True to Others  
   - Integrity shows in consistent words and deeds, especially in our councils and callings; we avoid self-interest, the honors of men, and any appearance of impropriety while seeking the Lord’s will together.

3) True to Our Divine Identity  
   - Remembering who we are in God’s plan guards against self-deception and apostasy; like Joseph and Hyrum, we remain faithful to revealed truth and who the Lord is shaping us to become.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Luke 22:42; Doctrine and Covenants 19:18–19 — The Savior’s integrity in Gethsemane; He “did not shrink.”  
- John 16:33 — “Be of good cheer; I have overcome the world.”  
- Daniel 6 — Daniel’s integrity and God’s deliverance.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 1:16 — Warning against walking “in [our] own way.”  
- Alma 30 (Korihor) — The danger of denying known truth and self-deception.  
- Joseph Smith—History 1:25 — “I knew it, and I knew that God knew it.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 124:15 — Hyrum and “the integrity of his heart.”  
- Job 31:6 — “Let me be weighed in an even balance.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Overcome the World and Find Rest” (Oct. 2022).  
- President Dallin H. Oaks, “The Two Great Commandments” (Oct. 2018).  
- President Spencer W. Kimball on integrity (Teachings/addresses).  
- Preach My Gospel (updated): Integrity added as a Christlike attribute.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where is the world subtly pulling me to lower my standards, and how will I respond with integrity?  
- If my recent choices were made public, would they reflect my covenants and love for God?  
- In councils and callings, how can I better seek the Lord’s will and avoid the “honors of men”?  
- What does being “true to others” look like online, in private messages, and in anonymous posts?  
- How does remembering my divine identity change the way I speak, decide, and act under pressure?  
- What is my “lions’ den” right now, and how will I show faith like Daniel?  
- When have I felt the temptation to shrink, and how did the Savior’s example help me press forward?

## 8. All Will Be Well Because of Temple Covenants - President Henry B. Eyring

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/18eyring?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/18eyring?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Henry B. Eyring testifies that making and keeping temple covenants brings the Savior’s peace, power, revelation, and enduring family bonds so we can face any trial with confidence that, through Jesus Christ, all will be well.

### Haiku

Floodwaters rise, still—  
Covenants bind hearts with peace;  
In His house, all’s well.

### Key Points

1. Temple covenants bring Christ’s peace amid adversity.

2. Keeping covenants invites power, purity, and revelation.  
   - Frequent temple worship creates a lifelong pattern of devotion that draws the Holy Ghost, increases access to the power of Jesus Christ, and steadies us on the covenant path.

3. Sealing ordinances anchor eternal families and guide life choices.  
   - Temple covenants turn hearts within families, protect against pride and selfishness, and prepare us to respond to prophetic direction—“walk away easily” when the Lord calls.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 14:27 — The Savior’s peace that dispels fear.  
- Malachi 4:5–6; Doctrine and Covenants 2 — Hearts turned to fathers and children through Elijah’s sealing power.  
- Matthew 16:19; Doctrine and Covenants 132:19 — Keys that bind on earth and in heaven; eternal marriage.  
- 2 Corinthians 12:7–10 — “Thorn in the flesh”; grace in trials.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 4:2 — Serve with all your heart, might, mind, and strength; covenant devotion.  
- President Russell M. Nelson:  
  - “The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation” (Oct 2021) — Everything we believe and every promise converge in the temple.  
  - “Let God Prevail” (Oct 2020) and subsequent teachings — Covenants remove us from “neutral ground” and bind us to God with love and mercy.  
  - Teachings on increased access to the power of Jesus Christ through temple covenants.  
- Gospel principles: Sealing and eternal families; companionship of the Holy Ghost; personal revelation in holy temples; covenant loyalty that protects against pride and selfishness; family prayer and service softening hearts.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I felt the Savior’s peace specifically because of my temple covenants?  
- What practical patterns can I adopt (or renew) to attend the temple more frequently and to remember my covenants daily?  
- How do my covenants help me receive and recognize personal revelation during life’s crises?  
- In what ways can I foster “hearts turning” in my family through prayer, service, and temple work?  
- What does “live so that when the call comes, you can walk away easily” mean for my current priorities?  
- How does living “inside” my covenants increase my access to the power of Jesus Christ?  
- What attitudes of pride or selfishness do my covenants invite me to discard to strengthen my marriage and family?  
- If I am not currently temple worthy or endowed, what is my next faithful step on the covenant path?

## 9. “Be Still, and Know That I Am God” - Elder David A. Bednar

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/21bednar?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/21bednar?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder David A. Bednar teaches that “being still” is a covenant-rooted focus on Jesus Christ that anchors our lives to His rock through sacred times and holy places—especially Sabbath, temple, and home—so the Holy Ghost can steady us to do and overcome hard things.

### Haiku

Be still; know your God.  
Build on the Redeemer’s rock—  
Sabbath, temple, home.

### Key Points

1) Be Still
- Stillness is more than silence; it is an inner spiritual steadiness that comes as we remember and rely on God “at all times and in all things, and in all places,” despite life’s noise and commotion.

2) Build Upon the Rock
- We must actively build our personal foundation upon Jesus Christ through covenants and ordinances; these covenant connections act like anchors to bedrock, giving us hope, stability, and the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost.

3) Sacred Times and Holy Places—Especially the Home
- The Sabbath, meetinghouses, and temples are holy settings that teach and renew stillness in our souls, and our homes should be the ultimate blend of sacred time and holy place where Church and home are gathered together in Christ so we can face trials without distress or despair.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Psalm 46:10; Doctrine and Covenants 101:16 — “Be still, and know that I am God.”
- Helaman 5:12 — Build your foundation upon the rock of Christ.
- Ether 12:4 — Hope in Christ as “an anchor to the souls of men.”
- Moroni 7:28 — Christ advocates the cause of the children of men.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:45–46 — Virtue garnish thy thoughts; confidence before God; the Holy Ghost as constant companion.
- Doctrine and Covenants 59:9–13 — Sabbath worship and offering the sacrament as sacred time and sign to God.
- 2 Nephi 31:20 — A perfect brightness of hope through steadfastness in Christ.
- 2 Corinthians 4:8–9 — Troubled yet not distressed; persecuted but not forsaken.

Gospel principles to study:
- Covenant connection to Jesus Christ through ordinances.
- The Sabbath as a sign of our love for God.
- The temple as a holy place to learn eternal truths and receive power.
- The home as the primary place of spiritual formation and enduring stillness.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does “be still” look like in my daily rhythm beyond simply being quiet? What will I stop or start this week to cultivate inner stillness?
- How are my covenants functioning as “anchor pins” to connect my life to Christ’s rock? How do I review, remember, and renew them?
- In what specific ways can my Sabbath be a clearer sign to God of my love and devotion?
- How can our home better combine sacred time and holy place? What one small, consistent change would invite more of the Spirit?
- How has temple worship helped me “know and remember” God? What will I do to attend and serve more deliberately?
- When “mighty winds” blow, which parts of my spiritual foundation hold firm and which need reinforcing?
- How do I recognize the Holy Ghost’s gift of calm assurance? What conditions in my life help me feel it more consistently?
- What’s the difference between assuming Christ is my foundation and intentionally building upon Him each day?

## 10. Rise! He Calleth Thee - Elder Massimo De Feo

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/22de-feo?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/22de-feo?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder De Feo teaches that the gospel gives us clear spiritual vision amid trials as we focus on Jesus Christ, cast off the natural man, and heed His voice with real intent to follow Him.

### Haiku

Crowds hush, he cries out  
Cast off the beggar’s old coat—  
Christ clears our true sight

### Key Points

1. Focus on Jesus Christ and stand true to revealed truth  
   - Like Bartimaeus, disciples raise their witness above the world’s noise, stay faithful to covenants, and act in faith rather than be acted upon.

2. Cast off the natural man and rise through covenants  
   - Throwing away the “beggar’s coat” symbolizes repenting, rejecting excuses, and choosing a new life in Christ by making and keeping sacred covenants.

3. Hear His voice and follow with real intent  
   - We come to Jesus by listening to His voice amid other voices; miracles and spiritual clarity come as we follow Him “in the way” with the intent to keep following.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Mark 10:46–52 — Bartimaeus cries out, casts away his garment, receives sight, and follows Jesus.  
- 2 Nephi 2:14, 26–27 — Act and not be acted upon; agency to choose liberty in Christ.  
- Mosiah 3:19 — Put off the natural man and become a saint through the Atonement.  
- Matthew 14:28–31 — Peter walks on water when his focus stays on the Savior.  
- John 10:27 — “My sheep hear my voice.”  
- Moroni 10:4, 7 — Ask with real intent; miracles come by faith.  
- Gospel principles: Covenants and the covenant path; Repentance; Faith to act; Real intent; Discipleship and testimony; Spiritual vision versus worldly voices.  
- Related teachings: President Russell M. Nelson’s invitations to “Hear Him” and to stay on the covenant path.

### Questions to Ponder

- What voices in my life try to silence or dilute my testimony of Jesus Christ? How can I raise my witness above them?  
- What is my “beggar’s coat”—habits, excuses, or identities I need to cast off to rise in Christ?

## 30. Bridging the Two Great Commandments - Elder Gary E. Stevenson

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/53stevenson?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/53stevenson?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Gary E. Stevenson teaches that discipleship requires balanced, equal devotion to both “love God” and “love your neighbor,” like twin towers of a suspension bridge on which all gospel living depends.

### Haiku

Twin towers of love  
Upward, outward, bound by grace—  
Law and prophets hang.

### Key Points

1. Love the Lord with all your heart, soul, and mind  
   - We show love for God by keeping His commandments—through prayer, scripture study, temple worship, tithing, Sabbath observance, virtue, and daily small acts of devotion that keep us on the covenant path; such obedience leads to lasting happiness.

2. Love your neighbor as yourself  
   - Christlike love reaches all of God’s children regardless of background or circumstance; we actively minister, forgive, lift the weak, and serve—even when it’s inconvenient—seeking out the marginalized and the wounded.

3. Keep the two great commandments in balanced interdependence  
   - Like a suspension bridge, both “towers” must be equally strong: zeal for commandments without charity or love without accountability to God both lead to imbalance; intentional, incremental efforts create a “blueprint” for a discipleship that is both true and kind.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 22:36–40 — The two great commandments; “On these two hang all the law and the prophets.”
- John 14:15 — “If ye love me, keep my commandments.”
- Mosiah 2:41 — The “blessed and happy state” of those who keep the commandments.
- 2 Nephi 26:33 — “All are alike unto God.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 81:5; Isaiah 35:3 — “Succor the weak, lift up the hands which hang down, and strengthen the feeble knees.”
- Luke 10:25–37 — The good Samaritan as a model for loving our neighbor.
- Related teachings: President Russell M. Nelson on living the second great commandment as key to true discipleship; President Dallin H. Oaks on balancing love of God and love of neighbor.
- Gospel principles: covenant loyalty, charity, ministering, repentance and forgiveness, absolute truth and accountability to God, incremental growth through righteous habits.

### Questions to Ponder

- Which “tower” needs strengthening in my life right now—love of God or love of neighbor?
- What specific daily practices will help me love God with all my heart, soul, and mind?
- Who is a neighbor I can lift this week, even if it is inconvenient?
- How can I hold to absolute truth and covenants while showing Christlike compassion to those who choose differently than I do?
- What grudges or judgments should I let go of, and whom do I need to forgive?
- How does the truth that “all are alike unto God” refine my attitudes and language?
- In what ways do temple worship, prayer, and scripture study increase my capacity to minister?
- What simple “blueprint” (two or three concrete goals) will help me keep the two commandments with equal devotion?

Opposition tests—

3) Afflictions are consecrated for our gain

- 2 Nephi 2:5—We are “instructed sufficiently to know good from evil.”  
- Moroni 7:16—The Spirit of Christ is given to all to discern good and evil.  
- Alma 7:11–12—Christ takes upon Him our pains and sicknesses and knows how to succor us.  
- Helaman 5:12—Build on the rock of our Redeemer so the whirlwind has no power.  
- 3 Nephi 9:14—“Whosoever will come, him will I receive.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 122:7—“All these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:36—“Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Think Celestial!” (Oct. 2023)—Choosing an eternal perspective amid mortal opposition.

### Questions to Ponder

- How am I building my foundation on Jesus Christ so that life’s “whirlwind” has no power over me?  
- What is the “other side of the coin” in a present trial—what growth, gratitude, or blessing might God be shaping?  
- How can I better recognize the Spirit of Christ to discern good from evil in my daily choices?  
- What fear about the future can I reframe today by choosing to “think celestial” and trust the Lord?

## 34. Rejoice in the Gift of Priesthood Keys - President Russell M. Nelson

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/57nelson?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/04/57nelson?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Nelson rejoices in the Restoration of priesthood keys—especially those restored in the Kirtland Temple—and promises that faithful, regular temple worship unlocks gathering, Abrahamic, and sealing blessings, bringing divine power, protection, revelation, and joy to every covenant-keeping disciple.

### Haiku

Priesthood keys restored  
Open covenants, bind hearts;  
Temples light our way.

### Key Points

1. Restoration and meaning of priesthood keys  
   - In the Kirtland Temple, the Savior, Moses, Elias, and Elijah restored essential keys: gathering Israel, the gospel of Abraham, and the sealing power. These keys distinguish the Lord’s Church and enable ordinances and covenants that bless us in mortality and beyond.

2. Temples as the channel of power and protection  
   - The Kirtland dedicatory prayer (Doctrine and Covenants 109) teaches that the temple is a house of prayer, fasting, faith, learning, glory, order, and of God—promising answers, revelation, a fulness of the Holy Ghost, angelic help, courage, and that “no combination of wickedness” will prevail against the faithful.

3. God’s love extended to all through gathering, Abrahamic blessings, and sealing  
   - The gathering of Israel, the gospel of Abraham, and the sealing power demonstrate God’s universal love; through temple worship we help gather Israel on both sides of the veil and receive every blessing promised to Abraham, as the Church increases temple access worldwide.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 110:1–16 — Christ, Moses, Elias, and Elijah appear in the Kirtland Temple to confer keys.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 109 — Kirtland Temple dedicatory prayer; promises of revelation, power, protection, and a fulness of the Holy Ghost.  
- Malachi 4:5–6; Doctrine and Covenants 2 — Elijah’s promise to turn hearts of fathers and children; sealing power.  
- 2 Nephi 26:33 — “All are alike unto God”; God’s universal invitation.  
- 1 Nephi 8; 1 Nephi 11–15 — Holding fast to the iron rod amid mists of darkness.  
- Abraham 2:9–11 — The Abrahamic covenant: blessings to all nations.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 128:18 — The welding link of sealing across the veil.  
- Gospel principles: Priesthood keys and authority, the gathering of Israel, the Abrahamic covenant, the sealing power, temple worship as a source of personal revelation and divine protection, “think celestial” living.

### Questions to Ponder

- How would my life—and my family’s future—be different without restored priesthood keys?  
- What does the Savior’s promise “I will manifest myself … in this house” mean for me personally, and how have I seen it fulfilled?  
- Which temple promise from D&C 109 do I most need now, and what will I do to seek it?  
- How can I more fully “think celestial” by prioritizing regular temple worship within my current circumstances?  
- In what specific ways do priesthood keys bless women and men daily in the Church and at home?  
- How can I participate more actively in gathering Israel on both sides of the veil this month?  
- What obstacles keep me from the temple, and what covenant-centered steps will I take to overcome them?
