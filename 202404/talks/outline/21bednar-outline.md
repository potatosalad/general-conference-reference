### Summary

Elder David A. Bednar teaches that “being still” is a covenant-rooted focus on Jesus Christ that anchors our lives to His rock through sacred times and holy places—especially Sabbath, temple, and home—so the Holy Ghost can steady us to do and overcome hard things.

### Haiku

Be still; know your God.  
Build on the Redeemer’s rock—  
Sabbath, temple, home.

### Key Points

1) Be Still
- Stillness is more than silence; it is an inner spiritual steadiness that comes as we remember and rely on God “at all times and in all things, and in all places,” despite life’s noise and commotion.

2) Build Upon the Rock
- We must actively build our personal foundation upon Jesus Christ through covenants and ordinances; these covenant connections act like anchors to bedrock, giving us hope, stability, and the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost.

3) Sacred Times and Holy Places—Especially the Home
- The Sabbath, meetinghouses, and temples are holy settings that teach and renew stillness in our souls, and our homes should be the ultimate blend of sacred time and holy place where Church and home are gathered together in Christ so we can face trials without distress or despair.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Psalm 46:10; Doctrine and Covenants 101:16 — “Be still, and know that I am God.”
- Helaman 5:12 — Build your foundation upon the rock of Christ.
- Ether 12:4 — Hope in Christ as “an anchor to the souls of men.”
- Moroni 7:28 — Christ advocates the cause of the children of men.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:45–46 — Virtue garnish thy thoughts; confidence before God; the Holy Ghost as constant companion.
- Doctrine and Covenants 59:9–13 — Sabbath worship and offering the sacrament as sacred time and sign to God.
- 2 Nephi 31:20 — A perfect brightness of hope through steadfastness in Christ.
- 2 Corinthians 4:8–9 — Troubled yet not distressed; persecuted but not forsaken.

Gospel principles to study:
- Covenant connection to Jesus Christ through ordinances.
- The Sabbath as a sign of our love for God.
- The temple as a holy place to learn eternal truths and receive power.
- The home as the primary place of spiritual formation and enduring stillness.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does “be still” look like in my daily rhythm beyond simply being quiet? What will I stop or start this week to cultivate inner stillness?
- How are my covenants functioning as “anchor pins” to connect my life to Christ’s rock? How do I review, remember, and renew them?
- In what specific ways can my Sabbath be a clearer sign to God of my love and devotion?
- How can our home better combine sacred time and holy place? What one small, consistent change would invite more of the Spirit?
- How has temple worship helped me “know and remember” God? What will I do to attend and serve more deliberately?
- When “mighty winds” blow, which parts of my spiritual foundation hold firm and which need reinforcing?
- How do I recognize the Holy Ghost’s gift of calm assurance? What conditions in my life help me feel it more consistently?
- What’s the difference between assuming Christ is my foundation and intentionally building upon Him each day?
