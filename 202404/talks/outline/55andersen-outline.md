### Summary

Elder Neil L. Andersen teaches that in the commotion of the last days, the Lord is dotting the earth with temples to strengthen His covenant people—arming them with power, placing His name upon them, surrounding them with angels, and filling them with the Holy Ghost as they worship and keep sacred covenants.

### Haiku

Holy houses rise—  
in storm and noise, hearts find rest;  
angels guard our way.

### Key Points

1) Why so many temples now: In prophesied days of confusion and failing hearts, the Lord brings His holy houses closer to His covenant people to steady, protect, and prepare them for His coming.

2) Kirtland Temple promises today: The dedicatory prayer (D&C 109) outlines enduring blessings of temple worship—being armed with God’s power, taking Christ’s name upon us more fully, receiving angelic help, and growing into a fulness of the Holy Ghost.

3) Invitations to act: Increase regular, worthy temple worship; participate in family history and vicarious ordinances; pray for temples being planned and built; and live so temple covenants shape identity, choices, and discipleship beyond the temple.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- D&C 109:15, 22 — Kirtland Temple dedicatory promises: fulness of the Holy Ghost; armed with power; His name upon us; angels have charge over us.  
- D&C 110 — The Savior appears in the Kirtland Temple; priesthood keys restored by Moses, Elias, Elijah.  
- D&C 45:32; D&C 87:8 — Stand in holy places and be not moved.  
- D&C 88:91; Luke 21:26; 2 Timothy 3:1–5 — Last-days commotion, failing hearts, and self-centeredness.  
- D&C 93:1 — Promise to see the Savior’s face as we forsake sin, come unto Him, and obey.  
- President Russell M. Nelson’s prophetic assurances that the Lord will perform mighty works before His return; see also “Think Celestial!” (Oct 2023) for covenant-centered perspective.  
- Hymn: “The Spirit of God” — A hymn of temple dedication and latter-day power.  
- Gospel principles: Covenant identity; endowment of spiritual power; ministering of angels; the sanctifying influence of the Holy Ghost; redeeming the dead through family history and temple ordinances; praying for and supporting temple building.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does being “armed with power” from the temple look like in my daily discipleship this week?  
- How can I take the Savior’s name more completely upon me through my temple covenants?  
- What specific sacrifice or schedule change would help me attend the temple more regularly and more reverently?  
- When have I felt the influence of angels or heaven’s nearness through family history or temple service?  
- How can temple worship strengthen someone I know whose heart is “failing” amid today’s commotion?  
- In what ways do I invite a “fulness of the Holy Ghost” before, during, and after my temple worship?  
- How can our family or class pray and act to support temple building and dedication efforts worldwide?  
- What one “think celestial” decision can I make that better aligns my life with my temple covenants?
