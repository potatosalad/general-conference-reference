### Summary

Elder Matthew L. Carpenter teaches that when we faithfully keep our covenants—especially the new and everlasting covenant of marriage—the Holy Spirit of Promise seals those ordinances so their blessings become “fruit that remains” for eternity, with compassionate guidance for married, single, divorced, and repenting members.

### Haiku

Fruit sealed, not to spoil;  
Spirit seals faithful vows made—  
Love cleaves, God exalts.

### Key Points

1. The Holy Spirit of Promise seals faithful covenants for eternity  
   - Ordinances receive eternal validity when sealed by the Holy Ghost upon those who are true and faithful, fulfilling the Savior’s charge to bring forth “fruit that remains.”

2. Exaltation and the two bonds of celestial marriage  
   - Exaltation requires entering into and keeping the new and everlasting covenant of marriage by proper authority. Keeping it means honoring two bonds: the lateral bond (cleaving to one’s spouse in love, fidelity, and unity) and the vertical bond (keeping temple covenants with God—obedience, sacrifice, the gospel, chastity, and consecration).

3. Hope, agency, and God’s timing amid varied life circumstances  
   - Faithful individuals retain promised personal blessings even if a spouse is unfaithful or a marriage ends; God will not force anyone into a sealed relationship. Single members are assured that no eternal blessings will be withheld in the Lord’s time and way. Those who have broken covenants can fully return through repentance and the healing power of Jesus Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 15:16 — “That your fruit should remain.”
- Ephesians 1:13 — “Sealed with that holy Spirit of promise.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 131:1–4 — Degrees in the celestial glory; the necessity of the new and everlasting covenant of marriage for the highest degree.
- Doctrine and Covenants 132:7, 19 — Sealing authority and eternal marriage.
- Doctrine and Covenants 76:53 — The Holy Spirit of Promise.
- Doctrine and Covenants 42:22 — “Thou shalt love thy wife with all thy heart, and shalt cleave unto her and none else.”
- Abraham 2:11 — Promises of the Abrahamic covenant (posterity, priesthood, gospel).
- Gospel principles: Holy Spirit of Promise; temple covenants (obedience, sacrifice, the gospel, chastity, consecration); sealing keys held by the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve; agency in eternal relationships; repentance and the Atonement of Jesus Christ.
- Related teachings: President Russell M. Nelson on beginning with the end in mind and the centrality of celestial marriage; President Dallin H. Oaks on agency—no one is forced into a sealing.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does “fruit that remains” look like in my life right now, and how can I better invite the Holy Ghost to seal my faithful efforts?
- How can I more fully “cleave” to my spouse (or prepare to do so) through daily counsel, time, and Christlike love?
- Which temple covenants (obedience, sacrifice, the gospel, chastity, consecration) do I most need to strengthen to deepen my vertical bond with God?
- What boundaries and habits will help me maintain complete fidelity—emotionally, digitally, and physically—in my discipleship and marriage?
- How does trusting God’s timing bring peace if I am single, divorced, or in complex family circumstances?
- In what ways can sincere repentance and counsel with priesthood leaders help me return to the covenant path and feel the Savior’s healing?
- How can “beginning with the end in mind” shape my daily choices so promised blessings can be sealed upon me and my family?
