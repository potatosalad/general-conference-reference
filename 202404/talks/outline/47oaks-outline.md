### Summary

President Dallin H. Oaks teaches that covenants are sacred commitments central to God’s plan, the Restoration, and temple worship—binding us to Jesus Christ and qualifying us for the blessings of salvation and exaltation.

### Haiku

Covenants bind hearts;  
Symbols remind, temples teach—  
Bound to Christ, we rise.

### Key Points

1. Covenants as sacred commitments
   - Covenants structure both society and discipleship; like judges, soldiers, or medics who pledge duty, disciples covenant with God, and symbols (missionary tags, wedding rings) remind us of responsibilities.

2. Covenants in scripture and the Restoration
   - From the Abrahamic covenant to Book of Mormon disciples, God’s work moves forward by covenant; the restored Church centers on baptismal and sacrament covenants that bind us to Christ and keep us on the covenant path.

3. Temple covenants and lifelong discipleship
   - Temple ordinances and covenants, including the faithful wearing of the garment, bind us to the Savior, empower daily holiness, and are essential for exaltation; temples worldwide exist to extend these blessings to God’s children.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Abrahamic covenant: Genesis 17; Abraham 2:9–11
- “Record of the Jews … contains the covenants”: 1 Nephi 13:23
- God’s covenant people and protection: 1 Nephi 14:14
- A new covenant in Christ: Hebrews 8:8–10; 9:15
- Baptismal covenant: Doctrine and Covenants 20:37
- Sacrament prayers (renewing covenants): Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79; Moroni 4–5
- Restoration preface—broken and reestablished covenant: Doctrine and Covenants 1:15, 22
- Promised blessings of the faithful—“all things are theirs”: Doctrine and Covenants 76:55–59
- Eternal life, “the greatest of all the gifts of God”: Doctrine and Covenants 14:7
- President Russell M. Nelson: “covenant path” and “Think Celestial!” (Oct. 2023)
- President Gordon B. Hinckley on renewing baptismal covenants through the sacrament

### Questions to Ponder

- What daily choices show that I am “willing to take upon [me] the name of Jesus Christ” and “always remember him”?
- Which symbols (temple garment, wedding ring, missionary tag, temple recommend) help me remember my covenants—and how can I honor them more reverently?
- How does “thinking celestial” change the way I approach sacrifice, service, and obedience to my covenants?
- What next step on the covenant path (preparing for baptism, endowment, sealing, regular temple worship, family history) is the Lord inviting me to take?
- How can I better support loved ones in keeping their covenants—with encouragement, example, and charity?
- When have temple covenants given me strength in trials, as they did for the early Saints leaving Nauvoo?
