### Summary

President Henry B. Eyring testifies that making and keeping temple covenants brings the Savior’s peace, power, revelation, and enduring family bonds so we can face any trial with confidence that, through Jesus Christ, all will be well.

### Haiku

Floodwaters rise, still—  
Covenants bind hearts with peace;  
In His house, all’s well.

### Key Points

1. Temple covenants bring Christ’s peace amid adversity.  
   - The Teton Dam experience taught that sealed families can face uncertainty without fear because of promises made in the house of the Lord.

2. Keeping covenants invites power, purity, and revelation.  
   - Frequent temple worship creates a lifelong pattern of devotion that draws the Holy Ghost, increases access to the power of Jesus Christ, and steadies us on the covenant path.

3. Sealing ordinances anchor eternal families and guide life choices.  
   - Temple covenants turn hearts within families, protect against pride and selfishness, and prepare us to respond to prophetic direction—“walk away easily” when the Lord calls.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 14:27 — The Savior’s peace that dispels fear.  
- Malachi 4:5–6; Doctrine and Covenants 2 — Hearts turned to fathers and children through Elijah’s sealing power.  
- Matthew 16:19; Doctrine and Covenants 132:19 — Keys that bind on earth and in heaven; eternal marriage.  
- 2 Corinthians 12:7–10 — “Thorn in the flesh”; grace in trials.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 4:2 — Serve with all your heart, might, mind, and strength; covenant devotion.  
- President Russell M. Nelson:  
  - “The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation” (Oct 2021) — Everything we believe and every promise converge in the temple.  
  - “Let God Prevail” (Oct 2020) and subsequent teachings — Covenants remove us from “neutral ground” and bind us to God with love and mercy.  
  - Teachings on increased access to the power of Jesus Christ through temple covenants.  
- Gospel principles: Sealing and eternal families; companionship of the Holy Ghost; personal revelation in holy temples; covenant loyalty that protects against pride and selfishness; family prayer and service softening hearts.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I felt the Savior’s peace specifically because of my temple covenants?  
- What practical patterns can I adopt (or renew) to attend the temple more frequently and to remember my covenants daily?  
- How do my covenants help me receive and recognize personal revelation during life’s crises?  
- In what ways can I foster “hearts turning” in my family through prayer, service, and temple work?  
- What does “live so that when the call comes, you can walk away easily” mean for my current priorities?  
- How does living “inside” my covenants increase my access to the power of Jesus Christ?  
- What attitudes of pride or selfishness do my covenants invite me to discard to strengthen my marriage and family?  
- If I am not currently temple worthy or endowed, what is my next faithful step on the covenant path?
