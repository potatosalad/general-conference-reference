### Summary

Elder Alonso teaches that placing Jesus Christ at the center of our lives—especially amid trials—brings healing, revelation, rest, and enduring joy through His Atonement.

### Haiku

Bread breaks; hearts are healed—  
Gratitude opens heaven;  
Christ-centered, we rest.

### Key Points

1) Come unto Christ and find rest: The Savior’s invitation to take His yoke is a divine promise of hope, healing, forgiveness, and peace through His infinite Atonement, regardless of our circumstances.

2) Focus determines power: Turning our attention to the Father and the Son—expressed through gratitude, trust, and discipleship—aligns us with heaven’s help; the feeding of the five thousand and Alma the Younger’s conversion illustrate how Christ-centered focus transforms lack and anguish into abundance and joy.

3) Build on the Rock: When we build our lives on Jesus Christ and follow living prophets, we receive revelation, strength to overcome, and joy that endures; His gospel becomes the unshakable foundation of our daily choices and family life.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 11:28–30 — “Come unto me… take my yoke… ye shall find rest.”
- Matthew 14:13–21 — Feeding the five thousand; gratitude precedes heaven’s multiplying power.
- Alma 36:3 — Trust in God and be supported in trials.
- Alma 36:17–21 — Alma’s Christ-centered repentance and the joy that replaced pain.
- Helaman 5:12 — Build upon the rock of our Redeemer.
- John 4:10–14 — The Savior’s “living water” that heals and satisfies the soul.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Joy and Spiritual Survival” (Oct. 2016) — Joy comes from and because of Christ.
- Gospel principles: The Atonement of Jesus Christ; Discipleship and covenants; Revelation and peace; Gratitude and faith-focused living; Following living prophets.

### Questions to Ponder

- What burden am I carrying that I can consciously “yoke” with the Savior this week?
- In my current trial, where is my focus—on the scarcity/problem or on the Savior and gratitude to the Father? How can I shift that focus today?
- What daily practices help me build on “the rock of our Redeemer” (prayer, scriptures, sacrament, temple, service)? Which one will I strengthen now?
- When have I experienced the Savior’s “living water”? What invited that flow, and how can I return to it?
- How can I center Jesus Christ more intentionally in my home conversations, routines, and decisions?
- Which prophetic counsel, if acted on this week, would unlock greater joy in Christ for me or my family?
- Whom can I “feed” or lift—despite my own limits—as the Savior did after hearing of John’s death?
