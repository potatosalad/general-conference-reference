### Summary

Elder Christofferson teaches that our eternal destiny hinges on how we receive and live “the testimony of Jesus,” inviting us to be valiant disciples by making and keeping covenants, nourishing daily faith, heeding prophets, openly witnessing of Christ, and pursuing personal holiness.

### Haiku

Hold fast to His name—  
valiant hearts choose covenant light,  
think celestial now.

### Key Points

1. What the testimony of Jesus is: The Holy Spirit witnesses that Jesus is the Son of God, Creator, Messiah, and Redeemer—He died, rose the third day, and ascended; there is no other name by which salvation comes, and living prophets and apostles bear a united witness that He lives.
2. Our response determines our glory: Celestial heirs “receive” the testimony of Jesus through covenants and faithful living; terrestrial souls accept it but are not valiant—lukewarm or casual; telestial souls reject the gospel, its covenants, and its prophets.
3. How to be valiant in the testimony of Jesus: 
   - Nourish testimony daily through prayer, scripture, Sabbath worship, sacrament, repentance, ministering, and temple worship. 
   - Stand as a public witness of Christ and heed His living prophets. 
   - Lead and lift at home, forgive offenses, and choose an eternal perspective.
   - Seek sanctification and a mighty change of heart by yielding to God and acting now to repent.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 76:51–53, 69–82 — Degrees of glory; “valiant in the testimony of Jesus.”
- Mosiah 3:17 — “No other name” whereby salvation comes.
- John 1 (see JST John 1:1–4) — The Word, Light, and Life.
- Romans 8:17 — “Joint-heirs with Christ.”
- Moroni 6:4 — Nourished by the good word of God.
- Mosiah 18:9 — Stand as witnesses of God at all times.
- Revelation 3:16 — The danger of being lukewarm.
- Alma 27:27 — People of Ammon “distinguished for their zeal toward God.”
- Helaman 3:35 — Sanctification comes by yielding our hearts to God.
- 3 Nephi 27:20 — Be sanctified by the Holy Ghost; stand spotless.
- Mosiah 5:7–13 — Retain the name of Christ in your hearts; know His voice.
- Alma 34:33 — Do not procrastinate the day of your repentance.
- D&C 45:3–5 — Christ, our Advocate with the Father.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Think Celestial!” (Oct 2023) and “Let God Prevail” (Oct 2020) — Aim for the celestial and give God your time.
- President Dallin H. Oaks on the kingdoms of glory — Clarifies the plan and our choices.
- The Living Christ: The Testimony of the Apostles (2000) — Modern apostolic witness of the resurrected Lord.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does “receiving the testimony of Jesus” look like in my daily discipleship and covenants?
- In what ways might I be “lukewarm” or casual, and how will I act now to become valiant?
- Which small and simple practices most nourish my testimony each day? What will I adjust this week?
- How can I more openly and joyfully witness of Christ in my home, work, and community?
- What recent prophetic counsel have I heeded—and what counsel do I still need to act on?
- How can I help my family move from “casually engaged” to “anxiously engaged” discipleship?
- When I am offended or hurt, how will I keep my eyes on eternity, forgive, and stay on the covenant path?
- What does “think celestial” change about a current decision I am making?
- In what specific way will I “yield my heart to God” and seek sanctification through the Holy Ghost this week?
