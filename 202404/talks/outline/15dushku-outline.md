### Summary

Elder Dushku teaches that most testimonies grow through many small, consistent “rays” of revelation rather than one dramatic “pillar of light,” and as we remember and gather these rays, our faith in Jesus Christ becomes bright enough to dispel darkness.

### Haiku

Pillars start as rays;  
Line upon line, light grows bright—  
Hear Him in small peace.

### Key Points

1. Rays, not fireworks: God’s common pattern is to reveal truth “line upon line”—through quiet, continuous rays of light—so we should not be discouraged if we haven’t had spectacular spiritual experiences.
2. Notice and gather your rays: The Spirit speaks in varied, personal ways (peace to mind, impressions to mind and heart, scripture that “speaks,” love in service, remembered tender mercies); as we recognize, record, and remember these moments, “light cleaveth unto light” and our testimony strengthens.
3. Live worthy of continual light: As covenant disciples, we are “living in revelation”; with faith, repentance, and diligence, our accumulated rays grow “brighter and brighter until the perfect day,” pointing us to the Father and the Son.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Joseph Smith—History 1:15–17: The First Vision and the “pillar of light.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:22–23: “Did I not speak peace to your mind…?”
- Doctrine and Covenants 8:2–3: Revelation comes to mind and heart by the Holy Ghost.
- Doctrine and Covenants 50:24: Light grows “brighter and brighter until the perfect day.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:46; John 1:9: The Spirit/Light of Christ gives light to every person.
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:12–13, 40: Light fills the immensity of space; “light cleaveth unto light… truth embraceth truth.”
- Alma 32:28–43: Nurturing a seed of faith—experimenting, remembering, and growing.
- 1 Nephi 1:20; Elder David A. Bednar, “The Tender Mercies of the Lord” (Apr 2005): Recognizing God’s personalized mercies.
- Elder David A. Bednar, teachings on “living in revelation.”
- President Spencer W. Kimball’s counsel to avoid expecting only the spectacular and to notice the steady flow of revelation.
- President Russell M. Nelson’s invitation to “immerse [ourselves] in the glorious light of the Restoration.”

Gospel principles:
- The Light of Christ and the Gift of the Holy Ghost
- Personal revelation: patterns, patience, and discernment
- “Line upon line” growth and spiritual memory (recording sacred experiences)
- Faith, repentance, and honoring covenants as conduits for light
- Choosing Christ’s light over worldly darkness

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I recently felt a “ray” of spiritual light—however small—and how can I better remember and record it?
- Do I sometimes expect the spectacular and miss the steady flow of the Spirit? How can I adjust my expectations?
- Which channels of revelation (scripture, prayer, service, ordinances, music, teaching, quiet thoughts) most often bring me light?
- How does honoring my covenants increase my capacity to perceive and receive revelation?
- In times of anxiety, depression, or spiritual dryness, how can I recognize “tender mercies” in hindsight?
- What practices could I begin this week (journaling, regular temple worship, focused questions in prayer) to gather my rays “together in one”?
- How can I help family members or class members identify and value their own rays of testimony?
- What is one choice I can make today to “choose Him over the darkness of the world”?
