### Summary

Sister Andrea Muñoz Spannaus teaches youth to overcome life’s “Goliaths” by anchoring themselves to Jesus Christ through love of God, faith in Christ, divine identity, daily repentance, covenants that connect us to His power, and a personal testimony.

### Haiku

Small stones in my sling,  
Covenants bind me to Christ—  
Giants fall; I stand.

### Key Points

1) Prepare your “stones” of discipleship  
- Like David, come prepared: love God, exercise faith in Jesus Christ, remember your divine identity, practice daily repentance, and access God’s power through covenants; these strengths equip you to face the world with courage and joy.

2) Stay firmly connected to the Rock  
- Power comes through connection to Christ—especially through covenants and obedience—giving clarity, confidence, and strength to resist sin, withstand opposition, and endure trials.

3) Build and defend your personal testimony  
- Personal spiritual experiences form a testimony no one can take; loving, seeking, and defending truth brings freedom and joy, and invites the Savior to guide you as you “hold to the iron rod.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 1 Samuel 17: David and Goliath—faith in God defeats giants.  
- Matthew 22:37–38: The first great commandment—love God with all your heart.  
- Alma 7:11–13: Christ bears sins, pains, infirmities; He knows how to succor us.  
- John 16:33: “Be of good cheer; I have overcome the world.”  
- Matthew 7:24–27: House on the rock—strength is in the connection to Christ.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:36: “Look unto me in every thought.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:20–21: “In the ordinances… the power of godliness is manifest.”  
- 1 Nephi 8:30; 11:25: Holding to the iron rod (the word of God) leads safely to the tree of life.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 58:42–43: Forgiveness through sincere repentance.  
- President Russell M. Nelson on identity: we are children of God, children of the covenant, and disciples of Jesus Christ (see, e.g., “Choices for Eternity,” 2022).  
- Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf, “Jesus Christ Is the Strength of Youth” (Oct. 2022)—the power is in our connection to the Rock.  
- For the Strength of Youth: A Guide for Making Choices—love God, daily repentance, covenant connection to Christ.

### Questions to Ponder

- What “Goliaths” am I facing right now, and how will I invite the Savior to fight with me?  
- Which of the five stones (love for God, faith in Christ, divine identity, daily repentance, covenants) is strongest for me today? Which one needs strengthening this week?  
- How do my covenants practically increase God’s power in my life? What specific promise am I relying on?  
- When have I felt “firmly attached” to the Rock? What daily habits keep that connection strong?  
- How do I remind myself of my true identity when I feel discouraged or pressured?  
- What is one way I will practice daily repentance that is specific, sincere, and joyful?  
- What spiritual experience anchors my testimony? How can I nurture and defend that testimony in loving ways at school, online, and at home?  
- What is my “sixth stone” to add this week (e.g., prayer, temple attendance, service, seminary, scripture study), and how will I act on it?
