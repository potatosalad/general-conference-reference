### Summary

Elder Gerrit W. Gong teaches that through Jesus Christ’s Atonement, our covenants, and God’s eternal perspective, “all things” in mortality—even trials—can be consecrated and work together for our ultimate good.

### Haiku

Storms shift, yet He weaves  
Covenant threads through our trials—  
All things for our good.

### Key Points

1) Trust God’s eternal purpose and nature  
   God’s work and glory are to bring all things together for our good; as we love God, walk uprightly, and remember our covenants, Christ sanctifies us and helps us put off the natural man and become whole in Him.

2) Trials can be consecrated for our gain  
   Mortality includes sorrow, illness, and loss, but the Lord does not send trials to punish; in His timing, heartbreaks can bless us, and we should mourn with and minister to one another without empty platitudes.

3) Covenants, scriptures, and temples are God’s means to make all things right  
   The Book of Mormon draws us to Christ now; ministering creates covenant belonging; regular temple worship and family history work help gather Israel and bring redemption on both sides of the veil—where “everything” we believe comes together.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Romans 8:28 — All things work together for good to them that love God.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 90:24; 98:3 — As we walk uprightly, all things shall work together for our good.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:7–8; 122:7 — “But for a small moment”; all these things will give thee experience and be for thy good.  
- Alma 7:11–12 — Christ succors His people according to their infirmities.  
- Mosiah 3:19 — Put off the natural man and become a saint through the Atonement of Christ.  
- Job 1:21 — “The Lord gave, and the Lord hath taken away; blessed be the name of the Lord.”  
- Matthew 7:9–11 — God gives good gifts; He does not give stones or serpents.  
- Genesis 50:20 — God can turn evil intentions to good.  
- Mosiah 18:9 — Mourn with those that mourn; comfort those in need of comfort.  
- Moroni 10:32 — Come unto Christ and be perfected in Him.  
- Obadiah 1:21 — Saviors on Mount Zion; temple and proxy work.  
- The Book of Mormon (1 Nephi 1–Moroni 10) — A witness that draws us to Christ in our day.  
- Hymns: “Be Still, My Soul”; “Come, Come, Ye Saints.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson: “Everything we believe and every promise God has made to His covenant people come together in the temple.”

### Questions to Ponder

- Where have I seen the Lord quietly weave “all things” for my good, even if only in hindsight?  
- How can I better “remember my covenants” daily so God’s promises have greater power in my life?  
- In a current trial, what might it look like to trust God’s timing and see it as “but for a small moment”?  
- How can I minister in a way that truly mourns with and listens to others, avoiding cliché answers?  
- What specific step can I take this week to deepen my temple worship or family history service?  
- Which Book of Mormon passage has most recently drawn me to Christ, and why?  
- What does “put off the natural man” look like in my discipleship right now?
