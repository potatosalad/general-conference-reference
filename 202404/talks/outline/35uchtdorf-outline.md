### Summary

Elder Uchtdorf teaches that the “higher joy” we all seek is found in following Jesus Christ—drawing near to God, actively looking for joy, and lifting others—bringing peace that endures opposition and outlasts worldly happiness.

### Haiku

Draw near, seek, then serve,  
Higher than sun-split clouds’ mirth—  
Christ’s peace fills the soul.

### Key Points

1. Godly joy is the purpose of the plan and is higher than worldly happiness  
   - Joy is central to God’s plan: “men are that they might have joy.” Worldly pleasures fade, but the Savior offers enduring, eternal joy that brings “life more abundantly,” even amid opposition and sorrow.

2. Three invitations that open the path to higher joy  
   - Draw near unto God: like the woman who touched Christ’s garment, exercise faith to reach for Him and be healed.  
   - Look for it: “Seek, and ye shall find.” We tend to find what we’re looking for—cultivate eyes to see joy and reasons to rejoice.  
   - Bear one another’s burdens: service multiplies joy—what we give returns “pressed down, shaken together, and running over.”

3. The blessings of walking in His Way  
   - As we keep commandments and honor covenants, the Savior grants His peace “not as the world giveth,” a steady assurance that penetrates sorrow, diminishes loneliness, and lifts our hearts “higher” toward eternal hope.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Nephi 2:25 — “Men are, that they might have joy.”  
- John 10:10 — “I am come that they might have life… more abundantly.”  
- John 14:27 — Christ’s peace is unlike the world’s.  
- Matthew 7:7 — “Seek, and ye shall find.”  
- Mark 5:25–34 — The woman with an issue of blood; drawing near in faith.  
- Mosiah 2:17 — “When ye are in the service of your fellow beings…”  
- Luke 6:38 — “Good measure, pressed down… running over.”  
- 2 Nephi 2:11 — “Opposition in all things.”  
- Philippians 4:7 — “The peace of God, which passeth all understanding.”  
- D&C 59:18–20 — God created things to please the eye and gladden the heart.  
- 1 Corinthians 15:42–54 — From corruption to incorruption through Christ.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Joy and Spiritual Survival” (Oct. 2016) — Joy comes from Jesus Christ and our covenants.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where am I currently looking for happiness, and how can I re-center that search on Jesus Christ?  
- What does “drawing near” to God look like for me this week in prayer, scripture, temple worship, or repentance?  
- How can I actively “look for” small, daily evidences of God’s goodness and joy?  
- Who around me needs lifting, listening, or loving—and what simple act of service can I offer today?  
- How have my covenants yoked me to the Savior’s peace “not as the world giveth”?  
- In what ways has opposition or sorrow deepened, rather than diminished, my capacity for joy?  
- What worldly pursuits might be crowding out time and attention for higher, lasting joy?  
- How can I better welcome mental and emotional health support while I seek spiritual healing in Christ?  
- What would “Higher, Father, higher!” mean in my discipleship right now?  
- Which promise from this talk will I claim and act on before next Sunday?
