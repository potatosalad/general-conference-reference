### Summary

Elder De Feo teaches that the gospel gives us clear spiritual vision amid trials as we focus on Jesus Christ, cast off the natural man, and heed His voice with real intent to follow Him.

### Haiku

Crowds hush, he cries out  
Cast off the beggar’s old coat—  
Christ clears our true sight

### Key Points

1. Focus on Jesus Christ and stand true to revealed truth  
   - Like Bartimaeus, disciples raise their witness above the world’s noise, stay faithful to covenants, and act in faith rather than be acted upon.

2. Cast off the natural man and rise through covenants  
   - Throwing away the “beggar’s coat” symbolizes repenting, rejecting excuses, and choosing a new life in Christ by making and keeping sacred covenants.

3. Hear His voice and follow with real intent  
   - We come to Jesus by listening to His voice amid other voices; miracles and spiritual clarity come as we follow Him “in the way” with the intent to keep following.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Mark 10:46–52 — Bartimaeus cries out, casts away his garment, receives sight, and follows Jesus.  
- 2 Nephi 2:14, 26–27 — Act and not be acted upon; agency to choose liberty in Christ.  
- Mosiah 3:19 — Put off the natural man and become a saint through the Atonement.  
- Matthew 14:28–31 — Peter walks on water when his focus stays on the Savior.  
- John 10:27 — “My sheep hear my voice.”  
- Moroni 10:4, 7 — Ask with real intent; miracles come by faith.  
- Gospel principles: Covenants and the covenant path; Repentance; Faith to act; Real intent; Discipleship and testimony; Spiritual vision versus worldly voices.  
- Related teachings: President Russell M. Nelson’s invitations to “Hear Him” and to stay on the covenant path.

### Questions to Ponder

- What voices in my life try to silence or dilute my testimony of Jesus Christ? How can I raise my witness above them?  
- What is my “beggar’s coat”—habits, excuses, or identities I need to cast off to rise in Christ?  
- How do my covenants help me act in faith instead of being acted upon?  
- When have I clearly “heard His voice,” and what patterns help me hear Him more consistently?  
- In what specific way will I show “real intent” to follow Jesus after receiving answers or blessings?  
- Where am I looking when the winds and waves of life rise—at Christ, or at the storm?  
- What daily practices sharpen my spiritual vision (scripture study, prayer, temple worship, Sabbath, service)?  
- Whom can I bless this week by “becoming His voice” and testifying of the Savior?  
- What trial am I facing now that the gospel can help me face with greater faith and peace?  
- How will I rise when He calleth me—today?
