### Summary

President Dallin H. Oaks led the sustaining of the First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, General Authority Seventies, Area Seventies, and General Officers, acknowledged releases and emeritus designations, and affirmed the Church’s governance by common consent under priesthood keys.

### Haiku

Hands lift like one heart,
Common consent binds our faith—
Keys guide us in love.

### Key Points

1. Common consent and sustaining. Members express support for leaders “in the usual way,” with a respectful process for registering concerns through proper priesthood channels, reflecting agency, order, and unity in the Church.
2. Priesthood keys and orderly governance. The First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve, and quorums of the Seventy function under priesthood keys; calls, releases, and reorganizations show the Lord’s pattern of leadership by councils and continuing revelation.
3. Gratitude, continuity, and worldwide service. The Church honors released and emeritus leaders and welcomes new leaders—including General Authority and Area Seventies and a new Sunday School General Presidency—demonstrating ongoing growth and the continuity of the Lord’s work across the earth.

- In what ways do priesthood keys operating through councils bless me and my family?

- How can I show gratitude to released leaders and actively support newly called leaders in my ward and stake?
