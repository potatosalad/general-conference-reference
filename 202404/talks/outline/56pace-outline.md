### Summary

President Mark L. Pace teaches that daily, prayerful study of the Book of Mormon invites the Holy Ghost, strengthens homes and classes, and protects us from personal apostasy in this final dispensation.

### Haiku

Hold fast to God’s word—  
tree of life within our reach,  
daily light grows bright.

### Key Points

1) The Book of Mormon anchors us in the last dispensation  
- While there will be no general apostasy, we must guard against personal apostasy; the Book of Mormon is the keystone that keeps us close to Jesus Christ and His living prophet.

2) Daily study invites the Holy Ghost and transforms homes and classes  
- Scripture study isn’t only to learn facts; it nourishes the soul, brings joy, deepens conversion, and improves Sunday teaching as we bring weekly learning to class.

3) Personal, intentional engagement leads to personal revelation  
- Like Lehi’s loving call and President Pace’s mother’s invitation, we come to know for ourselves by reading and praying daily; President Nelson promises divine guidance as we do so.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 1 Nephi 5:21–22 — The scriptures are “of great worth” and it is “wisdom in the Lord” to carry them with us.  
- 1 Nephi 8:14–16 — Lehi’s “loud voice” invitation to partake of the fruit: personal experience with God’s love.  
- Jacob 1:1–4 — Preserving sacred records for future generations.  
- Jacob 4:6 — “We search the scriptures… our faith becometh unshaken.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 50:22 — Both teacher and learner are edified together.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “The Book of Mormon: What Would Your Life Be Like without It?” (Oct. 2017) — Daily Book of Mormon study leads to better decisions and personal revelation.  
- Gospel principles: Personal revelation by the Holy Ghost; avoiding personal apostasy; the Book of Mormon as keystone; home-centered, Church-supported learning; following living prophets.

### Questions to Ponder

- What has the Holy Ghost taught me this week during my Book of Mormon study?  
- How has daily scripture study helped me make better decisions? What changed?  
- In what specific ways can I “hold fast” to the word of God in my current circumstances?  
- How can I make my personal copy of the Book of Mormon more meaningful (marking, journaling impressions, acting on promptings)?  
- What intentional invitations—like Lehi’s “loud voice”—can I extend in my home to help loved ones partake of God’s love?  
- What obstacles hinder my daily study, and what simple plan will help me overcome them?  
- How can my weekday study better prepare me to edify and be edified in Sunday classes?  
- When have I felt the Holy Ghost confirm truth as I read, and how can I seek that witness again?
