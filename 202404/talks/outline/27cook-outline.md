### Summary

Elder Quentin L. Cook teaches that true belonging comes as we become one with Christ through His Atonement and covenants, exercising our moral agency to repent, keep His commandments, and include all of God’s children on the covenant path.

### Haiku

One with Christ we live  
Covenant hearts gathered in  
Choose, repent, be one

### Key Points

1) Oneness in Christ is the essence of belonging  
   - The Savior’s Intercessory Prayer pleads that His followers “may be one” with Him and the Father; mercy and belonging are not based on lineage, status, or achievement but on turning to Christ through faith, repentance, and obedience.

2) Agency and discipleship require deliberate choice  
   - Moral agency is central to the plan of salvation; while all are invited, conduct and covenants matter—our choices carry consequences, so we “think celestial,” prioritize family, and actively choose Christ’s way.

3) The revealed order of the Church unites us through covenants  
   - Doctrine and Covenants 20 outlines foundational truths and the covenant path: simple, spiritual qualifications for baptism and the sacrament prayers; these ordinances, with temple covenants, justify and sanctify us by grace and gather Israel in loving unity.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 17:21–23 — The Savior’s Intercessory Prayer for oneness in Him and the Father.
- 2 Nephi 9:41 — Christ as the “Keeper of the Gate.”
- 2 Nephi 2:27 — Moral agency: choosing liberty and eternal life through the Mediator.
- Moses 4:3 — Lucifer sought to destroy agency; God’s plan preserves it.
- Alma 41:10 — “Wickedness never was happiness.”
- 2 Nephi 26:33 — “All are alike unto God”; the universal invitation to come to Christ.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:17–36 — Core doctrines: God, Creation, Fall, and salvation through Christ’s Atonement.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:37 — Spiritual qualifications for baptism (humility, repentance, willingness to take His name, endure to the end).
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:75–79 — Sacrament prayers and covenant renewal.
- Grace, justification, and sanctification through Jesus Christ’s Atonement (see D&C 20 and Book of Mormon teachings).
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Think Celestial!” (Oct. 2023) — Setting priorities with an eternal perspective.
- President Russell M. Nelson, teachings on gathering Israel (e.g., “Let God Prevail,” Oct. 2020) — Welcoming all into the covenant path.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does being “one with Christ” look like in my daily decisions, relationships, and worship?
- How do the baptismal qualifications in D&C 20:37 shape my ongoing conversion and covenant keeping?
- In what ways does the sacrament help me receive the Savior’s justifying and sanctifying grace each week?
- Where might I be relying on pedigree, status, or accomplishments instead of the Savior’s Atonement?
- How am I using my agency to “think celestial” and place family and discipleship above lesser pursuits?
- What small differences am I tempted to magnify into “chasms,” and how can I widen my circle of oneness?
- Which commandment or Christlike attribute should I choose to act on this week to deepen my unity with Him?
- How can I help gather Israel—on both sides of the veil—by inviting others into covenant belonging?
