### Summary

Elder Rasband teaches that words matter—God’s words, prophets’ words, and our own—and invites us to feast on the words of Christ, sustain the living prophet, and speak with love and the Spirit.

### Haiku

Words shape hearts and worlds.  
Feast on His word; think heavenward.  
Thank you, sorry, love.

### Key Points

1. The Lord’s words have power to save and transform.
   - God’s word creates light, endures forever, and, when we “feast” on it, tells us all things we should do; we “hear Him” through scripture, revelation, and the Atonement’s healing.
2. Prophets’ words guide us to Christ and deserve our sustaining action.
   - President Nelson’s counsel (including “Think celestial”) frames our choices; Elder Rasband shared a temple-dedication experience where revelation led him to include prophetic phrases—evidence that the Lord values His prophet’s words.
3. Our words reveal our discipleship and can heal or harm.
   - In homes and online, choose language that lifts; simple, sincere phrases—“Thank you,” “I am sorry,” and “I love you”—invite unity and the Spirit as we strive to “speak with the tongue of angels.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 31:5 — The word of God has more power than the sword.  
- Genesis 1:3 — “Let there be light”: the creative, commanding power of God’s word.  
- Matthew 24:35 — “My words shall not pass away.”  
- John 14:23 — Love the Savior by keeping His words.  
- Luke 1:38 — Mary: “Be it unto me according to thy word.”  
- 2 Nephi 32:3 — Feast upon the words of Christ; they will tell you all things you should do.  
- 2 Nephi 31:13; 32:2 — Speak with the tongue of angels by the power of the Holy Ghost.  
- 2 Nephi 1:15 — Encircled in the arms of His love.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Think Celestial!” (Oct. 2023) — A prophetic lens for daily decisions.  
- Principle: Sustain and act on the prophet’s counsel (see George Albert Smith’s teaching on sustaining).  
- Principle: Christlike communication—charity in word and deed; be peacemakers.

### Questions to Ponder

- When and how do I most clearly “hear Him” in scripture and personal revelation? What will I change to feast more consistently on His word?  
- Which recent prophetic invitations (e.g., “Think celestial,” “Become a peacemaker”) do I need to act on this week?  
- In what settings (home, work, online) do my words most need the Spirit’s influence?  
- Who needs to hear “Thank you,” “I am sorry,” or “I love you” from me today? What will I do about it?  
- How can I better sustain the prophet so that I not only agree with his counsel but also adjust my habits and priorities to follow it?  
- What phrases or patterns of speech should I retire so I can “speak with the tongue of angels”?
