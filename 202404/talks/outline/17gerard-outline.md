### Summary

Elder Jack N. Gerard teaches that integrity is a Christlike attribute modeled perfectly by the Savior and invites us to live true to God, to others, and to our divine identity—especially amid the world’s pull.

### Haiku

Choose truth, come what may  
Christ drank the bitter cup, whole—  
Walk in covenant.

### Key Points

1) True to God  
   - Integrity begins with loving God and embracing absolute truth; we resist worldliness, trust the doctrine of Christ, and “do what is right” even when it sets us apart.

2) True to Others  
   - Integrity shows in consistent words and deeds, especially in our councils and callings; we avoid self-interest, the honors of men, and any appearance of impropriety while seeking the Lord’s will together.

3) True to Our Divine Identity  
   - Remembering who we are in God’s plan guards against self-deception and apostasy; like Joseph and Hyrum, we remain faithful to revealed truth and who the Lord is shaping us to become.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Luke 22:42; Doctrine and Covenants 19:18–19 — The Savior’s integrity in Gethsemane; He “did not shrink.”  
- John 16:33 — “Be of good cheer; I have overcome the world.”  
- Daniel 6 — Daniel’s integrity and God’s deliverance.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 1:16 — Warning against walking “in [our] own way.”  
- Alma 30 (Korihor) — The danger of denying known truth and self-deception.  
- Joseph Smith—History 1:25 — “I knew it, and I knew that God knew it.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 124:15 — Hyrum and “the integrity of his heart.”  
- Job 31:6 — “Let me be weighed in an even balance.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Overcome the World and Find Rest” (Oct. 2022).  
- President Dallin H. Oaks, “The Two Great Commandments” (Oct. 2018).  
- President Spencer W. Kimball on integrity (Teachings/addresses).

### Questions to Ponder

- Where is the world subtly pulling me to lower my standards, and how will I respond with integrity?  
- If my recent choices were made public, would they reflect my covenants and love for God?  
- In councils and callings, how can I better seek the Lord’s will and avoid the “honors of men”?  
- What does being “true to others” look like online, in private messages, and in anonymous posts?  
- How does remembering my divine identity change the way I speak, decide, and act under pressure?  
- What is my “lions’ den” right now, and how will I show faith like Daniel?  
- When have I felt the temptation to shrink, and how did the Savior’s example help me press forward?
