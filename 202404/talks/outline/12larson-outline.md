### Summary

The 2023 Church Auditing Department Report affirms that sacred funds were authorized, recorded, and used according to revealed governance, approved budgets and policies, and prudent financial principles of provident living.

### Haiku

Stewards of tithes stand—  
faith, trust, and discipline keep  
covenant funds safe.

### Key Points

1) Revealed financial governance: By direction in Doctrine and Covenants 120, the Council on the Disposition of the Tithes (First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, and Presiding Bishopric) authorizes how Church funds are used.

2) Independent assurance: The Church Auditing Department—staffed by credentialed professionals and independent of other departments—provides reasonable assurance that contributions, expenditures, and assets are properly recorded and safeguarded.

3) Provident living in practice: The Church models the principles it teaches—living within a budget, avoiding debt, and saving for times of need—while administering sacred funds.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 120 — The Council on the Disposition of the Tithes.
- Doctrine and Covenants 119 — The law of tithing as a standing law.
- Malachi 3:10 — Promises and blessings associated with tithing.
- Luke 16:10–12 — Faithfulness in small things and true stewardship.
- Doctrine and Covenants 104:11–13 — Stewardship and accountability before God.
- Gospel principles: Stewardship; Accountability; Common consent and council governance; Provident living (budgeting, avoiding debt, prudent saving); Integrity and transparency in handling sacred funds.

- In what ways can I better mirror the Church’s example of provident living—budgeting, avoiding unnecessary debt, and saving for future needs?
- What does being a faithful steward over “small things” (Luke 16) look like in my personal and family finances?
- How does paying tithing strengthen my covenant relationship with the Lord and my trust in His promises?
