### Summary

Elder Taylor G. Godoy teaches that when uncertainty and trials threaten our footing, we should turn first to Heavenly Father in sincere prayer, act in faith, accept His timing and ways, and we will not fall.

### Haiku

Call when night grows cold;
Heaven answers in His time—
Stand, and move with faith.

### Key Points

1. Call first upon God in prayer
   - Prayer is our covenant connection to Heavenly Father; He hears and answers in personal, powerful ways that steady us when life is painful or uncertain.
2. Accept God’s timing and act in faith
   - Trust that His ways are higher; combine prayer with humble, persistent action and continue on the covenant path while awaiting His answer.
3. Remember witnesses of deliverance
   - Scriptural and personal examples (Nephi, Helaman’s warriors, Moses, Abraham, Joseph Smith) show that calling on God brings assurance, peace, courage, and miracles according to His will.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 7:7 — Ask, seek, knock: the Lord’s promise to those who call.
- 1 Nephi 17:51 — “How is it that he cannot instruct me?” Trusting the Lord’s capacity to guide.
- Isaiah 55:8–9 — His thoughts and ways are higher than ours.
- Doctrine and Covenants 64:32 — “All things must come to pass in their time.”
- 2 Nephi 32:9 — Pray always so your performance is for the welfare of your soul.
- Alma 58:10–12 — Pouring out souls to God brings assurance, peace, faith, and hope.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:1–2 — “O God, where art thou?” Praying when heaven feels distant.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “The Power of Spiritual Momentum” (Apr. 2022) — Keep moving forward with faith as you pray.
- Gospel principles: Prayer with real intent; faith and works together; humility to accept God’s will; endurance on the covenant path; trusting God amid others’ agency and life’s pains.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I “called” first on God rather than relying on my own strength, and how did it change the outcome?
- What faithful actions can I take after praying to show the Lord I trust His answer and timing?
- How do I respond when God’s answer differs from my expectations or arrives later than I hoped?
- What simple, daily reminders could be my personal “Call, don’t fall” signs?
- How can I build spiritual momentum in prayer during discouraging or silent seasons?
- Whom can I support this week who feels close to “falling,” and how can my prayers and actions help steady them?
