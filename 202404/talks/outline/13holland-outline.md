### Summary

President Jeffrey R. Holland bears personal witness that God hears every prayer and answers in His perfect timing, urging us—through his recent loss, illness, and renewed consecration—to pray like the Savior and live as covenant-keeping disciples preparing for eternal life.

### Haiku

A hidden fire moves,  
Morning, noon, night—pleading faith;  
His timing is love.

### Key Points

1) Prayer that changes us: God hears every prayer and answers each according to His love and timetable; persistent, sincere prayer—vocal or silent—draws heaven’s help and protection, especially amid grief and trial.

2) Pray as the Savior prayed: Jesus prayed often, in many places and postures, and “more earnestly” in Gethsemane; when we don’t know how to pray, we begin and the Holy Ghost guides our petitions—even for enemies.

3) Urgent, covenant discipleship: The Lord seeks disciples He “knows”—faithful, covenant-keeping Saints who rely on angels, priesthood power, prophets, revelation, and Christ’s grace, coming boldly to the “throne of grace” as we prepare for eternal life.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Luke 18:1–8 — The importuning widow: pray always and not faint.  
- Alma 34:17–27 — Pray continually, in homes, for “those who are around you.”  
- James 5:16 — The fervent prayer of the righteous “availeth much.”  
- D&C 10:5; 3 Nephi 18:15 — Pray always to overcome temptation.  
- Romans 8:26 — The Spirit helps when we “know not what we should pray for.”  
- Matthew 5:44 — Pray for enemies and those who despitefully use you.  
- Luke 9:29; Matthew 26:39; Luke 22:44; D&C 19:18 — The Savior’s varied, earnest prayers; His Atonement and “blood from every pore.”  
- Matthew 7:21–23 (JST Matt. 7:23) — Being truly known by Christ through covenant discipleship.  
- Hebrews 4:16; Hebrews 12:22–24 — Come boldly to the throne of grace; Mount Zion, the city of the living God.  
- Job 42:3 — “Things too wonderful” God reveals to the refined faithful.  
- Hymns: “Prayer Is the Soul’s Sincere Desire” (no. 145); “Sweet Hour of Prayer” (no. 142).  
- Principle: Gratitude as holy thought—“gratitude is happiness doubled by wonder.”

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I recognized God answering differently than I asked? How did I feel His love and timing in it?  
- What simple changes can make my prayers more frequent, specific, and sincere (morning/day/night; vocal and in my heart)?  
- How can I let the Holy Ghost guide my prayers when I don’t know what to say—or when praying for those who have hurt me?  
- In what ways can I use prayer as a shield against temptation this week?  
- What “cross” will I more earnestly take up to be known by the Savior as His covenant disciple?  
- How can I invite collective faith (family prayer, fasting, temple prayer rolls, priesthood blessings) into my current challenges?  
- Which prophetic teachings (including President Nelson’s) will I act on now to live with greater urgency and consecration?  
- How will I “come boldly to the throne of grace” this week to receive mercy, help, and spiritual power?
