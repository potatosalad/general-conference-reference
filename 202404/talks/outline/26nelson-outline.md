### Summary

Brother Michael T. Nelson teaches that loving relationships, covenant faithfulness, and united ward councils—modeled by Helaman and the stripling warriors—are Heaven’s way to help youth learn revelation, lead with faith, and gather Israel.

### Haiku

Helaman leads youth  
Love, covenants, councils guide—  
Trust God; let them act.

### Key Points

1) Lead with love and trust: Like Helaman, leaders and parents “number” the youth—know them by name, see their divine identity, build trust, and invite them to counsel, choose, and act; stepping back appropriately lets them learn the pattern of revelation.

2) Covenant-centered discipleship: Honor covenants and trust in Christ’s deliverance; parents have the primary responsibility to teach at home, modeling faith so the gospel is written on hearts and youth become “true at all times.”

3) The Lord’s organization surrounds the rising generation: Bishops counsel with Young Women presidents; councils and ministering mobilize the ward; use inspired resources (Gospel Library, Gospel Living, FSY guide) so the whole ward focuses on strengthening youth and helping them gather Israel.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 53; 56–58 (Helaman and the stripling warriors: “true at all times,” faith taught by mothers, preserved through exactness and trust in God)
- Alma 24:6–19 (laying down the “weapons of rebellion”—the power of covenant keeping)
- Alma 57:21, 26 (obedience with exactness; the preservation of the 2,060)
- Doctrine and Covenants 68:25–28 (parents’ duty to teach children)
- President Russell M. Nelson, Hope of Israel (youth leading the gathering of Israel)
- Elder Dale G. Renlund: See others through Heavenly Father’s eyes (true worth of souls)
- President Henry B. Eyring: Youth learn most from what they feel from you about who they are
- Gospel principles: councils and ministering; pattern of revelation; For the Strength of Youth: A Guide for Making Choices; bishop’s example as priests quorum president
