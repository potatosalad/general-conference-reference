### Summary

Sister J. Anette Dennis teaches that making and keeping sacred covenants—symbolized in baptism, the sacrament, temple ordinances, and wearing the garment of the holy priesthood—helps us “put on” Jesus Christ, deepening our covenant (hesed) relationship with God and inviting His protection, power, and guidance.

### Haiku

Clothed in covenant,  
armor of light, Christ within—  
love’s covering stays.

### Key Points

1) Covenants deepen our relationship with God  
- Covenants invite a hesed (covenantal love) relationship, allowing God to pour out promised blessings, change our nature through Christ’s Atonement, and draw us closer to Him.

2) Ordinances and symbols help us “put on Christ”  
- Baptism symbolizes death and rebirth in Christ; the sacrament symbolizes receiving His flesh and blood; temple covenants center on the Savior, and wearing the garment daily reminds us that His Atonement “covers” us—becoming an armor of light.

3) Live the covenant path—personally and intentionally  
- God sets covenant terms; we witness our willingness through sacred actions. Wearing the garment is a personal sign to God, not to others. All are invited to prepare for temple covenants (not limited to missionaries or engaged couples) to receive increased power and capacity in Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Romans 13:12–14 — “Put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ”; the “armor of light.”
- Galatians 3:27 — “As many of you as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ.”
- Genesis 3:21 — Coats of skins as covering; pointing to Christ’s atoning “covering” (kaphar).
- Mosiah 18:8–10 — Baptismal covenant and belonging to Christ.
- 3 Nephi 18:1–12 — The sacrament as remembrance of Christ’s body and blood.
- Ephesians 6:11–13 — Whole armor of God; protection through righteousness and faith.
- John 1:29 — Jesus Christ, the Lamb of God.
- Gospel principles: The covenant path; hesed (covenant love); ordinances as teaching symbols; the Atonement as a covering; sacred nature of the garment of the holy priesthood as a personal sign to God.
- President Russell M. Nelson’s teachings on covenants and temples (e.g., Let God Prevail; The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation; Overcome the World and Find Rest; Think Celestial!).

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean for me, today, to “put on the Lord Jesus Christ” in thought, word, and action?
- How do the symbols in baptism, the sacrament, and the temple deepen my understanding of Christ and my covenants?
- In what ways have I felt God’s hesed—His covenantal love—because I have kept my covenants?
- How does wearing the garment of the holy priesthood influence my discipleship and daily choices?
- What protections, power, or guidance have I noticed as I strive to honor my covenants?
- If I have not yet received my endowment, what steps can I take with my bishop/branch president to prepare worthily for temple covenants?
- How can I better use the sacrament each week to renew my covenant relationship and invite Christ’s power into my life?
