### Summary

Elder Gary E. Stevenson teaches that discipleship requires balanced, equal devotion to both “love God” and “love your neighbor,” like twin towers of a suspension bridge on which all gospel living depends.

### Haiku

Twin towers of love  
Upward, outward, bound by grace—  
Law and prophets hang.

### Key Points

1. Love the Lord with all your heart, soul, and mind  
   - We show love for God by keeping His commandments—through prayer, scripture study, temple worship, tithing, Sabbath observance, virtue, and daily small acts of devotion that keep us on the covenant path; such obedience leads to lasting happiness.

2. Love your neighbor as yourself  
   - Christlike love reaches all of God’s children regardless of background or circumstance; we actively minister, forgive, lift the weak, and serve—even when it’s inconvenient—seeking out the marginalized and the wounded.

3. Keep the two great commandments in balanced interdependence  
   - Like a suspension bridge, both “towers” must be equally strong: zeal for commandments without charity or love without accountability to God both lead to imbalance; intentional, incremental efforts create a “blueprint” for a discipleship that is both true and kind.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 22:36–40 — The two great commandments; “On these two hang all the law and the prophets.”
- John 14:15 — “If ye love me, keep my commandments.”
- Mosiah 2:41 — The “blessed and happy state” of those who keep the commandments.
- 2 Nephi 26:33 — “All are alike unto God.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 81:5; Isaiah 35:3 — “Succor the weak, lift up the hands which hang down, and strengthen the feeble knees.”
- Luke 10:25–37 — The good Samaritan as a model for loving our neighbor.
- Related teachings: President Russell M. Nelson on living the second great commandment as key to true discipleship; President Dallin H. Oaks on balancing love of God and love of neighbor.
- Gospel principles: covenant loyalty, charity, ministering, repentance and forgiveness, absolute truth and accountability to God, incremental growth through righteous habits.

### Questions to Ponder

- Which “tower” needs strengthening in my life right now—love of God or love of neighbor?
- What specific daily practices will help me love God with all my heart, soul, and mind?
- Who is a neighbor I can lift this week, even if it is inconvenient?
- How can I hold to absolute truth and covenants while showing Christlike compassion to those who choose differently than I do?
- What grudges or judgments should I let go of, and whom do I need to forgive?
- How does the truth that “all are alike unto God” refine my attitudes and language?
- In what ways do temple worship, prayer, and scripture study increase my capacity to minister?
- What simple “blueprint” (two or three concrete goals) will help me keep the two commandments with equal devotion?
