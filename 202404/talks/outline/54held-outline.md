### Summary

Elder Mathias Held teaches that opposition is essential for agency, growth, and joy; as agents we choose our responses to both self-made mistakes and unavoidable trials, turning to Jesus Christ whose Atonement consecrates afflictions for our gain.

### Haiku

Opposition tests—  
we act, not be acted on;  
Christ steadies our path.

### Key Points

1) Agency requires opposition  
- God gave us the power to act, not merely be acted upon; opposing choices help us learn from both right and wrong decisions, and repentance lets us correct course and grow.

2) We choose our response to trials  
- Many hardships are beyond our control, but we can “adjust, learn, seek, choose,” exercising faith, asking the Lord for help, and building on the rock of Jesus Christ so the storms have no power over us.

3) Afflictions are consecrated for our gain  
- Opposition’s “two sides of the coin” refine us: sorrow deepens joy, hunger heightens gratitude; with the Savior’s succor, our experiences shape us for good as we “think celestial,” trust God, and remain grateful.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Nephi 2:11—“There is an opposition in all things.”  
- 2 Nephi 2:16—“The Lord God gave unto man that he should act for himself.”  
- 2 Nephi 2:2—The Lord will consecrate afflictions for our gain.  
- 2 Nephi 2:5—We are “instructed sufficiently to know good from evil.”  
- Moroni 7:16—The Spirit of Christ is given to all to discern good and evil.  
- Alma 7:11–12—Christ takes upon Him our pains and sicknesses and knows how to succor us.  
- Helaman 5:12—Build on the rock of our Redeemer so the whirlwind has no power.  
- 3 Nephi 9:14—“Whosoever will come, him will I receive.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 122:7—“All these things shall give thee experience, and shall be for thy good.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:36—“Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Think Celestial!” (Oct. 2023)—Choosing an eternal perspective amid mortal opposition.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where have I seen past opposition lead me to deeper faith, humility, or joy in Christ?  
- In a current challenge, how can I act as an agent—“adjust, learn, seek, choose”—instead of feeling merely acted upon?  
- What “wrong turn” in my life needs correction through repentance, and what first step will I take this week?  
- How am I building my foundation on Jesus Christ so that life’s “whirlwind” has no power over me?  
- What is the “other side of the coin” in a present trial—what growth, gratitude, or blessing might God be shaping?  
- How can I better recognize the Spirit of Christ to discern good from evil in my daily choices?  
- What fear about the future can I reframe today by choosing to “think celestial” and trust the Lord?
