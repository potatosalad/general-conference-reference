### Summary

Elder Brent H. Nielson bears personal witness—of what he has both seen and heard—that the ongoing Restoration of Jesus Christ’s gospel is accelerating worldwide through temples, missionary work, and covenant disciples who are blessed as they keep God’s commandments, making now the best time to be a Latter-day Saint.

### Haiku

We speak what we know—  
Seen and heard, the work rolls on;  
Saints, armed with light, rise.

### Key Points

1) Witnesses of Christ: The Lord’s servants are “especial witnesses,” and like scriptural disciples, we are to testify of what we have seen and heard; recording our witness strengthens faith and advances the Lord’s work.

2) The ongoing Restoration: The Church is expanding across the earth—temples, family history, and missionary work are flourishing—fulfilling prophecy and the Savior’s commission to teach all nations and gather Israel on both sides of the veil.

3) Covenant discipleship and blessings: The true miracle is faithful members worldwide who are “armed with righteousness”; as we make and keep covenants and keep God’s commandments, we enjoy the “blessed and happy state” and unite our testimonies in Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 107:25 — Seventy as “especial witnesses.”
- Mormon 1:1 — Mormon’s record of things “seen and heard.”
- Acts 4:19–20 — Peter and John: “We cannot but speak the things which we have seen and heard.”
- 3 Nephi 17:16–17 — The people’s witness of what they saw and heard of the Savior.
- Matthew 28:19–20 — The Great Commission to teach and baptize all nations.
- 1 Nephi 14:14 — Saints “armed with righteousness and with the power of God.”
- Mosiah 2:41 — The “blessed and happy state” of commandment keepers.
- Matthew 13:16 — “Blessed are your eyes, for they see: and your ears, for they hear.”
- Isaiah 29:14; 2 Nephi 25:17 — “A marvelous work and a wonder.”
- Joseph Smith’s prophecy that the truth of God will go forth to every land (History of the Church 4:540).
- Gospel principles: Bearing testimony, the ongoing Restoration, gathering Israel (missionary and temple/family history work), covenant living, unified witness under living prophets and apostles.

### Questions to Ponder

- What have I personally seen and heard from the Spirit that confirms the Restoration of the gospel?
- How can I record my witness this week (journal entry, family testimony night, sharing with a friend)?
- In what ways can I speak like Peter and John—with meekness and courage—about my faith in Christ?
- Where do I notice the ongoing Restoration in my life, ward, and community?
- What step will I take to participate in gathering Israel (missionary invitation, ministering, daily example)?
- How will I engage in temple and family history work to bless my kindred dead this week?
- Which commandment is the Spirit inviting me to keep more fully to enjoy the “blessed and happy state”?
- How can I be more intentionally “armed with righteousness” in my daily choices and media use?
- What can I do to support current and future missionaries and hasten the work in my area?
- How can our class bear a united witness that strengthens new members and returning friends?
