### Summary

Elder Ulisses Soares teaches that sacred temple covenants in Jesus Christ give us covenant confidence—power, assurance, and transformation—to face today’s challenges and become higher and holier disciples.

### Haiku

Temple light within,  
Covenants kindle courage—  
Stand and be not moved.

### Key Points

1) Covenant confidence through Christ: Making and keeping temple covenants fills us with quiet, certain assurance in God’s promises, strengthening us to overcome doubt, fear, and adversity while increasing love and service.

2) Preparation changes experience, which transforms life: As we humbly center on the Savior, pursue personal worthiness, and prepare intentionally for the temple, our temple worship deepens—and that divine power flows into our daily living.

3) Live temple truths daily: Being “temple bound” means securing ourselves to Jesus Christ—scheduling regular worship, removing unkind feelings to retain the Spirit, and carrying the temple’s holiness into our homes and relationships.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Hebrews 10:35 — “Cast not away therefore your confidence.”
- 1 Nephi 3:15; 4 — Nephi’s covenant confidence to obtain the plates.
- 1 Nephi 14:14 — Saints “armed with righteousness and with the power of God.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 45:32 — Stand in holy places and be not moved.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:45 — “Then shall thy confidence wax strong in the presence of God.”
- President Russell M. Nelson, Closing Remarks (Oct. 2019) — Personal worthiness and total conversion in preparing for the temple.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation” (Oct. 2021) — The temple at the center of faith and spiritual fortitude.
- Gospel principles: Holiness to the Lord; ordinances of salvation and exaltation; discipleship that is higher and holier; continual preparation and frequent temple worship; purifying our hearts from unkindness to retain the Spirit.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does covenant confidence look like in my life this week, and how does it change how I face a specific trial?
- How can I change my preparation so my next temple visit changes my experience and transforms my life outside the temple?
- Which unkind feelings or habits do I need to discard so the Spirit can be “unrestrained” in my life and home?
- In what practical ways can my family become more “temple bound” (e.g., scheduling our next appointment, family temple preparation)?
- How am I centering my discipleship more fully on Jesus Christ and His atoning power as I make and keep covenants?
- How can I help children and youth develop joyful, confident anticipation for temple worship and lifelong covenant keeping?
