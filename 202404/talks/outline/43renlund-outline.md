### Summary

Elder Dale G. Renlund teaches that living the doctrine of Christ repeatedly, iteratively, and intentionally builds spiritual momentum that transforms us through covenants, the Holy Ghost, and Christlike ministering.

### Haiku

Row toward Christ each day,  
Small strokes build holy power—  
Waves fade as love flows.

### Key Points

1) Live the doctrine of Christ as a virtuous cycle  
- Faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, covenants (baptism and sacrament), receiving the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end are not one-time steps but a repeating, improving cycle that creates spiritual momentum when aimed toward the Savior.

2) Daily discipleship invites the Spirit and divine power  
- Nourish faith daily through prayer, scripture study, repentance, and heeding promptings; the sacrament renews baptismal covenants and invites the companionship of the Holy Ghost; temple covenants connect us more firmly to Jesus Christ, the true source of power.

3) Minister like the Savior—steady, kind, and nonjudgmental  
- Help others at the right time and in the right way; avoid comparison and condemnation; keep ministering with love so they are drawn to Christ, who heals—our role is to love and encourage.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Nephi 31 (the doctrine of Christ; enduring to the end)  
- Mosiah 5:2 (“no more disposition to do evil, but to do good continually”)  
- 3 Nephi 18:28–32 (sacrament and continuing to minister: “I shall heal them”)  
- 2 Nephi 10:24 (saved only in and through the grace of God)  
- John 3:5; Acts 2:38 (born of water and Spirit; the gift of the Holy Ghost)  
- Alma 32 (nourishing faith daily like a seed)  
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:20–21 (power of godliness manifested in ordinances)  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “The Power of Spiritual Momentum” (Apr. 2022)

### Questions to Ponder

- Where have I “stopped paddling” spiritually, and what small daily action will restart my momentum this week?  
- How can I make my prayers, scripture study, and repentance more intentional and iterative rather than routine?  
- In what ways does the sacrament renew my baptismal covenant and invite the Holy Ghost into my life each week?  
- What Christlike attribute is the Spirit inviting me to develop next, and what will I do to practice it daily?  
- How can I better align my direction with the Savior, not just my speed?  
- Which covenant am I preparing to make or more fully keep, and how will I access the Lord’s power through it?  
- Who around me needs a steady hand, a towrope, or simple encouragement—and how can I minister without judgment?  
- How does seeing repentance as joyful change the way I respond when I “fall out of the kayak”?
