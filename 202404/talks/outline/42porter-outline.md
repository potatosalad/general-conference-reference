### Summary

President Susan H. Porter teaches that prayer is a loving gift from Heavenly Father that helps us know He is there, grow to become more like Jesus Christ, and show His love to others—trusting His guidance and timing.

### Haiku

Pray—He is there now;  
Ask what He would have you do;  
Grow, then share His love.

### Key Points

1. Pray to know
   - Heavenly Father, a glorified being and Father of our spirits, hears every prayer; as we pour out our hearts and then listen, He helps us know He is real and we are never alone—shift from asking “why?” to “what can I do and learn?”
2. Pray to grow
   - Through Jesus Christ and His Atonement, prayer brings power to change hearts and develop Christlike attributes; consistent, sincere prayers invite peace and courage to act.
3. Pray to show
   - Pray to be an instrument to comfort, include, forgive, and serve; honor others’ agency while trusting God’s timing—family blessings (including temple sealings) often come after faithful, patient prayer.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 7:7; Luke 11:9 — Ask, seek, knock: God invites us to pray and receive.
- Alma 37:37 — Counsel with the Lord in all your doings; He will direct you for good.
- 1 Nephi 16:23–31 — Nephi’s broken bow: asking “what” to do leads to guidance and provision.
- 2 Nephi 32:9 — Pray always so the Lord may consecrate your performance.
- D&C 130:22; Hebrews 12:9 — The Father has a body of flesh and bones; He is the Father of our spirits.
- 2 Nephi 2:27 — Agency: the Lord will not force choices; He invites and persuades.
- Moroni 7:48; Mosiah 2:17 — Pray for charity and serve others; service is love in action.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives” (Apr 2018) — Pour out your heart in prayer and then listen.
- Children’s Songbook, “A Child’s Prayer” (no. 12) — Faith-filled pattern: “Pray, He is there; speak, He is listening.”
- D&C 128:18 — The sealing work and hearts turned to family across the veil.

### Questions to Ponder

- When I pray, do I make time to listen? How do I recognize thoughts and feelings from the Spirit?
- What recent experience strengthened my witness that Heavenly Father is really there?
- Where could I replace a “why is this happening?” prayer with “what would Thou have me do or learn?”
- Which Christlike attribute do I want to grow this month, and what specific daily prayer and action will I pair with it?
- How can I help a child or youth build a simple, consistent habit of heartfelt prayer?
- Whom can I comfort, include, forgive, or serve today as an answer to their prayer?
- How do I exercise faith when prayers for loved ones seem unanswered because of agency? What can I do faithfully while I wait?
- What does “pray to show” look like in my ministering and in my home this week?
- How can temple covenants and family history work be part of my prayers for multigenerational blessings?
- If every child in our ward prayed daily, what would change in our homes and community—and what can I do to encourage it?
