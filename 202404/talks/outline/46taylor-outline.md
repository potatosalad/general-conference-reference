### Summary

Elder Brian K. Taylor teaches that while miracles and deliverance come in different ways and timings, the Lord consecrates our afflictions for our gain and can swallow up our suffering in the joy of Christ as we put Him first, think celestial, and focus on covenant joy.

### Haiku

Furnace flames refine—  
sorrow, swallowed by His joy;  
scarred hands heal our hearts.

### Key Points

1) God’s timing in miracles varies, but His love is constant  
- Some are healed immediately, others gradually, and some must endure; yet the Lord knows the end from the beginning and does all things for our welfare and happiness.

2) Trials refine us when viewed with divine purpose and perspective  
- Afflictions can sanctify and educate the soul; thinking celestial lightens our load and turns despair into hope, as seen in faithful disciples who find peace amid grief.

3) Three divine principles invite power in adversity  
- Put Jesus Christ first to deepen faith; envision your eternal destiny to increase hope; and focus on joy—“the joy set before” the Savior—to receive strength as your will aligns with the Father’s.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Mark 10:52; Mark 8:22–25; 2 Corinthians 12:7–10 — Different timings of miracles; “when I am weak, then am I strong.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:7–8; 122:7 — “Thine adversity… shall be but a small moment”; “all these things… shall be for thy good.”
- 2 Nephi 2:2 — God can consecrate afflictions for our gain.
- Hebrews 5:8–9 — Christ “learned… obedience by the things which he suffered.”
- Isaiah 48:10 — “Refined… in the furnace of affliction.”
- Hebrews 12:2 — “For the joy that was set before him [He] endured the cross.”
- Mosiah 15:7 — Christ’s will “swallowed up in the will of the Father.”
- Alma 31:38 — Afflictions “swallowed up in the joy of Christ.”
- Matthew 11:28; John 14:18 — “Come unto me… I will not leave you comfortless.”
- Mosiah 24:14–15 — Burdens eased so they are not felt.
- Alma 7:11–12 — The Savior knows how to succor His people.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Think Celestial!” — Eternal perspective lightens our load and orients us to eternal reward.
- Elder D. Todd Christofferson on adversity — Turn to God so suffering refines rather than embitters.
- President Camille N. Johnson, “Jesus Christ Is Relief” — As we bring the Savior’s relief to others, we receive it ourselves.
- Gospel principles: Agency and consecration of trials; covenant belonging; hope in Christ; enduring well; joy as spiritual power.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I seen the Lord’s hand in my life even when the timing of a hoped-for miracle was different than I wanted?
- What does “think celestial” look like in my current trial this week?
- How can I put Jesus Christ first in my thoughts and routines so that faith displaces fear?
- Which eternal promises (covenant blessings, temple covenants, resurrection, sealing) most strengthen my hope right now?
- What “joy set before me” can I fix in my view to endure well today?
- How might my afflictions be consecrated for my gain? What growth is God inviting me to receive?
- Whom can I lift this week so that the Savior’s relief flows to them—and to me?
