### Summary

Elder Patrick Kearon testifies that Heavenly Father’s plan and Jesus Christ’s Atonement are designed to bring every child home—removing barriers through mercy and redemption—and invite us to repent, change, and bind ourselves to Him through covenants.

### Haiku

No roadblocks in God’s plan  
Shepherd seeks the lost with love  
Open arms lead home

### Key Points

1. God’s plan welcomes, not excludes  
   Elder Kearon contrasts a roadblock policeman with God’s “fabulous” plan, emphasizing that the Father relentlessly seeks to bring us home—His intent is happiness, redemption, mercy, and salvation for all.

2. Christ removes the barriers  
   Through the infinite Atonement of Jesus Christ, the obstacles of sin and death are overcome; His ministry and the rent temple veil witness that access to the Father is opened to all who come unto Him.

3. Our part: repent, change, and covenant  
   The Savior’s inclusive invitation is coupled with a call to transform—evict the natural man, experience a mighty change of heart, keep commandments, and yoke ourselves to Him; by grace, these are within our reach.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- The plan’s intent: happiness, mercy, redemption, salvation  
  - Alma 42:8, 15 (plan of happiness; plan of mercy)  
  - Alma 34 (plan of redemption)  
  - Moses 6:62; 2 Nephi 9 (plan of salvation through the Only Begotten)

- Christ’s inclusive reach  
  - 2 Nephi 26:24 (He doeth nothing save it be for the benefit of the world)  
  - 2 Peter 3:9 (not willing that any should perish)  
  - Alma 5:33 (Mine arm of mercy is extended)  
  - Luke 15 (Good Shepherd and lost sheep)

- Repentance and transformation  
  - Mosiah 3:19 (put off the natural man)  
  - Alma 5:14 (mighty change of heart)  
  - John 8:11 (go, and sin no more)

- Access to the Father through Christ  
  - Matthew 27:51 (veil of the temple rent)  
  - Matthew 11:28–30 (take His yoke upon you)

- Gospel principles to study: God’s loving intent; the covenant path; enabling grace; the universality of the Atonement; discipleship that both welcomes and invites change.

### Questions to Ponder

- How does knowing God’s intent is to bring me home change how I see commandments, covenants, and church participation?  
- Where have I assumed there is a roadblock in my life with God—and what step of faith could I take toward Him this week?  
- In what specific way can I “evict the natural man” and seek a mighty change of heart through Christ’s grace?  
- Who around me feels turned away or unworthy, and how can I emulate the Savior’s welcoming reach toward them?  
- When have I felt the Savior “rent the veil” for me—lifting my burdens as I yoke myself to Him?  
- What do I need to bring to Jesus Christ today (pain, sin, fear, doubt) so He can heal me and help me move forward on the covenant path?
