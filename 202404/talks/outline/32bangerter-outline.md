### Summary

Elder Bangerter teaches that each of us was foreordained in the premortal life with divine identity and purpose, and as we protect our private, quiet moments to seek revelation, God will reveal how to serve Him now—especially in the gathering of Israel.

### Haiku

Before earth’s dawn, known—  
Quiet prayers unveil the call;  
Rise and serve with Him.

### Key Points

1) You are foreordained children of God.  
- We lived with God before mortality, received agency, and were appointed to specific missions.  
- Foreordination is real but conditional—opportunities come as we choose righteousness and walk the covenant path.

2) Revelation comes in protected private times.  
- Guard your quiet moments for prayer, scripture study, wholesome recreation, uplifting music, and pondering your patriarchal blessing.  
- In stillness, the Spirit confirms identity, gifts, and assignments; Elder Bangerter’s father taught him to “protect the private times,” which anchored him to Christ.

3) Act in the gathering of Israel.  
- Young men are asked to prepare for and serve missions; for young women, missionary service is a powerful, prayerfully chosen option.  
- As you keep covenants and serve in youth quorums/classes, the temple, ministering, and missions, God will unfold His plan and empower you through Christ’s Atonement.

- 2 Nephi 2:27 — Agency to choose liberty and eternal life.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–28 — Anxiously engaged in a good cause.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Decisions for Eternity” (Oct. 2013).

- What truths has God already shown me about my divine identity and gifts?

- What does my patriarchal blessing teach about my foreordained work?

- Young women: How will I seek and recognize revelation about missionary service?  
- Which covenant I’ve made needs fresh attention to help me fulfill my foreordination?

- What specific action will I take to participate in the gathering of Israel today?
