### Summary

Elder Paul B. Pieper teaches that a deep, personal relationship with God is built as we choose to place complete, continuous trust in Him—especially through “trust-stretching” experiences that invite us to turn to the Lord first and act in faith.

### Haiku

Fall back, heart open—  
Trust turns trials to tutors;  
He catches; we rise.

### Key Points

1) Trust is the foundation of a relationship with God  
- Though we are His spirit children, meaningful relationship requires our choice to trust Him; Heavenly Father shows His trust in us through the plan of salvation—agency, commandments, and the Savior’s atoning power accessed by repentance.

2) Healing broken trust and learning God’s character  
- Past betrayals can make trusting God hard; we can heal by learning who He is, recording experiences of His love and power, praying sincerely, and turning to Him first rather than to the world’s solutions.

3) God stretches us to grow our trust  
- Like Nephi’s successive challenges (plates, broken bow, building a ship), God tutors us through repeated invitations; as we act, our trust deepens, our covenants strengthen us, and God proves He will “catch” us—leading us to become one with the Father and the Son.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Proverbs 3:5–6 — Trust in the Lord with all your heart; acknowledge Him first.  
- John 17:21 — The Savior’s desire that we become one with the Father and the Son.  
- 1 Nephi 3–4 — Trust to obtain the brass plates.  
- 1 Nephi 16:18–32 — Trust when Nephi’s bow broke.  
- 1 Nephi 17–18 — Trust to build a ship.  
- 2 Nephi 4:34 — “O Lord, I have trusted in thee, and I will trust in thee forever.”  
- Gospel principles: Agency and the plan of salvation; repentance through Jesus Christ; covenants that bind us to God; personal revelation and prayer; choosing faith over fear.  
- Referenced teachings: Elder Dale G. Renlund on God’s goal that His children choose to do right; President Russell M. Nelson on how knowing God’s character increases our trust in Him.

### Questions to Ponder

- When I face a challenge, do I turn to the Lord first? What would that look like this week?  
- What current “trust-stretching” invitation is God giving me, and what is my next faithful step?  
- How have past breaches of trust affected my relationship with God, and what practices (study, prayer, recording spiritual experiences) can help me heal and trust Him more?  
- In my prayers, am I asking “Why is this happening?” or “What do You want me to learn?”  
- How do repentance and covenants practically increase my trust in Jesus Christ day to day?  
- Whom can I support as they learn to trust God, and how can I be part of God’s “catching” arms for them?
