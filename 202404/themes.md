### Summary

This conference centered on Jesus Christ and covenant belonging. Again and again, leaders testified that making and keeping sacred covenants—especially in the temple under restored priesthood keys—binds us to the Savior and brings His power, protection, revelation, and enduring joy. President Nelson rejoiced in priesthood keys and invited regular temple worship; Presidents Oaks and Eyring emphasized that covenants are at the heart of the Restoration and that, through Christ, “all will be well.” Many taught that holy places and holy patterns (Sabbath, temple, and home) help us “be still,” receive the Holy Ghost, and face today’s commotion with covenant confidence.

The doctrine of Christ is to be lived repeatedly and intentionally. Testimony usually grows “ray by ray” as we pray, repent, partake of ordinances, and minister. Leaders called us to be valiant in the testimony of Jesus, feast on the Book of Mormon, sustain living prophets, and speak with integrity and love. Discipleship means loving God and neighbor in balanced devotion, building unity and true belonging in Christ, and strengthening the rising generation through relationships and revelation.

Trials and opposition are part of God’s wise plan. Through the Atonement of Jesus Christ, our afflictions can be consecrated for our gain; miracles and ministering angels attend covenant keepers according to God’s timing. As we trust the Lord, call upon Him in prayer, and walk the covenant path, He gathers us, heals us, and brings us safely home.

### Questions to Ponder

- Doctrine and Covenants 110; 2:1–3; 109: “Kirtland keys” and the temple dedicatory prayer—How do temple covenants and priesthood keys bring the Lord’s power into my daily life?
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:19–22—In what ways do ordinances manifest “the power of godliness” to me and my family?
- Malachi 4:5–6; Doctrine and Covenants 2; 128—How are sealing power and family history work strengthening my living and my dead?
- Psalm 46:10; Alma 58:11—What holy times and places help me be still so the Holy Ghost can steady me?
- 2 Nephi 31–32; 3 Nephi 11—Which step in the doctrine of Christ (faith, repentance, ordinances, Holy Ghost, enduring) do I need to iterate with greater intent this week?
- Moroni 7:29–33; Ether 12:6—How can I more faithfully invite miracles and the ministry of angels while trusting God’s timing?
- Romans 8:28; 2 Nephi 2:11—How is the Lord consecrating current opposition “for my good,” and how will I choose to act as an agent, not an object?
- Helaman 5:12; Alma 56–58—What relationships and practices will better anchor the rising generation to Christ?
- Matthew 22:36–40; John 13:34–35—How can I more equally and visibly live both great commandments this month?
- Proverbs 3:5–6; Enos 1—What “trust-stretching” act of faith will I take to turn to the Lord first?
- Moroni 10:3–5; President Mark L. Pace—What is my plan for daily, prayerful Book of Mormon study to invite the Spirit and prevent personal apostasy?
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:41–46; James 3:2–10—How will I use words with charity and lead with integrity and persuasion?
- Revelation 12:11; Elder D. Todd Christofferson—What does it look like for me to be “valiant in the testimony of Jesus” in public and private?
- Doctrine and Covenants 26:2; 107—How does sustaining by common consent and stewardship strengthen my trust in the Lord’s governance of His Church?
