1. Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers - Presented by President Dallin H. Oaks



President Dallin H. Oaks led the sustaining of the First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, General Authority Seventies, Area Seventies, and General Officers, acknowledged releases and emeritus designations, and affirmed the Church’s governance by common consent under priesthood keys.

2. Church Auditing Department Report, 2023 - Presented by Jared B. Larson

To the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

The 2023 Church Auditing Department Report affirms that sacred funds were authorized, recorded, and used according to revealed governance, approved budgets and policies, and prudent financial principles of provident living.

3. Motions of a Hidden Fire - President Jeffrey R. Holland

God hears every prayer we offer and responds to each of them according to the path He has outlined for our perfection.

President Jeffrey R. Holland bears personal witness that God hears every prayer and answers in His perfect timing, urging us—through his recent loss, illness, and renewed consecration—to pray like the Savior and live as covenant-keeping disciples preparing for eternal life.

4. Put Ye On the Lord Jesus Christ - Sister J. Anette Dennis

Through honoring our covenants, we enable God to pour out the multitude of promised blessings associated with those covenants.

Sister J. Anette Dennis teaches that making and keeping sacred covenants—symbolized in baptism, the sacrament, temple ordinances, and wearing the garment of the holy priesthood—helps us “put on” Jesus Christ, deepening our covenant (hesed) relationship with God and inviting His protection, power, and guidance.

5. Pillars and Rays - Elder Alexander Dushku

We too can have our own pillar of light—one ray at a time.

Elder Dushku teaches that most testimonies grow through many small, consistent “rays” of revelation rather than one dramatic “pillar of light,” and as we remember and gather these rays, our faith in Jesus Christ becomes bright enough to dispel darkness.

6. Covenant Confidence through Jesus Christ - Elder Ulisses Soares

When we enter the Lord’s house, we embark on a sacred journey of learning to become higher and holier disciples of Christ.

Elder Ulisses Soares teaches that sacred temple covenants in Jesus Christ give us covenant confidence—power, assurance, and transformation—to face today’s challenges and become higher and holier disciples.

7. Integrity: A Christlike Attribute - Elder Jack N. Gerard

Living a life of integrity requires us to be true to God, to each other, and to our divine identity.

Elder Jack N. Gerard teaches that integrity is a Christlike attribute modeled perfectly by the Savior and invites us to live true to God, to others, and to our divine identity—especially amid the world’s pull.

8. All Will Be Well Because of Temple Covenants - President Henry B. Eyring

There is nothing more important than honoring the covenants you have made or may make in the temple.

President Henry B. Eyring testifies that making and keeping temple covenants brings the Savior’s peace, power, revelation, and enduring family bonds so we can face any trial with confidence that, through Jesus Christ, all will be well.

9. “Be Still, and Know That I Am God” - Elder David A. Bednar

We can be still and know that God is our Heavenly Father, we are His children, and Jesus Christ is our Savior.

Elder David A. Bednar teaches that “being still” is a covenant-rooted focus on Jesus Christ that anchors our lives to His rock through sacred times and holy places—especially Sabbath, temple, and home—so the Holy Ghost can steady us to do and overcome hard things.

10. Rise! He Calleth Thee - Elder Massimo De Feo

The gospel is not a way to avoid challenges and problems but a solution to increase our faith and learn how to deal with them.

Elder De Feo teaches that the gospel gives us clear spiritual vision amid trials as we focus on Jesus Christ, cast off the natural man, and heed His voice with real intent to follow Him.

11. A Record of What I Have Both Seen and Heard - Elder Brent H. Nielson

There has never been a better time to be a member of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

Elder Brent H. Nielson bears personal witness—of what he has both seen and heard—that the ongoing Restoration of Jesus Christ’s gospel is accelerating worldwide through temples, missionary work, and covenant disciples who are blessed as they keep God’s commandments, making now the best time to be a Latter-day Saint.

12. Jesus Christ at the Center of Our Lives - Elder Jose L. Alonso

The profound questions of the soul, those that surface in our darkest hours and highest trials, are addressed through the unwavering love of Jesus Christ.

Elder Alonso teaches that placing Jesus Christ at the center of our lives—especially amid trials—brings healing, revelation, rest, and enduring joy through His Atonement.

13. All Things for Our Good - Elder Gerrit W. Gong

In time and eternity, the purpose of Creation and the nature of God Himself are to bring all things together for our good.

Elder Gerrit W. Gong teaches that through Jesus Christ’s Atonement, our covenants, and God’s eternal perspective, “all things” in mortality—even trials—can be consecrated and work together for our ultimate good.

14. In Support of the Rising Generation - Brother Michael T. Nelson

It is relationships in the lives of the youth that have the greatest influence on their choices.

Brother Michael T. Nelson teaches that loving relationships, covenant faithfulness, and united ward councils—modeled by Helaman and the stripling warriors—are Heaven’s way to help youth learn revelation, lead with faith, and gather Israel.

15. Be One with Christ - Elder Quentin L. Cook

We are united by our love of and faith in Jesus Christ and His Atonement. The essence of truly belonging is to be one with Christ.

Elder Quentin L. Cook teaches that true belonging comes as we become one with Christ through His Atonement and covenants, exercising our moral agency to repent, keep His commandments, and include all of God’s children on the covenant path.

16. Miracles, Angels, and Priesthood Power - Elder Shayne M. Bowen

If you desire the blessings of the priesthood, including miracles and the ministry of angels, walk the path of covenants God has made available.

Elder Shayne M. Bowen testifies that priesthood keys have been restored in the latter days and, as we walk the covenant path and act in faith, we can experience miracles, the ministry of angels, and the guiding power of the Holy Ghost according to God’s will and timing.

17. Foreordained to Serve - Elder Steven R. Bangerter

Our Heavenly Father desires to reveal to you your personal foreordination, and He will do so as you seek to learn and follow His will.

Elder Bangerter teaches that each of us was foreordained in the premortal life with divine identity and purpose, and as we protect our private, quiet moments to seek revelation, God will reveal how to serve Him now—especially in the gathering of Israel.

18. Faithful to the End - Sister Andrea Muñoz Spannaus

By His hand, you will be able to bring down every Goliath that appears in your life.

Sister Andrea Muñoz Spannaus teaches youth to overcome life’s “Goliaths” by anchoring themselves to Jesus Christ through love of God, faith in Christ, divine identity, daily repentance, covenants that connect us to His power, and a personal testimony.

19. Fruit That Remains - Elder Matthew L. Carpenter

Having the Holy Ghost seal our ordinances is essential if we want to have the promised blessings for all eternity.

Elder Matthew L. Carpenter teaches that when we faithfully keep our covenants—especially the new and everlasting covenant of marriage—the Holy Spirit of Promise seals those ordinances so their blessings become “fruit that remains” for eternity, with compassionate guidance for married, single, divorced, and repenting members.

20. A Higher Joy - Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf

May we all seek and find the higher joy that comes from devoting our lives to our Heavenly Father and His Beloved Son.

Elder Uchtdorf teaches that the “higher joy” we all seek is found in following Jesus Christ—drawing near to God, actively looking for joy, and lifting others—bringing peace that endures opposition and outlasts worldly happiness.

21. Words Matter - Elder Ronald A. Rasband

Words set a tone. They voice our thoughts, feelings, and experiences, for good or bad.

Elder Rasband teaches that words matter—God’s words, prophets’ words, and our own—and invites us to feast on the words of Christ, sustain the living prophet, and speak with love and the Spirit.

22. Pray, He Is There - President Susan H. Porter

I invite you to pray to know Heavenly Father is there, pray to grow to become like Him, and pray to show His love to others.

President Susan H. Porter teaches that prayer is a loving gift from Heavenly Father that helps us know He is there, grow to become more like Jesus Christ, and show His love to others—trusting His guidance and timing.

23. The Powerful, Virtuous Cycle of the Doctrine of Christ - Elder Dale G. Renlund

I invite you to live the doctrine of Christ repeatedly, iteratively, and intentionally and help others on their way.

Elder Dale G. Renlund teaches that living the doctrine of Christ repeatedly, iteratively, and intentionally builds spiritual momentum that transforms us through covenants, the Holy Ghost, and Christlike ministering.

24. Trust in the Lord - Elder Paul B. Pieper

Our relationship with God will grow only to the degree we are willing to place our trust in Him.

Elder Paul B. Pieper teaches that a deep, personal relationship with God is built as we choose to place complete, continuous trust in Him—especially through “trust-stretching” experiences that invite us to turn to the Lord first and act in faith.

25. God’s Intent Is to Bring You Home - Elder Patrick Kearon

Everything about the Father’s plan for His beloved children is designed to bring everyone home.

Elder Patrick Kearon testifies that Heavenly Father’s plan and Jesus Christ’s Atonement are designed to bring every child home—removing barriers through mercy and redemption—and invite us to repent, change, and bind ourselves to Him through covenants.

26. Swallowed Up in the Joy of Christ - Elder Brian K. Taylor

I testify that our Heavenly Father hears your tearful pleadings and will always respond in perfect wisdom.

Elder Brian K. Taylor teaches that while miracles and deliverance come in different ways and timings, the Lord consecrates our afflictions for our gain and can swallow up our suffering in the joy of Christ as we put Him first, think celestial, and focus on covenant joy.

27. Covenants and Responsibilities - President Dallin H. Oaks

The Church of Jesus Christ is known as a church that emphasizes making covenants with God.

President Dallin H. Oaks teaches that covenants are sacred commitments central to God’s plan, the Restoration, and temple worship—binding us to Jesus Christ and qualifying us for the blessings of salvation and exaltation.

28. The Testimony of Jesus - Elder D. Todd Christofferson

My invitation is to act now to secure your place as one who is valiant in the testimony of Jesus.

Elder Christofferson teaches that our eternal destiny hinges on how we receive and live “the testimony of Jesus,” inviting us to be valiant disciples by making and keeping covenants, nourishing daily faith, heeding prophets, openly witnessing of Christ, and pursuing personal holiness.

29. Call, Don’t Fall - Elder Taylor G. Godoy

If we call out to God, I testify we will not fall.

Elder Taylor G. Godoy teaches that when uncertainty and trials threaten our footing, we should turn first to Heavenly Father in sincere prayer, act in faith, accept His timing and ways, and we will not fall.

30. Bridging the Two Great Commandments - Elder Gary E. Stevenson

Our ability to follow Jesus Christ depends upon our strength and power to live the first and second commandments with balance and equal devotion.

Elder Gary E. Stevenson teaches that discipleship requires balanced, equal devotion to both “love God” and “love your neighbor,” like twin towers of a suspension bridge on which all gospel living depends.

31. Opposition in All Things - Elder Mathias Held

To be able to exercise our agency, we need to have opposing options to consider.

Elder Mathias Held teaches that opposition is essential for agency, growth, and joy; as agents we choose our responses to both self-made mistakes and unavoidable trials, turning to Jesus Christ whose Atonement consecrates afflictions for our gain.

32. Temples, Houses of the Lord Dotting the Earth - Elder Neil L. Andersen

As you come worthily and prayerfully to His holy house, you will be armed with His power.

Elder Neil L. Andersen teaches that in the commotion of the last days, the Lord is dotting the earth with temples to strengthen His covenant people—arming them with power, placing His name upon them, surrounding them with angels, and filling them with the Holy Ghost as they worship and keep sacred covenants.

33. It Is Wisdom in the Lord That We Should Have the Book of Mormon - President Mark L. Pace

It is my prayer that reading the Book of Mormon this year will be a joy and a blessing for each of us.

President Mark L. Pace teaches that daily, prayerful study of the Book of Mormon invites the Holy Ghost, strengthens homes and classes, and protects us from personal apostasy in this final dispensation.

34. Rejoice in the Gift of Priesthood Keys - President Russell M. Nelson

Priesthood keys govern how the priesthood of God may be used to bring about the Lord’s purposes and bless all who accept the restored gospel.

President Nelson rejoices in the Restoration of priesthood keys—especially those restored in the Kirtland Temple—and promises that faithful, regular temple worship unlocks gathering, Abrahamic, and sealing blessings, bringing divine power, protection, revelation, and joy to every covenant-keeping disciple.
