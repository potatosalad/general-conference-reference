1. Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers - Presented by President Dallin H. Oaks



President Dallin H. Oaks conducted the sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers by common consent, invited members to manifest their support (or share concerns appropriately), expressed gratitude for released leaders, and announced new callings, reaffirming the Church’s order under living prophets, seers, and revelators.

2. Church Auditing Department Report, 2024 - Presented by Jared B. Larson

To the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints

The Church Auditing Department reported that, under the direction of the Council on the Disposition of the Tithes (Doctrine and Covenants 120), Church funds in 2024 were received, spent, and safeguarded in all material respects according to approved budgets, policies, and accounting practices, reflecting gospel principles of provident, debt-averse stewardship.

3. As a Little Child - President Jeffrey R. Holland

I testify that babies and children and youth are images of the kingdom of God flourishing on earth in all of its strength and beauty.

President Jeffrey R. Holland teaches that to truly follow Jesus Christ we must become as little children—humble, pure, trusting, and loving—and he illustrates this through scripture, contrasts with pride, and a moving story of a deacon’s courageous priesthood service, concluding with a witness of the Restoration and of Jesus Christ.

4. Spiritually Whole in Him - President Camille N. Johnson

Wholeness does not necessarily mean physical and emotional restoration in this life. Wholeness is born of faith in and conversion to Jesus Christ.

President Camille N. Johnson teaches that while full physical and emotional healing may come in the Lord’s time, we can be spiritually whole now through faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, covenants, and letting His light shine through us.

5. Right Before Our Eyes - Elder Ronald A. Rasband

The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints is growing in members and families, missions and missionaries, meetinghouses and temples.

Elder Rasband testifies that the Lord is hastening His work right before our eyes—seen in the worldwide growth of temples, missionary service, and Church education—and invites us to hasten our holiness by worshiping in the temple, giving the Lord equal time, and trusting wholly in Jesus Christ.

6. The Atonement of Jesus Christ Provides the Ultimate Rescue - Elder Quentin L. Cook

As we turn to Jesus Christ, the Savior of the world, He rescues us from the storms of life through His Atonement.

Elder Quentin L. Cook testifies that the Atonement of Jesus Christ is the ultimate rescue from sin, death, and life’s storms, inviting us to be rescuers, live joyfully in gratitude, and regularly remember the Savior through sacrament and temple worship.

7. My Love for the Savior Is My “Why” - Elder Ricardo P. Giménez

I love our Savior. This is the real and most powerful reason why I do what I do.

Elder Ricardo P. Giménez teaches that our deepest motivation for discipleship—our “why”—should be love for Jesus Christ, which transforms traditions, goals, and prophetic invitations into covenant-centered pathways that bring us to Him.

8. “Draw Near unto Me” - President Henry B. Eyring

Jesus Christ loves each of us. He offers us the opportunity to draw closer to Him.

President Henry B. Eyring teaches that we draw nearer to Jesus Christ through prayer, scripture study, covenant remembrance (especially the sacrament), and cheerful service, which invites the Spirit, strengthens our witness, and brings the Savior’s love and power into our lives and families.

9. Cherishing Life - Elder Neil L. Andersen

Life is a most precious part of our Father’s perfect plan, and by His decree we cherish and preserve life.

Elder Neil L. Andersen testifies that disciples of Jesus Christ cherish and protect mortal life—especially the unborn—by ministering with Christlike love, upholding revealed doctrine on the sanctity of life and moral agency, and trusting the healing power of the Savior’s Atonement.

10. Divine Authority, Sublime Young Men - President Steven J. Lund

I am forever grateful that holders of the Aaronic Priesthood, with its powers, ordinances, and duties, do bless all of us.

President Lund teaches that the Aaronic Priesthood is divinely restored authority that prepares young men to bless the Church and the world through service, ordinances, and covenant-powered ministry—most centrally in the sacrament.

11. “Return unto Me … That I May Heal You” - Elder S. Mark Palmer

There is rejoicing in heaven over those who return. It is not too late for you to come back.

Elder S. Mark Palmer lovingly invites all who have stepped away—or are barely holding on—to return to Jesus Christ, promising patient healing, nourishing ordinances, and rejoicing in heaven as faith and discipleship are renewed.

12. Faith: A Bond of Trust and Loyalty - Elder Sandino Roman

Faith sprouts as we trust in Jesus Christ and blooms as we are faithful and loyal to Him.

Elder Sandino Roman teaches that faith in Jesus Christ begins as trust, matures into loyal covenant-keeping, and empowers us to face adversity while letting God’s will and miracles prevail.

13. Personal Preparation to Meet the Savior - Elder Dale G. Renlund

Follow the Savior’s teachings. His instructions are neither mysterious nor complex. When we follow them, we do not need to fear or be anxious.

Elder Dale G. Renlund teaches that we can joyfully prepare to meet the Savior—at His Second Coming or at life’s end—by covenanting to follow Him, taking the Holy Ghost as our guide, avoiding deception, magnifying our God-given gifts through His grace, and serving “the least of these.”

14. True to the Faith That Our Parents Have Cherished - Elder Hans T. Boom

Please learn and receive strength from the faith and testimonies of those who came before you.

Elder Hans T. Boom teaches the rising generation to draw strength from the faith and sacrifices of parents and pioneers, to make and keep covenants through trials, and to “carry on” by serving Christ—especially through missionary service—and building their lives on His rock.

15. “By This All Will Know That You Are My Disciples” - Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf

Our love for God and His children is a powerful testimony to the world that this is truly the Savior’s Church.

Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf teaches that the defining witness of Christ’s true Church is the love His disciples show, which invites belonging, bridges the gap between the ideal and the real, and unites diverse members in one body and one voice centered on Jesus Christ.

16. And We Talk of Christ - Elder Gary E. Stevenson

We are followers of Jesus Christ, and we seek to both receive and share His light.

Elder Gary E. Stevenson teaches that disciples of Jesus Christ receive and share His light by talking of, centering on, and testifying of the living, resurrected Savior—especially through Christ-focused worship and a higher, holier observance of Easter—and affirming the literal reality of the Resurrection.

17. Thou Art the Christ - Sister Amy A. Wright

We want our children to believe in Jesus Christ, belong to Jesus Christ and His Church through covenant, and strive to become like Jesus Christ.

Sister Amy A. Wright teaches that lifelong discipleship begins in the home and Primary as we help children believe in Jesus Christ, belong to Him and His Church through covenants, and become like Him through conversion and action.

18. The Plan of Mercy - Elder James R. Rasband

The Lord is merciful and our Heavenly Father’s plan of salvation is truly a plan of mercy.

Elder Rasband teaches that the Lord manifests Himself in mercy—especially in His temples—and that the Father’s plan of salvation is a plan of mercy, accessed through covenants, humble pleas for forgiveness, and pondering God’s mercy as taught in the Book of Mormon.

19. Our Heavenly Guidance System - Elder Sergio R. Vargas

As we focus our lives on Jesus Christ, we will find our way home, enduring to the end and rejoicing to the end.

Elder Sergio R. Vargas teaches that Jesus Christ is our divine “guidance system”—through the Light of Christ, covenants, and steady spiritual nourishment we can resist the hooks of mortality, endure with joy, and return home to our Heavenly Parents.

20. Worship - Elder D. Todd Christofferson

What does worshipping God mean for you and me?

Elder D. Todd Christofferson teaches that true worship includes devoted Sabbath and daily actions, a grateful heart that yields to God’s will, exclusive loyalty to the Father and the Son, and life-changing emulation of Them through covenants and the Holy Ghost.

21. The Times of Restitution of All Things - Elder David A. Bednar

The most important and glorious “good news” is the message that the Lord Jesus Christ has restored His gospel and Church in the latter days.

Elder David A. Bednar testifies that Jesus Christ has restored His gospel and Church in the latter days, outlining the divine sequence of the First Vision, the Book of Mormon, priesthood authority and keys, the formal organization of the Church, and our role in the dispensation of the fulness of times, and inviting all to seek a personal witness by the Holy Ghost.

22. Participate to Prepare for Christ’s Return - Elder Steven D. Shumway

Callings and other ways we embark in God’s work uniquely prepare us to meet the Savior.

Elder Steven D. Shumway teaches that saying yes to callings and other forms of service is God’s way of preparing us—and those we love—to meet the Savior at His return through steady progress, covenant living, and enabling grace.

23. Your Repentance Doesn’t Burden Jesus Christ; It Brightens His Joy - Sister Tamara W. Runia

The invitation to repent is an expression of God’s love. Saying yes to that invitation is an expression of ours.

Sister Tamara W. Runia teaches that repentance is a loving, hopeful invitation from Jesus Christ that does not burden Him but brightens His joy; our worth is constant, shame is from the adversary, and daily, affectionate obedience keeps us in a covenant relationship with the Savior who knows our “degree of difficulty” and advocates for us.

24. Compensating Blessings - Bishop Gérald Caussé

While many circumstances in life may be beyond our control, none of us is beyond the reach of the Lord’s infinite blessings.

Bishop Gérald Caussé teaches that when righteous desires are obstructed by circumstances beyond our control, the Lord—because of His perfect love, justice, and the Atonement of Jesus Christ—provides compensating blessings as we do all we can and offer Him our whole souls.

25. Eternity’s Great Gifts: Jesus Christ’s Atonement, Resurrection, Restoration - Elder Gerrit W. Gong

We find in Easter in Jesus Christ peace, becoming, and belonging—that which is enduringly real and joyful, happy and forever.

Elder Gerrit W. Gong testifies that through Jesus Christ’s Atonement, Resurrection, and Restoration, God meets us in our tears—healing and transforming us with peace, helping us become whole, and gathering us to belong in covenant families and His restored Church.

26. Joy Through Covenant Discipleship - Elder John A. McCune

As we bind ourselves to act as covenant disciples, our relationship with the Father and Son is enriched, our joy enhanced, and our eternal perspective expanded.

Covenant discipleship binds us to the Father and the Son, deepens our trust in Their perfect love and justice, and endows us with spiritual power to experience Christ’s joy and an eternal perspective—even amid mortal trials—while turning outward to gather Israel.

27. Divine Helps for Mortality - President Dallin H. Oaks

Our Heavenly Father’s plan provides helps to guide us through our mortal journeys.

President Oaks teaches that our Father’s plan equips us with divine helps—centered in Jesus Christ’s Atonement, the Light of Christ, commandments and covenants, and the Holy Ghost—to grow through opposition, choose good, and be prepared to meet the Lord.

28. Reverence for Sacred Things - Elder Ulisses Soares

Reverence for the sacred fosters genuine gratitude, expands true happiness, leads our minds to revelation, and brings greater joy to our lives.

Elder Ulisses Soares teaches that reverence for sacred things is the essence of spirituality—connecting us to God, deepening gratitude and joy, safeguarding revelation and covenants, guiding our use of technology, and shaping how we worship in our homes, sacrament meetings, and temples.

29. Charity—a Sign of True Discipleship - Elder Michael B. Strong

The real destination of discipleship is literally to become as Jesus Christ is.

Elder Michael B. Strong teaches that true discipleship is a deliberate journey to become like Jesus Christ, and its defining sign is charity—pure, active love shown through compassion, noticing unspoken needs, and helping others along the covenant path.

30. Beware the Second Temptation - Elder Scott D. Whiting

Don’t hide from those who will love and support you; rather, run to them.

Elder Scott D. Whiting teaches that after we fall to “first temptations,” the adversary’s “second temptation” is to hide from God and His people—yet we cannot hide from the Lord, and healing comes as we run to Christ, repent, and lean on a loving covenant community.

31. Harden Not Your Heart - Elder Christopher H. Kim

If we sincerely repent, humble ourselves, and trust and rely on the Lord, our hearts will be softened.

Elder Christopher H. Kim teaches that hardness of heart blinds us to the Spirit, but through daily repentance, humility, and trusting in Jesus Christ, the Lord will soften our hearts, deepen our understanding, and bless us with His Spirit.

32. Receive His Gift - Elder Patrick Kearon

You are a beloved daughter of God, you are a cherished son of God, and He has gifted you His perfect, holy Son.

Elder Patrick Kearon teaches that our Heavenly Father—the ultimate Giver—offers His greatest gift, Jesus Christ, and invites us to actively receive the transformative truth that we are God’s children, which unlocks every other divine gift.

33. The Love of God - Elder Benjamin M. Z. Tai

I joyfully witness that the Savior Jesus Christ is the love of God. His love for us is perfect, personal, and perpetual.

Elder Benjamin M. Z. Tai teaches that Jesus Christ is the embodiment of Heavenly Father’s perfect, personal, and perpetual love, which we can consistently feel as we exercise faith, repent and forgive, make and keep temple covenants, and serve others by following the Spirit.

34. Confidence in the Presence of God - President Russell M. Nelson

As we diligently seek to have charity and virtue fill our lives, our confidence in approaching God will increase.

President Russell M. Nelson teaches that as we fill our lives with charity and virtue—strengthened by temple worship and covenant keeping—our confidence to approach God now and in the world to come will “wax strong.”
