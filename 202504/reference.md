# General Conference 2025-04

## Themes

### Summary

This conference centered on Jesus Christ—His Atonement as our ultimate rescue and gift—and invited us to draw near to Him through humble, childlike faith, daily repentance, and covenant discipleship. Leaders repeatedly emphasized:
- Believing, belonging, and becoming in Christ: nurturing faith in homes and Primary; belonging through covenants and Church participation; becoming like Him through charity and service.
- Covenant worship: elevating the sacrament, making temple worship a priority, and living a holier Sabbath and a more Christ-focused Easter.
- Charity as the defining sign of true discipleship; letting love bridge differences and witness to the world that we are His.
- Personal preparation to meet the Savior: taking the Holy Ghost as our guide, softening our hearts, avoiding deception, and joyfully engaging in callings, missionary work, and ministering.
- Trust in God’s “plan of mercy”: opposition as a tutor, compensating blessings amid unmet righteous desires, and hope for those returning to the covenant path.
- Reliance on divine helps: Jesus Christ’s Atonement, the Light of Christ, commandments and covenants, and the Holy Ghost.
- Anchoring in the Restoration: priesthood authority and keys, worldwide temple growth, and the Lord hastening His work—under prophetic direction with provident, transparent stewardship.

The call was clear: receive the Father’s greatest Gift—His Son—bind yourself to Him through covenants, let His love transform you into a true disciple, and participate with joy in gathering Israel as you prepare to meet Him.

### Haiku

Children at His feet,  
Covenants bind hearts to Christ—  
Temple light, pure love.

## Talks

## 1. Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers - Presented by President Dallin H. Oaks

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/11oaks?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/11oaks?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Dallin H. Oaks conducted the sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers by common consent, invited members to manifest their support (or share concerns appropriately), expressed gratitude for released leaders, and announced new callings, reaffirming the Church’s order under living prophets, seers, and revelators.

### Haiku

Hands rise in one faith—  
Common consent binds our hearts;  
Christ orders His Church.

### Key Points

1. The law of common consent: Members were invited to sustain the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles as prophets, seers, and revelators, and to manifest support or share any opposition through proper priesthood channels (stake president).
2. Releases with gratitude: Several General Authority Seventies received emeritus status; the Young Men General Presidency and many Area Seventies were released, with heartfelt thanks for their devoted service.
3. New calls and continued order: New General Authority Seventies, a new Young Men General Presidency, and adjustments in leadership (including President Oaks as President of the Quorum of the Twelve and Elder Holland as Acting President) were sustained, reflecting the Lord’s ongoing, orderly guidance of His Church.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 26:2; 20:65–67 — The Church operates “by common consent” and leaders are called and sustained properly.
- Doctrine and Covenants 21:4–5; 1:38 — Heeding the prophet’s voice is heeding the Lord’s voice.
- Ephesians 4:11–13 — Apostles and prophets are given to perfect the Saints and unify the body of Christ.
- Mosiah 18:8–10 — Covenants to bear one another’s burdens inform how we sustain leaders and fellow Saints.
- Hebrews 13:17 — Be guided by those who watch over our souls.
- Articles of Faith 5 — Leaders are called of God by prophecy and laying on of hands.
- Gospel principles: Common consent; sustaining as a covenantal act of faith; gratitude for released servants; unity and order in the Lord’s Church.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean for me to sustain leaders beyond raising my hand?
- How can I seek and follow personal revelation as I support living prophets and apostles?
- If I have concerns, how can I address them faithfully and appropriately through priesthood channels?
- In what specific ways will I pray for, defend, and assist those newly called in my ward, stake, and the Church?
- How can I show gratitude and continued Christlike support to those who have been released?
- How does the principle of common consent strengthen unity in my family and congregation?
- What small, concrete action can I take this week to better sustain a leader or calling in my local unit?

## 2. Church Auditing Department Report, 2024 - Presented by Jared B. Larson

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/12larson?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/12larson?lang=eng)

### Summary

The Church Auditing Department reported that, under the direction of the Council on the Disposition of the Tithes (Doctrine and Covenants 120), Church funds in 2024 were received, spent, and safeguarded in all material respects according to approved budgets, policies, and accounting practices, reflecting gospel principles of provident, debt-averse stewardship.

### Haiku

Tithes in trusted hands  
Councils seek the will of God—  
Stewards save to bless.

### Key Points

1. Council-led stewardship: As revealed in Doctrine and Covenants 120, the First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, and Presiding Bishopric authorize the expenditure of sacred funds; Church entities disburse funds according to approved budgets, policies, and procedures.
2. Independent oversight: The Church Auditing Department, staffed by credentialed professionals and independent of other Church departments, provides reasonable assurance regarding contributions, expenditures, and the safeguarding of assets.
3. Audit opinion and example: For 2024, the auditors state that, in all material respects, funds were recorded and administered properly; the Church models provident living by budgeting, avoiding debt, and saving for times of need.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 120 — The Council on the Disposition of the Tithes directs how sacred funds are used.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 119 — The law of tithing and consecrated temporal service to the Lord.  
- Malachi 3:10 — Promises to tithe payers and the Lord’s “windows of heaven.”  
- Luke 16:10 — Faithfulness in small things as a measure of true discipleship and trustworthiness.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 104:11–15 — Stewardship, accountability, and consecrated resources.  
- Gospel principles: Provident living, accountability, living within a budget, avoiding unnecessary debt, saving for times of need, and sustaining councils.

### Questions to Ponder

- How does knowing that sacred funds are overseen by a prophetic council (D&C 120) and audited independently strengthen my trust in the Lord’s Church?  
- What can I learn from the Church’s example of budgeting, avoiding debt, and saving that I can apply in my home and calling?  
- In what ways does paying a full tithe invite me to be a more faithful steward over all the Lord has given me?  
- How do councils and accountability in my ward or organization help ensure our resources are used to bless people as the Lord intends?  
- What does it mean to seek “reasonable assurance” in spiritual and temporal matters, and how can I pair diligence with faith in my stewardship?  
- How can I better align my financial choices with the principle of “saving against a time of need” to be prepared to serve and bless others?

## 3. As a Little Child - President Jeffrey R. Holland

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/13holland?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/13holland?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Jeffrey R. Holland teaches that to truly follow Jesus Christ we must become as little children—humble, pure, trusting, and loving—and he illustrates this through scripture, contrasts with pride, and a moving story of a deacon’s courageous priesthood service, concluding with a witness of the Restoration and of Jesus Christ.

### Haiku

Become as children—  
humble hearts trust, love, and serve;  
Christ lifts broken ones.

### Key Points

1) Become as little children  
- Jesus taught that greatness in His kingdom comes through humility, purity, and submissiveness; scriptures and prophetic counsel contrast these childlike attributes with the vanity and pride that erode true discipleship.

2) Childlike faith in action  
- Easton Jolley’s determined, reverent service in passing the sacrament—despite severe physical challenges—models trust, courage, loyalty, and love, showing how priesthood service and the sacrament draw us to Christ.

3) Centered on Christ and the Restoration  
- President Holland bears witness of Joseph Smith’s First Vision, President Russell M. Nelson’s prophetic calling, the Book of Mormon as a keystone, and the healing strength of priesthood and prayer, urging us to truly follow and worship the Savior.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 18:1–4 — “Except ye… become as little children.”  
- Mosiah 3:19 — Overcoming the natural man by becoming “as a child.”  
- 3 Nephi 17:11–25 — “Behold your little ones”; the Savior’s tenderness toward children.  
- Ecclesiastes 1:2 — “Vanity of vanities”; the emptiness of pride and worldliness.  
- 1 Nephi 8:26–33; 11:35 — The great and spacious building: pride of the world.  
- Alma 31:27–28 — The Zoramites’ proud prayers vs. true worship.  
- John 17:4 — “I have… finished the work thou gavest me to do.”  
- Revelation 1:5; 22:13 — Jesus Christ, the Faithful Witness; Alpha and Omega.  
- Elder Richard L. Evans on taking Christ seriously—moving from admiration to obedient discipleship.  
- Gospel principles: humility, purity, trust, repentance, reverent prayer, sacrament-centered living, sustaining living prophets, the refining power of priesthood service.

### Questions to Ponder

- Which childlike attributes (humility, trust, meekness, love) do I most need to cultivate right now?  
- Where has pride or “vain imaginations” crept into my worship or discipleship, and how will I replace admiration with obedience to Christ?  
- How can I approach the sacrament more reverently so it changes my heart each week?  
- In what ways can I better support children and youth—and those with disabilities—so they can serve and grow in the gospel while honoring their agency?  
- How are priesthood and prayer currently “restoring” my life, and what covenant actions will I take to invite more of that power?  
- What “work” has God given me this week, and how will I, like Easton, finish it with faith and love?  
- How can our ward more fully “behold [our] little ones” and learn from their example of pure devotion to Christ?

## 4. Spiritually Whole in Him - President Camille N. Johnson

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/14johnson?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/14johnson?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Camille N. Johnson teaches that while full physical and emotional healing may come in the Lord’s time, we can be spiritually whole now through faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, covenants, and letting His light shine through us.

### Haiku

Oil in waiting lamps—  
faith turns weakness into light;  
whole before healed.

### Key Points

1) Wholeness vs. healing  
   - The Savior healed ten lepers, but only one was made whole; likewise, through faith and repentance like Enos, we can be spiritually whole in Christ even as we wait for physical and emotional healing.

2) The oil of conversion and covenant belonging  
   - The wise virgins were ready because their lamps were filled with the oil of conversion—made and kept through covenants, temple worship, obedience, and daily discipleship; we cannot borrow oil, but we can share our light.

3) Strength in Christ amid affliction; joyful witness  
   - Like Paul, the Lord’s grace can make us strong in weakness; like Mary Magdalene, disciples who know the risen Christ rejoice and testify while trusting that complete healing will come in the Resurrection.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Luke 17:11–19 — Ten lepers; gratitude, faith, and being “made whole.”
- Enos 1:1–8 — Forgiveness and wholeness “because of thy faith in Christ.”
- Matthew 25:1–13 — Ten virgins; oil of conversion; “I know you not” (v. 12).
- Doctrine and Covenants 33:17 — “Be faithful… having your lamps trimmed and burning.”
- Matthew 5:14–16; 3 Nephi 12:14–16 — Let your light shine.
- 2 Corinthians 12:7–10 — “My grace is sufficient… strength made perfect in weakness.”
- Philippians 4:4 — “Rejoice in the Lord alway[s].”
- Isaiah 25:8 — “He will swallow up death… wipe away tears.”
- John 20:11–16 — Mary Magdalene’s witness of the risen Lord.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:34–36 — “Many are called, but few are chosen.”
- Alma 11:43–44; 1 Corinthians 15:20–22 — Restoration and universal resurrection.
- Gospel principles: Conversion as covenant loyalty; faith-hope cycle; agency and submission of the heart; joyful endurance; sharing light while retaining personal oil; grace in weakness; temple worship deepens wholeness.

### Questions to Ponder

- In what specific ways can I choose to be spiritually whole in Christ while I wait for healing?  
- What does “oil of conversion” look like in my daily discipleship this week?  
- How do covenants (baptism, sacrament, temple) help me be known by the Bridegroom?  
- Where am I trying to “borrow oil” instead of building my own conversion?  
- How can I share my light without depleting my oil—supporting others while nurturing my own discipleship?  
- What “thorn in the flesh” invites me to rely more fully on the Savior’s grace?  
- How can gratitude (like the returning leper) deepen my wholeness in Christ today?  
- What practices (prayer, scripture, temple, service, repentance) most help me submit my heart to the Lord for change?  
- When have I felt “strength made perfect in weakness,” and what did I learn about Jesus Christ?  
- How can Mary Magdalene’s witness shape my own joyful testimony of the risen Lord this week?

## 5. Right Before Our Eyes - Elder Ronald A. Rasband

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/15rasband?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/15rasband?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Rasband testifies that the Lord is hastening His work right before our eyes—seen in the worldwide growth of temples, missionary service, and Church education—and invites us to hasten our holiness by worshiping in the temple, giving the Lord equal time, and trusting wholly in Jesus Christ.

### Haiku

Right before our eyes  
Temples rise, hearts turn to Christ—  
Hasten holiness.

### Key Points

1) Prophecy fulfilled and momentum building  
   - Ancient and modern prophecies are unfolding as the Church expands across the world; disciples are invited to stand tall, see the “majesty of this moment,” and echo “Hurrah for Israel.”

2) Temples and covenants hasten holiness  
   - With unprecedented temple growth, members are called to live temple worthy, attend regularly, and make and keep covenants for themselves and their ancestors; temple worship brings protection, peace, and power amid increasing adversarial assaults.

3) Gathering and learning by study and faith  
   - Missionary work is gathering souls to Christ; seminary, institute, Church universities, and BYU–Pathway expand access to spiritual and temporal preparation; all are counseled to “give the Lord equal time,” let God prevail, and turn to the Savior for healing and answers.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 88:118 — Seek learning by study and by faith.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:36 — Look unto the Lord in every thought; doubt not, fear not.  
- Matthew 11:29 — Learn of Christ and find rest unto your souls.  
- 1 Peter 5:8 — Be vigilant; the adversary seeks to devour.  
- Luke 17:23 — “Lo, here” and “lo, there” warnings; focus on the true work of the Lord.  
- Joseph Smith’s “Standard of Truth” declaration — No unhallowed hand can stop the work from progressing.  
- Elder Melvin J. Ballard (1926) — The South America “acorn to oak” prophecy.  
- President Russell M. Nelson — Let God Prevail; spiritual momentum; increased need for regular temple worship.  
- Gospel principles: Hastening the work, covenant path, gathering Israel (missionary and temple work), discipleship, consecrated time, education with the Savior at the center.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where do I see the Lord hastening His work “right before my eyes” in my family, ward, or community?  
- What specific pattern of regular temple worship will I adopt this month to “hasten my holiness”?  
- How will I “give the Lord equal time” each day in scripture study, prayer, temple attendance, and ministering?  
- In what ways can I personally participate in the gathering of Israel (missionary efforts, invitations, family history, ordinances for the dead)?  
- How can I better unite my learning “by study and by faith” in school, work, or home?  
- What doubts, wounds, or questions will I bring to Jesus Christ, and how will I “look unto [Him] in every thought”?  
- How can I help youth and new members catch the “Hurrah for Israel” spirit of joyful discipleship?  
- What time or habits should I reallocate so God can more fully prevail in my life?

### Summary

President Russell M. Nelson teaches that as we fill our lives with charity and virtue—strengthened by temple worship and covenant keeping—our confidence to approach God now and in the world to come will “wax strong.”

### Haiku

Charity, virtue—  
steady hearts before God’s throne;  
temples teach us peace.

### Key Points

1) Charity and peacemaking invite the Spirit: True disciples reject anger, hostility, and contention; charity toward “all men” softens our speech, ends judging, and makes us instruments of the Prince of Peace.

2) Virtue purifies thought and increases spiritual confidence: Garnishing thoughts “unceasingly” with virtue displaces impure, cruel, and anxious thinking and strengthens our ability to pray and feel the Holy Ghost.

3) Covenants and temple worship prepare us for the Savior’s return: Making and keeping covenants in the temple expands our capacity for charity and virtue, builds confidence to approach God, and helps us intentionally prepare for the Second Coming.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 121:45–46 — Charity and virtue lead to confidence that “wax[es] strong in the presence of God.”
- Moroni 7:47–48 — Charity is the pure love of Christ; pray to be filled with it.
- 3 Nephi 11:29–30 — Contention is not of Christ; peacemaking invites His Spirit.
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:88 — The Lord sends angels to bear us up.
- Isaiah 9:6 — Jesus Christ is the Prince of Peace.
- Matthew 24:36; Malachi 4:5 — Prepare for the “great and dreadful day” of the Lord; we do not know the day or hour.
- Gospel principles: Covenant keeping; confidence before God in prayer; constant companionship of the Holy Ghost; temple worship; discipleship in public and private discourse.

- Related message: President Russell M. Nelson, “Peacemakers Needed” (April 2023).

### Questions to Ponder

- How would my prayers change if I believed—right now—that God hears me, knows my needs, and sends angels to help?
- Where can I replace contention with peacemaking in my home, ward, workplace, or online interactions?
- What thoughts most need “virtue” added to them, and what practical steps will help me garnish my thoughts unceasingly?
- What covenant I’ve made could I honor more intentionally this week?
- How does regular temple worship increase my capacity to love and to think virtuously?
- Whom is the Lord inviting me to stop judging and start serving with Christlike charity?
- What media, habits, or patterns fuel anxiety or impure thoughts—and what will I remove or replace?
- In what specific ways am I preparing for the Second Coming, personally and as a family?
