### Summary

Bishop Gérald Caussé teaches that when righteous desires are obstructed by circumstances beyond our control, the Lord—because of His perfect love, justice, and the Atonement of Jesus Christ—provides compensating blessings as we do all we can and offer Him our whole souls.

### Haiku

Bound by mortal ties,  
Grace fills gaps our hands can’t span—  
He accepts our hearts.

### Key Points

1. God’s perfect love and just plan ensure access to promised blessings  
   - The Lord knows our situations and “inviteth all” to come; His judgments mercifully account for our desires, performance, and the “degrees of difficulty” imposed by circumstance.

2. The Lord accepts willing hearts when we are hindered  
   - As we do all we can and align our will with His, He judges according to the desire of our hearts and, when we are prevented, He “accepts of [our] offerings.”

3. Christ’s Atonement empowers endurance and enables compensation—often through others and temple work  
   - The Savior succors our pains and infirmities; He frequently sends help through ministering “angels,” and invites us to participate in compensating blessings for others—especially via vicarious temple ordinances.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Nephi 26:33 — “He inviteth all to come unto him.”
- Alma 7:11–12 — Christ bears our pains, sicknesses, and infirmities.
- Omni 1:26 — “Offer your whole souls as an offering unto him.”
- 2 Nephi 25:23 — “By grace… after all we can do.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 124:49 — When hindered, the Lord “accept[s] of their offerings.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 137:7–9 — Judged “according to the desire of their hearts” (Alvin’s vision).
- President Dallin H. Oaks — Teachings on being blessed for righteous desires even when action is prevented.
- Elder Neal A. Maxwell — The Lord mercifully accounts for our desires and the difficulty of our circumstances.
- President Lorenzo Snow — Faithful Saints will not lose eternal blessings for opportunities not available in mortality.
- Gospel principles: Compensating blessings; willingness vs. ability; ministering and “angels” on both sides of the veil; temple and family history work as instruments of divine compensation.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where do I feel righteously motivated yet constrained, and how has the Lord already compensated me in those areas?
- What does “offer your whole soul” look like in my current season of life?
- How can I better balance “after all we can do” with trusting fully in Christ’s grace?
- In what specific ways can I be an “angel” this week—matching my gifts, time, and resources to someone else’s need?
- What temple or family history action will I take to participate in the Lord’s compensating blessings for others?
- How might focusing on righteous desires change my prayers, goals, and sense of peace when outcomes are beyond my control?
