### Summary

The Church Auditing Department reported that, under the direction of the Council on the Disposition of the Tithes (Doctrine and Covenants 120), Church funds in 2024 were received, spent, and safeguarded in all material respects according to approved budgets, policies, and accounting practices, reflecting gospel principles of provident, debt-averse stewardship.

### Haiku

Tithes in trusted hands  
Councils seek the will of God—  
Stewards save to bless.

### Key Points

1. Council-led stewardship: As revealed in Doctrine and Covenants 120, the First Presidency, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, and Presiding Bishopric authorize the expenditure of sacred funds; Church entities disburse funds according to approved budgets, policies, and procedures.
2. Independent oversight: The Church Auditing Department, staffed by credentialed professionals and independent of other Church departments, provides reasonable assurance regarding contributions, expenditures, and the safeguarding of assets.
3. Audit opinion and example: For 2024, the auditors state that, in all material respects, funds were recorded and administered properly; the Church models provident living by budgeting, avoiding debt, and saving for times of need.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 120 — The Council on the Disposition of the Tithes directs how sacred funds are used.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 119 — The law of tithing and consecrated temporal service to the Lord.  
- Malachi 3:10 — Promises to tithe payers and the Lord’s “windows of heaven.”  
- Luke 16:10 — Faithfulness in small things as a measure of true discipleship and trustworthiness.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 104:11–15 — Stewardship, accountability, and consecrated resources.  
- Gospel principles: Provident living, accountability, living within a budget, avoiding unnecessary debt, saving for times of need, and sustaining councils.
