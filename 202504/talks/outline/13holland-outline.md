### Summary

President Jeffrey R. Holland teaches that to truly follow Jesus Christ we must become as little children—humble, pure, trusting, and loving—and he illustrates this through scripture, contrasts with pride, and a moving story of a deacon’s courageous priesthood service, concluding with a witness of the Restoration and of Jesus Christ.

### Haiku

Become as children—  
humble hearts trust, love, and serve;  
Christ lifts broken ones.

### Key Points

1) Become as little children  
- Jesus taught that greatness in His kingdom comes through humility, purity, and submissiveness; scriptures and prophetic counsel contrast these childlike attributes with the vanity and pride that erode true discipleship.

2) Childlike faith in action  
- Easton Jolley’s determined, reverent service in passing the sacrament—despite severe physical challenges—models trust, courage, loyalty, and love, showing how priesthood service and the sacrament draw us to Christ.

3) Centered on Christ and the Restoration  
- President Holland bears witness of Joseph Smith’s First Vision, President Russell M. Nelson’s prophetic calling, the Book of Mormon as a keystone, and the healing strength of priesthood and prayer, urging us to truly follow and worship the Savior.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 18:1–4 — “Except ye… become as little children.”  
- Mosiah 3:19 — Overcoming the natural man by becoming “as a child.”  
- 3 Nephi 17:11–25 — “Behold your little ones”; the Savior’s tenderness toward children.  
- Ecclesiastes 1:2 — “Vanity of vanities”; the emptiness of pride and worldliness.  
- 1 Nephi 8:26–33; 11:35 — The great and spacious building: pride of the world.  
- Alma 31:27–28 — The Zoramites’ proud prayers vs. true worship.  
- John 17:4 — “I have… finished the work thou gavest me to do.”  
- Revelation 1:5; 22:13 — Jesus Christ, the Faithful Witness; Alpha and Omega.  
- Elder Richard L. Evans on taking Christ seriously—moving from admiration to obedient discipleship.  
- Gospel principles: humility, purity, trust, repentance, reverent prayer, sacrament-centered living, sustaining living prophets, the refining power of priesthood service.

### Questions to Ponder

- Which childlike attributes (humility, trust, meekness, love) do I most need to cultivate right now?  
- Where has pride or “vain imaginations” crept into my worship or discipleship, and how will I replace admiration with obedience to Christ?  
- How can I approach the sacrament more reverently so it changes my heart each week?  
- In what ways can I better support children and youth—and those with disabilities—so they can serve and grow in the gospel while honoring their agency?  
- How are priesthood and prayer currently “restoring” my life, and what covenant actions will I take to invite more of that power?  
- What “work” has God given me this week, and how will I, like Easton, finish it with faith and love?  
- How can our ward more fully “behold [our] little ones” and learn from their example of pure devotion to Christ?
