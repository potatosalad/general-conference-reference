### Summary

Sister Amy A. Wright teaches that lifelong discipleship begins in the home and Primary as we help children believe in Jesus Christ, belong to Him and His Church through covenants, and become like Him through conversion and action.

### Haiku

“Thou art the Christ,”  
children see, feel, know, and do—  
hearts change, light fills home.

### Key Points

1) Believe in Jesus Christ  
- Faith centered in the living Christ empowers and saves.  
- Help children “see, feel, hear, and know” Him through testimony, scripture, reverent images, and the witness of the Holy Ghost.  
- The Sermon on the Mount reveals His character and the pattern for discipleship.

2) Belong to Jesus Christ and His Church  
- Covenants give us a new name and identity as children of Christ and bind us to His work.  
- Parents, leaders, and teachers prepare children for a lifetime on the covenant path; use Come, Follow Me (appendices A and B) as tools for simple, powerful conversations and lessons.

3) Become like Jesus Christ  
- Disciples are “doers of the word”: invite children to teach, testify, pray, and serve—then walk beside them as they act.  
- True conversion changes desires, priorities, and conduct; avoid casual discipleship by centering every home and meeting on Jesus Christ.  
- Testify of the real, glorified, resurrected, exalted Savior who is mighty to save.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 16:16 — Peter’s testimony: “Thou art the Christ.”  
- John 6:66–69 — “To whom shall we go? Thou hast the words of eternal life.”  
- 3 Nephi 11:10–17 — One by one, the people see, feel, and know the risen Lord.  
- Mosiah 5:2, 7 — Mighty change of heart; “children of Christ” through covenant.  
- 3 Nephi 27:27 — “What manner of men ought ye to be? Even as I am.”  
- James 1:22 — Be doers of the word, not hearers only.  
- Matthew 5–7 — Sermon on the Mount: Christlike attributes and higher law.  
- Gospel principles: Covenant belonging; the Light of Christ; gathering Israel; centrality of Christ in all teaching; President Russell M. Nelson’s teachings on faith as a conduit of divine power.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can we help children “see, feel, hear, and know” Jesus Christ in our homes and classes this week?  
- What specific covenants help children belong to Christ, and how can we teach their meaning before and after baptism?  
- How will we use Come, Follow Me appendices A and B to create simple, consistent, Christ-centered conversations?  
- In what ways can we invite children to teach, testify, pray, and serve—and how will we walk beside them as they do?  
- Where might our discipleship (or our meetings) have become casual, and how can we refocus everything on the Savior?  
- Which teaching from the Sermon on the Mount will our family live more fully this week?  
- How has conversion changed your desires, media, time, and priorities—and what is your next step to become more like Him?  
- How can we bear more explicit testimony of Jesus’s premortal divinity, Resurrection, and living reality to our children?
