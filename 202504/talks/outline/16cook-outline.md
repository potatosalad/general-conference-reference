### Summary

Elder Quentin L. Cook testifies that the Atonement of Jesus Christ is the ultimate rescue from sin, death, and life’s storms, inviting us to be rescuers, live joyfully in gratitude, and regularly remember the Savior through sacrament and temple worship.

### Haiku

Storms rise, temples shine—  
Hands lift hearts, Christ lifts all souls;  
Grace bridges the cold.

### Key Points

1) The Atonement is the ultimate rescue
- In a beautiful but fallen world, only Jesus Christ overcomes sin, death, and the unfairness of life; turning to Him brings peace, hope, and strength.

2) Pioneer rescue as a living parable
- The Willie and Martin handcart rescues model prophetic direction, courageous service, and Heaven’s help—reminding us to both receive and offer rescue.

3) Three invitations for our day
- a) Do all you can to rescue others, especially spiritually.  
- b) Gratefully accept Christ’s Atonement and choose joy—“live on the sunny side of the street.”  
- c) Consistently remember Christ through the sacrament and temple worship, including vicarious work that extends His rescue to our ancestors.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Mosiah 15:8–9 — Christ breaks the bands of death and makes intercession.  
- Alma 7:11–13 — The Savior succors us in afflictions, pains, and infirmities.  
- John 16:33 — “Be of good cheer; I have overcome the world.”  
- 1 Corinthians 15:20–22 — Resurrection and victory over death in Christ.  
- Alma 34:15–16; Alma 42:15 — Mercy satisfies the demands of justice through the Atonement.  
- Preach My Gospel: “All that is unfair about life can be made right through the Atonement of Jesus Christ.”  
- President James E. Faust, “The Refiner’s Fire” — Trials refine and anchor faith in God.  
- President Gordon B. Hinckley on the Atonement: the most majestic act of grace in history.  
- President Russell M. Nelson: Temple blessings prepare a people who prepare the world for the Lord’s coming.  
- Gospel principles: covenant belonging, sacrament-centered discipleship, temple covenant power, proxy service as spiritual rescue.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I felt the Savior “rescue” me—spiritually, emotionally, or temporally?  
- Who in my family, quorum/class, or neighborhood needs quiet, consistent spiritual rescue, and what will I do this week?  
- How does weekly sacrament worship help me personally “accept” the Atonement rather than just admire it?  
- What keeps me from living on the “sunny side of the street,” and how can faith in Christ reframe my outlook?  
- How does temple worship deepen my understanding of the Atonement and extend rescue to my ancestors?  
- What pioneers or rescuers (historical or modern) inspire me to follow prophetic counsel promptly?  
- In what specific trial right now can I look for the Lord’s hand “between storms”?
