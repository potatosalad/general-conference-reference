### Summary

President Lund teaches that the Aaronic Priesthood is divinely restored authority that prepares young men to bless the Church and the world through service, ordinances, and covenant-powered ministry—most centrally in the sacrament.

### Haiku

Bright line of priesthood  
Young hands bless the bread; hearts heal—  
Angels walk with them

### Key Points

1. Divine origin and worldwide impact of the Aaronic Priesthood  
   - John the Baptist restored the Aaronic Priesthood with keys of the ministering of angels, repentance, and baptism. Each ordination connects young men to a living line of authority that disrupts worldly patterns with godly power and launches them into lifelong ministry.

2. The preparatory priesthood forms disciples through real service and teaching  
   - Deacons, teachers, and priests learn to warn, expound, exhort, teach, and invite all to come unto Christ, and priests to preach and baptize. The story of “Alan and his boys” shows how consistent, loving ministering and priesthood service naturally lead to softened hearts, conversion, and temple ordinances.

3. The sacrament centers covenant power in times of need  
   - Aaronic Priesthood holders administer the sacrament—words of ordinance and covenant that promise the constant companionship of the Spirit. A bishop-father, amid profound grief, pronounced the prayers, and the promise was fulfilled: the Spirit brought comfort to him, his family, and the ward.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 13:1 — Restoration of the Aaronic Priesthood; keys of ministering of angels, repentance, and baptism.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:50, 59 — Duties of priests, teachers, and deacons to preach, warn, expound, exhort, teach, and invite.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79 — Sacrament prayers; covenant to take His name, remember Him, keep His commandments, and receive His Spirit.
- Numbers 3:10, 31; Deuteronomy 33:10 — Ancient responsibilities to handle sacred things and teach.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:41 — Priesthood influence through persuasion, long-suffering, gentleness, and love unfeigned.
- Gospel principles: Preparatory priesthood; priesthood keys vs. authority; ministering of angels; covenant power in the sacrament; mentoring youth in real, needed service; gathering Israel through priesthood service.

### Questions to Ponder

- How does recognizing the divine origin of the Aaronic Priesthood change the way I view ordinations and weekly sacrament service?
- In what specific, natural ways can deacons, teachers, and priests “warn, expound, exhort, teach, and invite” in our ward and community?
- How can leaders and families better mentor young men so their service is real, needed, and shaped by “love unfeigned”?
- What does it mean for me to “take upon [me] the name of Christ” each week, and how can I better “always remember Him”?
- When have I felt the promise of “His Spirit to be with [me]” fulfilled through the sacrament—especially in times of grief or crisis?
- How might the keys of the Aaronic Priesthood (ministering of angels, repentance, baptism) be more visible in our ward’s ministering and missionary efforts?
- Where is the Lord placing me—or the youth I influence—in “consequential times and places,” and how can I prepare now for those moments?
