### Summary

President Camille N. Johnson teaches that while full physical and emotional healing may come in the Lord’s time, we can be spiritually whole now through faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, covenants, and letting His light shine through us.

### Haiku

Oil in waiting lamps—  
faith turns weakness into light;  
whole before healed.

### Key Points

1) Wholeness vs. healing  
   - The Savior healed ten lepers, but only one was made whole; likewise, through faith and repentance like Enos, we can be spiritually whole in Christ even as we wait for physical and emotional healing.

2) The oil of conversion and covenant belonging  
   - The wise virgins were ready because their lamps were filled with the oil of conversion—made and kept through covenants, temple worship, obedience, and daily discipleship; we cannot borrow oil, but we can share our light.

3) Strength in Christ amid affliction; joyful witness  
   - Like Paul, the Lord’s grace can make us strong in weakness; like Mary Magdalene, disciples who know the risen Christ rejoice and testify while trusting that complete healing will come in the Resurrection.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Luke 17:11–19 — Ten lepers; gratitude, faith, and being “made whole.”
- Enos 1:1–8 — Forgiveness and wholeness “because of thy faith in Christ.”
- Matthew 25:1–13 — Ten virgins; oil of conversion; “I know you not” (v. 12).
- Doctrine and Covenants 33:17 — “Be faithful… having your lamps trimmed and burning.”
- Matthew 5:14–16; 3 Nephi 12:14–16 — Let your light shine.
- 2 Corinthians 12:7–10 — “My grace is sufficient… strength made perfect in weakness.”
- Philippians 4:4 — “Rejoice in the Lord alway[s].”
- Isaiah 25:8 — “He will swallow up death… wipe away tears.”
- John 20:11–16 — Mary Magdalene’s witness of the risen Lord.
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:34–36 — “Many are called, but few are chosen.”
- Alma 11:43–44; 1 Corinthians 15:20–22 — Restoration and universal resurrection.
- Gospel principles: Conversion as covenant loyalty; faith-hope cycle; agency and submission of the heart; joyful endurance; sharing light while retaining personal oil; grace in weakness; temple worship deepens wholeness.

### Questions to Ponder

- In what specific ways can I choose to be spiritually whole in Christ while I wait for healing?  
- What does “oil of conversion” look like in my daily discipleship this week?  
- How do covenants (baptism, sacrament, temple) help me be known by the Bridegroom?  
- Where am I trying to “borrow oil” instead of building my own conversion?  
- How can I share my light without depleting my oil—supporting others while nurturing my own discipleship?  
- What “thorn in the flesh” invites me to rely more fully on the Savior’s grace?  
- How can gratitude (like the returning leper) deepen my wholeness in Christ today?  
- What practices (prayer, scripture, temple, service, repentance) most help me submit my heart to the Lord for change?  
- When have I felt “strength made perfect in weakness,” and what did I learn about Jesus Christ?  
- How can Mary Magdalene’s witness shape my own joyful testimony of the risen Lord this week?
