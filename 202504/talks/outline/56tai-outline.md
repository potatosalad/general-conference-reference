### Summary

Elder Benjamin M. Z. Tai teaches that Jesus Christ is the embodiment of Heavenly Father’s perfect, personal, and perpetual love, which we can consistently feel as we exercise faith, repent and forgive, make and keep temple covenants, and serve others by following the Spirit.

### Haiku

Stars veiled by city—  
Faith clears the sky; Christ’s love shines,  
Calm above the storm.

### Key Points

1) Christ is the love of God  
   - The Savior’s Atonement reveals the Father’s love; He bears our sins, sorrows, pains, and sicknesses and sends the Holy Ghost, whose fruits include joy, peace, and faith. Lehi’s tree of life symbolizes this love, accessible through faith that clears “spiritual pollution.”

2) How to feel God’s love consistently  
   - Through the grace of Christ, we repeatedly repent, frankly forgive, keep commandments, and serve; we pray and study daily and avoid pride, contention, and rebellion. Temple covenants “bolster our spiritual foundations” and increase access to the Lord’s strengthening power and pure love; the temple can heal hearts and remove burdens.

3) Share His love—be a lighthouse  
   - Cultivate humility, charity, compassion, and patience; live the two great commandments. Follow a simple pattern: pause to remember you’re a child of God and be grateful, pray to know who needs His love, ask what to do, then act promptly. In life’s storms, come to Christ with courage; He will lift and save us, enabling us to guide others to Him.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Hebrews 11:1 — Faith is the “evidence of things not seen” that clears spiritual vision.  
- 1 Nephi 8; 11:21–23 — The tree and its fruit: “the love of God,” most sweet, desirable above all.  
- Isaiah 53:4–5; Alma 7:11–12 — Christ bears griefs, sorrows, pains, and sicknesses.  
- Galatians 5:22–23 — Fruits of the Spirit: love, joy, peace, faith.  
- Mosiah 24:13–15 — The Lord remembers covenants, lightens burdens, and helps us submit cheerfully.  
- Matthew 14:24–33 — “Be of good cheer… be not afraid”; Peter walks to Christ, who immediately saves.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:36 — “Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson’s invitations: remove spiritual debris, lay aside bitterness, and center life on the Savior and temple covenants to access His strengthening power and love.

### Questions to Ponder

- What “air and light pollution” (distractions, sins, or discouragements) cloud my view of God’s love, and what will I remove with the Savior’s help?  
- When have I “tasted” the fruit of the love of God? What invited that experience?  
- How will I use repentance and forgiveness this week to feel the Lord’s love more fully?  
- In what ways have temple covenants and worship increased my access to the Savior’s power and love?  
- Who around me needs to feel God’s love today, and what specific action will I take to help them feel it?  
- In my current storm, how can I be of good cheer, come to Christ, and let Him lift me “immediately”?  
- Which of the four invitations (pause, pray, ask, act) do I most need to practice more consistently?  
- How can I be a lighthouse of Christlike love in my home, quorum/class, and community?
