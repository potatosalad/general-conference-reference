### Summary

Elder Rasband teaches that the Lord manifests Himself in mercy—especially in His temples—and that the Father’s plan of salvation is a plan of mercy, accessed through covenants, humble pleas for forgiveness, and pondering God’s mercy as taught in the Book of Mormon.

### Haiku

Mercy fills His house—  
He softens hearts in temples;  
Spirit answers pleas.

### Key Points

1) Temples empower disciples and soften hearts
- The Kirtland Temple dedicatory prayer (D&C 109) promises that as Saints go forth, hearts will be softened and prejudice will yield to truth; time in the temple centers us on Jesus Christ and His mercy, blessing missionaries and all members.

2) The plan of salvation is a plan of mercy
- The Savior declared He will manifest Himself “in mercy” in His house (D&C 110); temple covenants, sealing power, and the Lord’s loyal, covenantal love (hesed) reveal that every element of the plan—Creation, Fall, Atonement, and our return to God—is merciful.

3) Pleading for mercy invites revelation and testimony
- From Joseph Smith to Enos and Lamoni’s father, seeking forgiveness opens the door to the Holy Ghost; Moroni teaches that remembering and pondering God’s mercy prepares us to receive a witness of the Book of Mormon.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 109: Kirtland Temple dedicatory prayer—softened hearts, favor before the world, sent by the Lord.
- Doctrine and Covenants 110: The Lord accepts the house and promises to manifest Himself “in mercy” in His temples.
- Exodus 25:17–22: The “mercy seat”—ancient sanctuary symbolism fulfilled in Christ.
- Moroni 10:3–5: Remember and ponder the Lord’s mercy to prepare for a spiritual witness.
- Alma 34:16: Mercy satisfies the demands of justice and encircles us in the arms of safety.
- Alma 7:11–12: Christ’s suffering enables Him to succor us in pains and afflictions.
- Alma 11:43; 40:23: Resurrection restores us to a perfect frame.
- Mosiah 3:11: The Atonement covers those who sinned in ignorance.
- 2 Nephi 2:25: “Men are, that they might have joy.”
- Mosiah 14:5 / Isaiah 53:5: “With his stripes we are healed.”
- Gospel principles: Temple covenants and sealing power; hesed (divine, covenantal mercy); repentance and forgiveness invite revelation; missionary preparation through temple worship and service.
- President Russell M. Nelson’s invitation to study D&C 109 and his teaching that the dedicatory promises apply to every dedicated temple.

### Questions to Ponder

- How has time in the temple softened my own heart—and how might that invite softened hearts in those I serve or teach?
- What specific mercy or forgiveness do I need to seek from the Lord this week to better feel the Holy Ghost?
- How does seeing the plan of salvation as a “plan of mercy” change my view of justice, repentance, and daily discipleship?
- When I apply Moroni 10:3, what acts of remembering and pondering the Lord’s mercy can I practice before I pray?
- Which Book of Mormon teaching about mercy (e.g., succor in afflictions, resurrection, arms of safety) speaks to my current situation, and why?
- How can temple attendance, service, and family history work help me extend God’s mercy to others and qualify for more of it in my life?
