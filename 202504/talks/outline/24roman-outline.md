### Summary

Elder Sandino Roman teaches that faith in Jesus Christ begins as trust, matures into loyal covenant-keeping, and empowers us to face adversity while letting God’s will and miracles prevail.

### Haiku

Trust turns into love,  
Covenant hearts stay loyal—  
Fear not, walk with Christ.

### Key Points

1) Faith as Trust in Christ: Faith is more than belief; it is a relationship of trust built by recognizing the Lord’s blessings, recording spiritually defining memories, and praying sincerely until the Spirit writes truth upon the heart.

2) Faith Flourishes with Loyalty: Faith “blooms” through covenant loyalty—making and honoring covenants shapes choices, deepens love for the Savior, and invites Him to manifest Himself as we keep His commandments.

3) Faith in Adversity and God’s Promises: We “walk undaunted” with lamps filled by faithful habits, expect miracles while letting God prevail, and receive the Lord’s promised strength, forgiveness, identity as His covenant children, and power to do His will.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 6:36 — “Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not.” (FSY 2025 theme)
- Hebrews 11:6 — “Without faith it is impossible to please God.”
- John 14:21 — The Savior manifests Himself to those who love Him and keep His commandments.
- Genesis 39:9 — Joseph’s loyalty to God in resisting temptation.
- Joseph Smith—History 1:25 — “I knew it … and I could not deny it … I would offend God.”
- 2 Corinthians 3:3 — Truth written on the “fleshy tables” of the heart.
- 2 Nephi 4:21 — “He hath filled me with his love…”
- Alma 5:26; 2 Nephi 1:15 — “Song of redeeming love”; “encircled … in the arms of his love.”
- Matthew 25:1–13 — Oil in our lamps: faithful, daily discipleship.
- “Faith is a principle of action and of power” — Gospel principle (see Bible Dictionary; Preach My Gospel).
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Drawing the Power of Jesus Christ into Our Lives” — Reach for the Lord’s power with the urgency of one gasping for air.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Let God Prevail” (Oct 2020) and “The Power of Spiritual Momentum” (Apr 2022) — Let God’s will lead; expect miracles.
- Elder Neil L. Andersen, “Spiritually Defining Memories” (Oct 2020) — Remember and record God’s interventions.
- Covenant love (hesed) and loyal faith (emunah) — Our loyal love in response to God’s merciful love.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I felt the Savior earn my trust through His love and help? How can I remember and record those spiritually defining moments?
- What daily habits will help the Spirit write truth on the “fleshy tables” of my heart so my prayers, scriptures, and temple worship feel personal and alive?
- Which covenant have I made that I most want to honor more fully this week? What will loyalty to that covenant look like in my choices?
- In what current challenge can I “walk undaunted” toward Christ? What does letting God prevail (rather than my preferred outcome) look like here?
- What specific temptations or pressures will I resist because I love the Lord? What would I not do because it would “offend God”?
- How can I keep oil in my lamp—what simple, steady practices refuel my faith when the path grows dark?
- How have I recently seen the Lord “manifest” Himself to me as I have kept His commandments?
- If I asked Heavenly Father today how He feels about me and my life’s direction, what answer would I hope to hear—and what change would I make?
