### Summary

Elder Scott D. Whiting teaches that after we fall to “first temptations,” the adversary’s “second temptation” is to hide from God and His people—yet we cannot hide from the Lord, and healing comes as we run to Christ, repent, and lean on a loving covenant community.

### Haiku

Shadows bid us hide;  
Grace calls us into the light—  
Run to healing hands.

### Key Points

1) Beware the “second temptation” to hide  
- After mistakes, Satan urges isolation and shame. Scripture affirms we cannot hide from God; instead, come to Him quickly with honesty and faith.

2) Run to Christ and His covenant community  
- Seek help from Jesus Christ through repentance, prayer, and priesthood leaders; do not withdraw from Church support. Create a culture of belonging, especially for new converts, and reject the accuser’s lies.

3) Mortal weakness is part of the plan; healing is in Christ  
- Earth life includes opposition and “first temptations,” but growth comes as we repent repeatedly, keep covenants, and access the Atonement of Jesus Christ over a lifetime.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Genesis 3; Moses 4: Adam and Eve hide—Satan’s “second temptation.”
- Jeremiah 23:24; Job 34:21–22; Psalm 139:1–8: We cannot hide from God’s presence and knowledge.
- Revelation 12:10: Satan as “the accuser.”
- 2 Nephi 2:11, 25: Opposition and joyful progression in God’s plan.
- Mosiah 3:19: The natural man and yielding to the Spirit.
- Alma 7:11–12: Christ takes upon Him our pains, sins, and infirmities; He can succor us.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Overcome the World and Find Rest” (Oct. 2022): Overcoming the world is a lifetime work through daily repentance and covenant power.
- Gospel principles: Repentance and confession, priesthood keys and pastoral care (bishops/branch presidents), ministering and belonging, covenant path endurance, resisting shame with truth.

### Questions to Ponder

- When I make a mistake, what does “hiding” look like for me, and what does “running to the Lord” look like practically this week?
- Whom has the Lord already placed in my life (family, friends, leaders) that I can turn to instead of isolating?
- What truths from scripture counter the accuser’s lies of “You don’t belong” or “You can’t change”?
- How can I make my ward or branch a place where new and returning members feel safe, seen, and needed?
- What is one act of honest confession or repentance I can take now to invite Christ’s healing?
- How do covenants empower me to overcome the world gradually, not instantly?
- In what ways have past attempts to hide led to greater pain, and how has turning to Christ brought relief?
- Who needs me to reach out this week so they won’t face their struggles alone?
