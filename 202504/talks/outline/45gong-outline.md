### Summary

Elder Gerrit W. Gong testifies that through Jesus Christ’s Atonement, Resurrection, and Restoration, God meets us in our tears—healing and transforming us with peace, helping us become whole, and gathering us to belong in covenant families and His restored Church.

### Haiku

Jesus weeps with us—  
night’s tears turn to morning joy;  
keys gather the light.

### Key Points

1) Jesus weeps and succors us through His Atonement  
   - The Savior intimately understands sorrow and joy; His Atonement heals grief, enables forgiveness, mends relationships (on both sides of the veil), and frees us from shame and perfectionism.

2) Resurrection brings joy, wholeness, and hope beyond death  
   - Because Christ rose, all will rise; He wipes away our tears, restores what was lost—and more—making us whole, physically and spiritually.

3) The Restoration continues Christ’s work to gather and bless all God’s family  
   - On Easter 1836 at Kirtland, priesthood keys were restored (gathering Israel, sealing power), uniting families for eternity; we rejoice each Easter and April 6 in the converging gifts of Atonement, Resurrection, and Restoration, extending God’s love to all His children across cultures and faiths.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 11:35; John 20:15; Psalm 30:5 — Jesus weeps; “joy cometh in the morning.”
- 3 Nephi 17:21–22 — The Savior’s joyful weeping and blessing of little children.
- Revelation 21:4; Mosiah 18:9 — He wipes away tears; “mourn with those that mourn.”
- Luke 22:44; Doctrine and Covenants 19:18 — Gethsemane: He bled from every pore.
- Luke 4:18; Isaiah 61:1–3, 10 — Heal the brokenhearted; beauty for ashes; garments of salvation.
- Romans 8:28; Genesis 45 — God’s purposes work for good; Joseph’s tears and God’s plan.
- Matthew 23:37; Jacob 5:41 — The Hen gathers; the Lord of the vineyard weeps, “What more could I have done?”
- Doctrine and Covenants 76:43–44 — “Saves all the works of His hands,” except those who willfully deny the Son after the Father reveals Him.
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:6 — He descended below and ascended above all things.
- Doctrine and Covenants 110:11–16 — At Kirtland, Moses, Elias, and Elijah restore priesthood keys.
- Hosea 11:1; Matthew 2:15 — “Out of Egypt have I called my son.”
- John 3:16 — God so loved the world.
- Hymn: “He Is Risen!” (“Death is conquered; man is free. Christ has won the victory.”)
- Gospel principles: Atonement, Resurrection, Restoration; covenant belonging; temple sealing power; ministering; repentance and reconciliation; gathering Israel; honoring truth and goodness across faith traditions.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I felt the Savior “weep” with me, and how did His Atonement change my sorrow into hope?
- Is there a relationship—on either side of the veil—that the Lord is inviting me to heal or reconcile?
- How does the promise of resurrection shape the way I face loss, illness, aging, or imperfection?
- What does it mean for me to wear the “garments of salvation” and the “robe of righteousness” in daily discipleship?
- How do priesthood keys and temple covenants bring belonging to my family and ward—who can I help gather?
- Where have I seen God give “beauty for ashes” in my life? What ashes can I place on the altar now?
- How can I better “mourn with those that mourn” and “comfort those that stand in need of comfort” this week?
- In what ways can I recognize and honor God’s love at work among people of other faiths while bearing witness of Christ?
- How can I let Easter and April 6 (Restoration) converge in my worship—remembering, rejoicing, and recommitting to Jesus Christ?
