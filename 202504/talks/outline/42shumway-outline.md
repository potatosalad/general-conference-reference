### Summary

Elder Steven D. Shumway teaches that saying yes to callings and other forms of service is God’s way of preparing us—and those we love—to meet the Savior at His return through steady progress, covenant living, and enabling grace.

### Haiku

Say yes to His work—  
Grace lifts the willing and weak;  
Prepare to meet Him.

### Key Points

1) Participation is preparation  
- We engage in God’s work not because He needs us but because we need the growth, blessings, and readiness that come from laboring with Him; like the Creation’s “it was good,” God delights in our honest progress, not perfection, and callings do not define worth.

2) Service is covenant living  
- Serving turns homes and wards into holy places to practice remembering Christ and bearing one another’s burdens; preparation accelerates when we stop asking what God will permit and start asking what He would prefer.

3) Grace magnifies willing disciples  
- As we act in faith beyond our abilities, God’s grace supplies power, turns weakness into strength, and makes our meager efforts enough; leaders should invite the “unlikely” to serve, let them learn and act, and help all represent Christ more than themselves.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 34:32 — This life is the time to prepare to meet God.  
- Genesis 1 — The Creation pattern: God recognizes progress (“it was good”); progress over perfection.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 76:5–6 — The Lord delights to honor those who serve Him; great shall be their reward.  
- Galatians 6:2 — Bear one another’s burdens and so fulfill the law of Christ.  
- Jacob 4:7 — By His grace we have power to do these things; weakness reveals our need for Christ.  
- Ether 12:27 — The Lord makes weak things become strong through humility and grace.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 107:99–100 — Let every man and woman learn their duty and act in all diligence.  
- Moses 1:39 — God’s work and glory invites us to labor with Him to bring souls to Christ.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “The Future of the Church: Preparing the World for the Savior’s Second Coming” (April 2020).  
- Elder David A. Bednar’s teachings on learning duty and acting (D&C 107:99–100).

### Questions to Ponder

- What calling or quiet invitation to serve have I hesitated to accept, and how would saying yes to Christ change me?  
- Where do I see “it is good” progress in my discipleship, even if the work isn’t finished?  
- How can I turn routine responsibilities into covenant-keeping worship that remembers Christ?  
- Who in my ward or family currently lacks a calling or feels “unlikely,” and how can I help them engage and grow?  
- In what specific ways is the Lord tutoring me through weakness to rely more fully on His grace?  
- This week, what would God prefer I do—not merely permit—so my service better represents the Savior?  
- How can I serve so that others leave remembering Christ more than they remember me?
