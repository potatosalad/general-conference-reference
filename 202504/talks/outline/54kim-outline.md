### Summary

Elder Christopher H. Kim teaches that hardness of heart blinds us to the Spirit, but through daily repentance, humility, and trusting in Jesus Christ, the Lord will soften our hearts, deepen our understanding, and bless us with His Spirit.

### Haiku

Stone hearts resist light;  
Repent, be meek—trust the Lord—  
Spirit makes flesh new.

### Key Points

1. What hardness of heart is and why it matters: Stubbornness and spiritual blindness lead us to resist the Holy Ghost, forget the Lord, and lose light—illustrated by Laman and Lemuel—while Nephi’s humility invited a softened heart and revelation.
2. How to soften our hearts: Practice daily repentance, cultivate humility and meekness, and rely on the Savior; as we yield our own wisdom and will, the Spirit increases our faith and understanding (as in the story of the sister moved by Moroni 7:43–44).
3. Promised blessings: Trusting Christ brings the companionship of the Holy Ghost, strength in trials, and revealed truths; the Redeemer gathers and protects those who refuse to harden their hearts.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 1 Nephi 15:4; 1 Nephi 7:8; 1 Nephi 17:45 — Hardness of heart, blindness of mind, and being “past feeling.”
- 1 Nephi 2:16 — The Lord softens humble hearts (Nephi’s example).
- Alma 12:10–11 — Lesser portion of the word when hearts are hardened; the Spirit withdraws.
- 3 Nephi 9:22; Mosiah 3:19 — Come as a little child; put off the natural man through meekness.
- Moroni 7:43–44 — Meekness and lowliness of heart enable real faith and hope.
- 2 Nephi 4:34 — Trust in God, not the arm of flesh.
- Doctrine and Covenants 11:12 — Trust the Spirit that leads to do good and walk humbly.
- Alma 36:3 — Trusting God supports us in trials, troubles, and afflictions.
- 2 Nephi 31:7 — Christ’s perfect humility at His baptism.
- Doctrine and Covenants 19:18–19 — The Savior’s submissive suffering in Gethsemane.
- 3 Nephi 10:6 — The Lord gathers those who will not harden their hearts.
- Gospel principles: Daily repentance, humility/meekness, reliance on Jesus Christ, the companionship of the Holy Ghost, agency to choose a softened heart, and the Book of Mormon’s role in the Restoration and in softening hearts.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where might subtle stubbornness or self-reliance be hardening my heart against the whisperings of the Spirit?
- What does “daily repentance” look like for me this week, and how will I know my heart is softening?
- In what decisions am I trusting “the arm of flesh” rather than the Lord? What would trusting Him change today?
- How does meekness increase faith and hope in my life and relationships?
- When have I felt the Spirit more fully after choosing humility over being right or being first?
- What specific practices (prayer, scripture study, service, temple worship) most reliably invite a softened heart for me?
- How can I help soften hearts in my home or ward without coercion—through example, patience, and Christlike love?
