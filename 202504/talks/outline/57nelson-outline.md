### Summary

President Russell M. Nelson teaches that as we fill our lives with charity and virtue—strengthened by temple worship and covenant keeping—our confidence to approach God now and in the world to come will “wax strong.”

### Haiku

Charity, virtue—  
steady hearts before God’s throne;  
temples teach us peace.

### Key Points

1) Charity and peacemaking invite the Spirit: True disciples reject anger, hostility, and contention; charity toward “all men” softens our speech, ends judging, and makes us instruments of the Prince of Peace.

2) Virtue purifies thought and increases spiritual confidence: Garnishing thoughts “unceasingly” with virtue displaces impure, cruel, and anxious thinking and strengthens our ability to pray and feel the Holy Ghost.

3) Covenants and temple worship prepare us for the Savior’s return: Making and keeping covenants in the temple expands our capacity for charity and virtue, builds confidence to approach God, and helps us intentionally prepare for the Second Coming.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 121:45–46 — Charity and virtue lead to confidence that “wax[es] strong in the presence of God.”
- Moroni 7:47–48 — Charity is the pure love of Christ; pray to be filled with it.
- 3 Nephi 11:29–30 — Contention is not of Christ; peacemaking invites His Spirit.
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:88 — The Lord sends angels to bear us up.
- Isaiah 9:6 — Jesus Christ is the Prince of Peace.
- Matthew 24:36; Malachi 4:5 — Prepare for the “great and dreadful day” of the Lord; we do not know the day or hour.
- Gospel principles: Covenant keeping; confidence before God in prayer; constant companionship of the Holy Ghost; temple worship; discipleship in public and private discourse.

- Related message: President Russell M. Nelson, “Peacemakers Needed” (April 2023).

### Questions to Ponder

- How would my prayers change if I believed—right now—that God hears me, knows my needs, and sends angels to help?
- Where can I replace contention with peacemaking in my home, ward, workplace, or online interactions?
- What thoughts most need “virtue” added to them, and what practical steps will help me garnish my thoughts unceasingly?
- What covenant I’ve made could I honor more intentionally this week?
- How does regular temple worship increase my capacity to love and to think virtuously?
- Whom is the Lord inviting me to stop judging and start serving with Christlike charity?
- What media, habits, or patterns fuel anxiety or impure thoughts—and what will I remove or replace?
- In what specific ways am I preparing for the Second Coming, personally and as a family?
