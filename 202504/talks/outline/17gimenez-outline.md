### Summary

Elder Ricardo P. Giménez teaches that our deepest motivation for discipleship—our “why”—should be love for Jesus Christ, which transforms traditions, goals, and prophetic invitations into covenant-centered pathways that bring us to Him.

### Haiku

Love is my “why” now—  
Traditions turn into light  
When they lead to Him.

### Key Points

1) Love for the Savior is the true “why”
- Discipleship becomes transformative when our actions—prayer, scripture study, service, and Church participation—are motivated by love for Jesus Christ, not just habit or checklists. This love deepens our covenantal connection with God and helps us “get it right,” not merely “do it right.”

2) Prophetic invitations are means to come unto Christ
- Repeated invitations from President Nelson to study conference messages and from President Oaks to establish Christ-centered Easter traditions are not tasks to complete but tools to learn of, feel, and follow the Savior more fully.

3) Covenants in the restored Church are essential
- A personal relationship with Christ is vital, but the restored Church provides priesthood ordinances and covenants that bind us to Him and grant access to His covenantal love, enabling us to progress toward the highest glory despite voices that dismiss the need for organized religion.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 3:16 — God’s love manifest in the gift of His Son; the heart of our “why.”
- Matthew 11:28–30 — “Come unto me” promises rest through covenant connection with Christ.
- Joseph Smith—History 1:16–20 — The First Vision and the Restoration affirm the necessity of the Lord’s Church and priesthood.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20; 84 — The Church and priesthood ordinances as God’s appointed means for salvation.
- President Russell M. Nelson, April and October 2024 — Invitations to study and use conference messages as a “litmus test” for truth.
- President Dallin H. Oaks — Invitation to establish Christ-centered Easter traditions that focus on the Savior’s Atonement and Resurrection.
- Gospel principles: Covenant path, priesthood ordinances, discipleship motivated by charity (the pure love of Christ), revelatory living through heeding living prophets.

### Questions to Ponder

- What is my personal “why” for the way I worship, serve, and keep commandments? How does love for the Savior shape it?
- Where have traditions or routines become checklist items for me, and how can I re-anchor them in Christ?
- How will I study recent conference messages so they lead me to the Savior rather than just adding to my to-do list?
- Which prophetic invitation am I being prompted to accept right now, and what Christ-centered purpose will guide my action?
- How can I redesign a family or ward tradition to better teach, remember, and rejoice in the Savior’s Atonement and Resurrection?
- In what ways do covenants and ordinances help me experience the Lord’s covenantal love more fully?
- How can I discern and resist voices that downplay the need for the restored Church while deepening my personal relationship with Christ?
- In my calling, how will I move from “doing it right” to “getting it right” by focusing outcomes on coming unto Christ?
