### Summary

Elder Sergio R. Vargas teaches that Jesus Christ is our divine “guidance system”—through the Light of Christ, covenants, and steady spiritual nourishment we can resist the hooks of mortality, endure with joy, and return home to our Heavenly Parents.

### Haiku

Light of Christ guides home;  
Hooks glitter—we fix on Him.  
Upstream, we find joy.

### Key Points

1) Remember your divine identity and destiny  
- We are beloved children of Heavenly Parents with a divine nature; focusing on Christ and our true identity helps us reject Satan’s counterfeit lures and offenses.

2) Nourish your spirit daily  
- Prayer, scripture study, Sabbath worship (especially the sacrament), and temple worship feed the soul and keep us safely on the covenant path amid life’s dangers.

3) Follow Christ home through covenants and endurance with joy  
- The Savior’s Atonement makes forgiveness, strength, and hope possible; as we make and keep covenants and ask for His help, He shares our burdens, helps us overcome the world, and fills our journey with joy independent of circumstance.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 30:44 — “All things denote there is a God”; creation as a witness of Christ’s guiding light.  
- Moroni 7:16–19 — The Spirit (Light) of Christ enables us to discern good from evil.  
- John 14:6 — Jesus Christ is “the way, the truth, and the life.”  
- Moses 1:12–13 — Moses resists Satan by remembering, “I am a son of God.”  
- Isaiah 53:3; John 16:33 — Christ, acquainted with grief, has overcome the world.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 58:42 — Through repentance, the Lord remembers our sins no more.  
- Mark 2:27 — “The sabbath was made for man”; sacrament-centered Sabbath renewal.  
- 2 Nephi 31:20 — Press forward in Christ, feasting on His word, enduring to the end.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 14:7 — Eternal life is “the greatest of all the gifts of God.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:7–8 — “Thine afflictions shall be but a small moment.”  
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World — Our premortal identity, divine nature, and destiny.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Joy and Spiritual Survival” (Oct. 2016) — Joy depends on our focus on Christ.  
- President Jeffrey R. Holland, “Like a Broken Vessel” (Oct. 2013) — Hope in a future day of wholeness.  
- Gospel principles: Divine identity and the covenant path; Light of Christ; repentance and forgiveness; sacrament and Sabbath worship; temple covenants; enduring to the end with joy; ministering and rescue.

### Questions to Ponder

- When have I most clearly felt the Light of Christ “map” me back toward God, and how can I heed that guidance more consistently?  
- Which “hooks” (offense, distraction, self-pity, counterfeit pleasures) most tempt me, and what Christ-centered practices will help me avoid them?  
- How can I better remember my divine identity—especially in moments of temptation, discouragement, or conflict?  
- What specific adjustments to my spiritual “menu” (prayer, scripture, Sabbath, temple) would most nourish me right now?  
- How do my covenants connect me to Christ’s power in daily burdens, and where do I need to ask more directly for His help?  
- In what ways can I focus on Christ so that my joy is less tied to circumstances and more anchored in Him?  
- Who around me might be “heading for the exit,” and how can I lovingly minister—prompted by the Spirit—to bring them back to Christ?
