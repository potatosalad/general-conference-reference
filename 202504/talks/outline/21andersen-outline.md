### Summary

Elder Neil L. Andersen testifies that disciples of Jesus Christ cherish and protect mortal life—especially the unborn—by ministering with Christlike love, upholding revealed doctrine on the sanctity of life and moral agency, and trusting the healing power of the Savior’s Atonement.

### Haiku

Life is a gift breathed,  
Love steadies trembling choices—  
Christ heals what we can’t.

### Key Points

1) Cherish and preserve life
- Life is central to God’s plan; individual identity precedes conception and endures eternally. The Church opposes elective abortion, with rare, prayerfully considered exceptions, reaffirming the sanctity of life.

2) Minister with Christlike love
- In moments of fear and uncertainty, compassionate ministering—spiritual, emotional, and temporal—helps parents feel the Savior’s love and make righteous choices; the Atonement offers healing and forgiveness to all who turn to Him.

3) Teach clearly and compassionately
- Speak openly with youth and in our quorums and organizations about chastity, moral agency, and the care of expectant mothers and their children; faithful examples (adoption, enduring medical trials, covenant loyalty) show how courageous disciples live these truths.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 13:35 — Disciples are known by their love one to another.  
- Moses 1:39 — God’s work and glory include bringing His children to immortality and eternal life.  
- 2 Nephi 2:26–27 — Moral agency: choosing liberty and eternal life through the Redeemer.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 18:10 — The worth of souls is great in the sight of God.  
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World — Sacred powers of procreation and the sanctity of life.  
- First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve statement (General Handbook) — The Church’s doctrine and rare exceptions regarding abortion.  
- Teachings cited: President Russell M. Nelson on ministering to “the one”; President Dallin H. Oaks on premortal identity; President Henry B. Eyring on seeing “what they cannot yet see”; President J. Reuben Clark Jr. on giving doctrine straight to youth.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I immediately and nonjudgmentally support someone facing an unanticipated pregnancy?  
- What does “cherishing life” look like in my daily words, choices, and ministering?  
- How can I teach the law of chastity and the sanctity of life to my family “straight, undiluted,” yet with tenderness and hope?  
- In rare, complex situations, how do we uphold doctrine while honoring personal revelation, privacy, and compassion?  
- If I or someone I love has experienced abortion, what steps of repentance and healing through Christ’s Atonement can begin today?  
- What ward and family resources (ministering, temporal help, counseling, adoption support) can we mobilize to bless parents and children?  
- How can we keep conversations centered on Jesus Christ—not politics—so love, faith, and truth guide our actions?
