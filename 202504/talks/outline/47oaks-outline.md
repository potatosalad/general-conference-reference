### Summary

President Oaks teaches that our Father’s plan equips us with divine helps—centered in Jesus Christ’s Atonement, the Light of Christ, commandments and covenants, and the Holy Ghost—to grow through opposition, choose good, and be prepared to meet the Lord.

### Haiku

Father’s plan guides us,  
Light, law, and Spirit refine—  
Trust Christ; walk His path.

### Key Points

1) God’s plan, agency, and opposition: We lived with God before this life; mortality provides a body, experience, and “opposition in all things,” with agency preserved—making the Savior’s Atonement essential for our progress and redemption.

2) Divine helps along the covenant path: The Lord gives the Light of Christ to all, reveals commandments and provides ordinances and covenants as guardrails, and offers manifestations of the Holy Ghost—with the gift of the Holy Ghost bestowed after baptism to guide, sanctify, and strengthen us.

3) Prepare and persist: Spiritual “maintenance” (daily prayer, scripture study, frequent repentance, weekly sacrament) keeps us ready like the wise virgins; humility and trusting God’s prophets over worldly wisdom help us stay on the covenant path toward eternal life.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Abraham 3:26 — Purpose of mortality and added glory
- Moses 4:4 — Satan’s intent to deceive and bind
- 2 Nephi 2:11 — “Opposition in all things”
- Alma 34:8; Alma 42:23 — The Atonement and resurrection
- Moroni 7:16; D&C 84:46; D&C 136:33 — The Light/Spirit of Christ to know good from evil
- 2 Nephi 31:17; D&C 20:77 — Remission of sins and the promise that His Spirit may always be with us
- Matthew 25:1–13 — Parable of the ten virgins and personal preparation
- Gospel principles to study: Agency, the Plan of Salvation (Plan of Happiness), the difference between manifestations of the Holy Ghost and the gift of the Holy Ghost, covenants and ordinances as the path and guardrails, spiritual maintenance (prayer, scripture, repentance, sacrament), trusting the Lord over worldly wisdom, the family’s divine role.

### Questions to Ponder

- How do I recognize the Light of Christ in my daily choices, and how can I respond to it more quickly?
- Which commandments and covenants most need my renewed attention and obedience this week?
- How can I better distinguish between a manifestation of the Holy Ghost and living worthy of the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost?
- In what ways is my spiritual maintenance (prayer, scripture study, repentance, sacrament) filling my “lamp” like the wise virgins?
- Where might I be trusting “the wisdom of man” over revealed truth, and how will I realign with the Lord?
- What specific help from the Savior’s Atonement do I need right now—forgiveness, strength to resist, comfort, or enabling power?
- How can I support the divine pattern of family in my current circumstances, and seek the Lord’s help where ideals are not possible?
- What change will I make this week to move more fully onto, or remain steady on, the covenant path?
