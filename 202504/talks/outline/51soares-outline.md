### Summary

Elder Ulisses Soares teaches that reverence for sacred things is the essence of spirituality—connecting us to God, deepening gratitude and joy, safeguarding revelation and covenants, guiding our use of technology, and shaping how we worship in our homes, sacrament meetings, and temples.

### Haiku

Shoes off, holy ground;  
Quiet hearts behold the Lord—  
Reverence gives voice.

### Key Points

1. Reverence is the essence of spirituality and transforms discipleship  
   - Like Moses at the burning bush, holy awe draws us nearer to God, anchors our divine identity, and elevates our lives with joy, gratitude, and revelation.

2. Irreverence disrupts revelation and weakens covenant devotion  
   - A casual world breeds carelessness toward the sacred; disciples safeguard their hearts by honoring covenants, language, images, and worship—and by refusing to trust the “arm of flesh,” including unwise reliance on technology or AI.

3. Practice reverence in holy spaces and daily worship  
   - In temples (removing “worldly shoes,” wearing white), at sacrament meeting (setting aside distractions, singing and partaking with intent), and at home (prayer, scripture, obedience), reverence invites the Spirit, creates sanctuaries of faith, and builds covenant confidence through trials.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Exodus 3:1–5 — “Put off thy shoes… for the place… is holy ground”: the pattern of approaching God with awe.
- 2 Nephi 4:34 — Trust in the Lord, not in the “arm of flesh.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 97:15–17 — The Lord’s house is to be a place of purity and reverence where His glory rests.
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:119 — Build a house of prayer, fasting, faith, learning, order, and God.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79 — Sacrament covenants and remembering Christ with exactness.
- Isaiah 58:13–14 — Making the Sabbath a delight through holy focus.
- Principles to study:  
  - Reverence as the foundation of spirituality and conduit for revelation  
  - Covenant keeping and accountability before God  
  - Temple worship and holy habits (removing distractions, clothing the soul in purity)  
  - Discernment in technology use; seeking the Holy Ghost over algorithmic substitutes  
  - Home as a sanctuary of faith

### Questions to Ponder

- Where is “holy ground” in my daily life, and what “worldly shoes” (habits, distractions, media) should I remove to enter it?
- How can I invite more awe and quiet gratitude into my prayers and scripture study this week?
- What specific changes will help my Sabbath and sacrament become more reverent and covenant-focused?
- In what ways might I be trusting the “arm of flesh”—including technology or AI—over the witness of the Holy Ghost?
- How can my home environment better reflect reverence for the sacred and become a sanctuary of faith?
- What language, images, or entertainment in my life may diminish reverence for Deity, and what will I adjust?
- How has reverent worship in the temple strengthened my covenant confidence during trials, and how can I seek that strength more consistently?
