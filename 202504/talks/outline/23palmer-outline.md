### Summary

Elder S. Mark Palmer lovingly invites all who have stepped away—or are barely holding on—to return to Jesus Christ, promising patient healing, nourishing ordinances, and rejoicing in heaven as faith and discipleship are renewed.

### Haiku

Roots still hold to light  
Return and be healed in Christ—  
Heaven sings your name.

### Key Points

1) A loving invitation to return: The Savior’s call—“Return unto me… that I may heal you”—is extended without condemnation; it’s not too late, you are needed, loved, and heaven rejoices when you come home.

2) How to come back and grow: Like the willow tree, the journey may require humility and pruning (repentance), patience over time, steady nourishment through the sacrament and temple worship, and responsiveness to the Spirit.

3) Christ’s healing and our role: The Lord knows your divine potential and never gives up; as disciples, we welcome, love, and minister without judgment, helping one another reclaim faith and joy in Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 3 Nephi 9:13 — “Return unto me… repent… be converted, that I may heal you.”
- John 6:67–68 — “Will ye also go away? … Thou hast the words of eternal life.”
- Luke 15:7, 10, 20–24 — Joy in heaven over those who repent; the welcoming Father.
- Alma 10:6 — Amulek: “I was called many times and I would not hear.”
- Alma 5:33 — His “arms of mercy are extended” to all.
- Alma 7:11–13 — The Savior heals and succors through His Atonement.
- 3 Nephi 18:1–12; Moroni 4–6 — The sacrament as weekly nourishment and renewal.
- Moroni 6:4–6 — Being “nourished by the good word of God” in the Church community.
- Gospel principles: Repentance and conversion bring healing; patience in spiritual growth; ordinances and covenants anchor us; sensitivity to the Holy Ghost; ministering with love, not judgment; divine potential in each soul.

### Questions to Ponder

- What “roots” of belief still connect me to Jesus Christ, and how can I strengthen them this week?
- Which “branches” (habits, offenses, pride, distractions) might the Lord invite me to lovingly prune so I can heal and grow?
- How can weekly sacrament and more intentional temple worship nourish my faith right now?
- When have I felt the Spirit calling me, and how can I better hear and respond?
- What does Peter’s response—“To whom shall we go?”—mean for me in moments of doubt or discouragement?
- How can I be like the neighbor in the story—seeing divine potential and refusing to give up on someone (including myself)?
- What words and actions communicate “welcome home” to a loved one who is returning?
- Where have I seen the Savior’s healing in my life, and what is my next step to come closer to Him?
