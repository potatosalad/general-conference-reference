### Summary

President Henry B. Eyring teaches that we draw nearer to Jesus Christ through prayer, scripture study, covenant remembrance (especially the sacrament), and cheerful service, which invites the Spirit, strengthens our witness, and brings the Savior’s love and power into our lives and families.

### Haiku

Draw near unto Him—  
He walks the road beside us;  
Hearts burn, burdens light.

### Key Points

1) Draw near through simple, steady devotion  
- Remember the Savior, seek Him in the scriptures, and ask through sincere prayer; consistent covenant keeping—especially at the sacrament—invites the Holy Ghost and rekindles closeness with Christ.

2) The risen Lord comes to those who seek Him  
- The Luke 24 account (empty tomb, Emmaus) shows the pattern: Christ “drew near,” opened the scriptures, brought peace, and moved disciples to witness; hearts “burn” as He teaches by the Spirit.

3) Covenant service makes us His witnesses and changes us  
- Mosiah 18 covenants call us to mourn, comfort, bear burdens, and stand as witnesses; as we serve, the Lord makes our burdens light, draws near to loved ones we worry about, perfects us in Him, and will one day say, “Well done.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 88:63 — Draw near, seek diligently, ask and receive, knock and it shall be opened.  
- Luke 24:5–7, 13–35, 36–48 — The empty tomb; the road to Emmaus; “Peace be unto you”; commissioned to preach repentance and remission of sins.  
- Mosiah 18:8–10 — Baptismal covenants to bear burdens, mourn, comfort, and stand as witnesses.  
- Moroni 10:32 — “Be perfected in him.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 100:1 — The Lord’s assurance to Joseph Smith and Sidney Rigdon about their families.  
- Matthew 25:21 — “Well done, thou good and faithful servant.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79 — Sacrament prayers: “always remember him” and keep His commandments.  
- Gospel principles: covenant remembrance, companionship of the Holy Ghost, ministering/charity, personal witness of the Resurrection, trusting the Lord with our loved ones.

### Questions to Ponder

- When do I feel closest to the Savior, and what patterns help me sustain that closeness daily?  
- How will I “always remember Him” more intentionally during the sacrament this week?  
- What would it look like to invite the Savior to “abide” with me at the close of each day?  
- Which single change to my scripture study would best reflect “seek me diligently”?  
- Whom can I serve cheerfully this week so the Lord can make both our burdens light?  
- How do I recognize the Spirit’s “burning” and the Savior’s “Peace be unto you” in my life?  
- In what specific ways can I stand as a witness of Christ in my home, work, and community?  
- How can I exercise faith to trust the Lord’s power and timing with a loved one who is wandering?
