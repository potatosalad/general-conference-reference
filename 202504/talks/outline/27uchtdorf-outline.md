### Summary

Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf teaches that the defining witness of Christ’s true Church is the love His disciples show, which invites belonging, bridges the gap between the ideal and the real, and unites diverse members in one body and one voice centered on Jesus Christ.

### Haiku

Love marks us as His.  
Hearts turn toward Christ as one—  
many notes, one song.

### Key Points

1. Love reveals true discipleship
   - Visitors most notice how they feel among us and how we treat each other; our goal in every gathering is to increase faith in Jesus Christ through Christlike love.

2. Bridging the ideal and the real
   - Though the Church is led by Christ, human weakness remains; don’t give up on the ideal—be patient, seek a mighty change of heart, and align our hearts with the Savior to become “of one heart and one mind.”

3. Belonging in the body and choir of Christ
   - Differences don’t make us misfits but needed parts of the body of Christ; unity comes not from sameness but from facing Christ together—add your unique note to the Savior’s “song of redeeming love,” and invite others to come and see.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 13:34–35 — “By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 1:21 — The Church restored “that faith also might increase in the earth.”
- Book of Mormon Title Page — “If there are faults, they are the mistakes of men; wherefore, condemn not the things of God.”
- Moses 7:18 — Zion: “of one heart and one mind.”
- Alma 5:14, 26 — The mighty change of heart; the “song of redeeming love.”
- 1 Samuel 16:7 — The Lord looks on the heart.
- 1 Corinthians 12:12–27 — Many members, one body in Christ.
- Galatians 3:28; 2 Nephi 26:33 — One in Christ; all are alike unto God.
- Mosiah 18:8–10 — “Come into the fold of God, and be called his people.”
- D&C 50:24; Proverbs 4:18 — Growing brighter “until the perfect day.”
- Luke 1:37; Matthew 19:26 — With God nothing is impossible.
- Gospel principles: Covenant belonging, gathering Israel, normal and natural invitations, patience with imperfection, unity through Christ-centered focus.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can my love at church help a visitor leave with stronger faith in Jesus Christ?
- Where do I see a gap between the ideal and the real in my ward, and how can I respond with patience and faith?
- What specific change of heart do I need to better align with the Savior rather than with personal preferences or cultural norms?
- Who in my ward or neighborhood might feel they don’t fit, and what will I do this week to help them belong?
- How can I make my invitations to “come and see” more normal and natural?
- In what ways can our class or quorum “face the same direction” toward Christ even when we differ in background or views?
- What unique gifts has God given me for the body of Christ, and what do I still need to learn from others’ gifts?
- How can our worship and service become a unified “one voice” that praises God and lifts hearts?
