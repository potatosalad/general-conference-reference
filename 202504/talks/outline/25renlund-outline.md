### Summary

Elder Dale G. Renlund teaches that we can joyfully prepare to meet the Savior—at His Second Coming or at life’s end—by covenanting to follow Him, taking the Holy Ghost as our guide, avoiding deception, magnifying our God-given gifts through His grace, and serving “the least of these.”

### Haiku

Follow Christ with trust  
Take the Spirit as your guide  
Serve the least; fear fades

### Key Points

1) Be spiritually self-reliant and undeceived (Ten Virgins)  
- Receive truth through covenants and ordinances, seek the constant companionship of the Holy Ghost “drop by drop,” and avoid deception through faithful counsel and temple worship.

2) Magnify what God has given you (Talents)  
- Do not compare; rely on the merits, mercy, and grace of Jesus Christ to develop your abilities and become the person God designed you to be.

3) Serve “the least of these” (Sheep and Goats)  
- Use your time, talents, and resources to bless the vulnerable; when you serve others, you serve God—preparation replaces fear with joyful hope.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 25: Parables of the Ten Virgins, the Talents, and the Sheep and Goats (how to prepare to meet Christ)  
- Doctrine and Covenants 45:56–57: The wise receive truth, take the Holy Spirit as their guide, and are not deceived  
- Matthew 24:4–5; Joseph Smith—Matthew 1:5–6: “Take heed that no man deceive you”  
- Moroni 7:12–19: Discernment—what invites to do good and persuades to believe in Christ is of God  
- Mosiah 2:17: “When ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God”  
- Ether 12:27: Through Christ’s grace, weaknesses can become strengths  
- Temple worship and the covenant path: Protection from deception, eternal perspective, power to keep the Spirit  
- President Russell M. Nelson’s recent counsel to prepare now for the Second Coming with joyful optimism

### Questions to Ponder

- What “drops of oil” (daily, private devotion) will I add this week to better receive the Holy Ghost?  
- In what ways am I seeking trusted, faithful counsel—and how does temple worship help me avoid deception?  
- Where am I tempted to compare instead of magnify my own God-given gifts? What is one step to develop my unique capacity with the Savior’s grace?  
- Which “least of these” can I see and serve this week? What time, talent, or resource will I consecrate to help?  
- What fears about the Second Coming linger in my heart, and how does following Jesus Christ replace that fear with hope?  
- Who in my life helps me love the Savior more, and how can I rely on them and the Holy Ghost like a cherished friend?  
- If the Lord asked today, “Why were you not the person I empowered you to be?” what would I want my answer to be—what will I change now?
