### Summary

Elder Michael B. Strong teaches that true discipleship is a deliberate journey to become like Jesus Christ, and its defining sign is charity—pure, active love shown through compassion, noticing unspoken needs, and helping others along the covenant path.

### Haiku

True love marks disciples—  
Compassion sees and serves needs;  
Hearts become like His.

### Key Points

1) Discipleship is deliberate: Following Jesus Christ is a continual, intentional commitment to emulate His attributes until His image is in our countenance.

2) Charity is the sign of true discipleship: Without charity we are nothing; Christ’s pure love—love in action—identifies His followers.

3) How we live charity: We show Christlike love by:
   - Showing compassion that alleviates suffering.
   - Noticing and ministering to unspoken needs.
   - Helping others progress on the covenant path, even in practical, unconventional ways.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 13:34–35 — Love one another; by this shall all know you are Christ’s disciples.
- Moroni 7:45–48 — Charity is the pure love of Christ; pray with all the energy of heart to be filled with it.
- 1 Corinthians 13:1–3 — Without charity we are nothing.
- Doctrine and Covenants 18:19 — Without faith, hope, and charity we can do nothing.
- Alma 5:14 — Receiving His image in our countenances.
- 2 Peter 1:4 — Partakers of the divine nature.
- Acts 10:38 — The Savior “went about doing good.”
- 3 Nephi 17:5–24 — Jesus’s compassion: healing, blessing children, angels ministering.
- Isaiah 41:13 — “I the Lord thy God will hold thy right hand… Fear not; I will help thee.”
- President Russell M. Nelson: “Make our discipleship our highest priority”; “Our adoration of Jesus is best expressed by our emulation of Jesus.”
- President Dallin H. Oaks: Discipleship is not casual or occasional; it is a continuous way of life.
- Preach My Gospel: “Charity, like faith, leads to action.”

### Questions to Ponder

- What deliberate daily practices help me better emulate the Savior’s character?
- Whose suffering around me needs compassionate relief today, and what is one concrete act I can take?
- What unspoken needs can I notice in my family, quorum/class, or ward? How will I quietly minister to them?
- Who could I help along the covenant path this week (welcoming the new, inviting the hesitant, encouraging the weary)?
- How can I better pray “with all the energy of heart” to be filled with Christlike love?
- When have I felt the Lord “hold my hand”? How can that assurance move me to act in love?
- What barriers (bias, busyness, fear) keep me from acting on charitable impressions, and how will I overcome them?
- What is one specific invitation I can extend (e.g., to attend sacrament meeting or the temple) and one service I can offer to make that invitation easier to accept?
