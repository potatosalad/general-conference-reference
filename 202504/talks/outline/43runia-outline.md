### Summary
Sister Tamara W. Runia teaches that repentance is a loving, hopeful invitation from Jesus Christ that does not burden Him but brightens His joy; our worth is constant, shame is from the adversary, and daily, affectionate obedience keeps us in a covenant relationship with the Savior who knows our “degree of difficulty” and advocates for us.

### Haiku
Repentance is light—  
Not a burden—brighter joy;  
Shame yields; hearts choose Him.

### Key Points
1. Repentance is love and hope, not punishment.  
   - God cares most about what we do after mistakes—turning to Him again and again. Repentance is a commandment and the covenant path away from pain; using the Atonement brings the Savior joy.

2. Your worth is unchanging; reject shame and choose godly sorrow.  
   - Satan uses shame to isolate and accuse, but the Lord never attacks our worth. Our worth is constant before God; godly sorrow turns us toward Christ, who delights to forgive without shaming or comparing.

3. Stay in covenant through affectionate obedience and trust Christ’s advocacy.  
   - The Savior knows our unique “degree of difficulty,” descends below all things to lift us, and is our Advocate with the Father. Choosing Him daily brings the “perfect brightness of hope” and disperses darkness.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles
- John 14:15 — “If ye love me, keep my commandments” (including the commandment to repent).
- Alma 29:1–2 — Desire to “cry repentance” to help us avoid suffering.
- Hebrews 12:2 — We are “the joy that was set before him.”
- 2 Nephi 31:20 — The “perfect brightness of hope.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 18:10 — “The worth of souls is great in the sight of God.”
- 3 Nephi 17 — The Savior prays for His people; consider His advocacy for you.
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:6; 122:8 — He “descended below all things.”
- 1 Nephi 8 — Mists of darkness, the iron rod, and the river of filth (staying on the path amid adversity).
- Malachi 4:2; 3 Nephi 25:2 — “Healing in his wings.”
- 2 Corinthians 7:10 — Godly sorrow works repentance unto salvation.
- Related talks to study: President Russell M. Nelson on repentance and daily change; Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf, “The Hope of God’s Light.”

### Questions to Ponder
- When you hear the word “repent,” what feelings arise? How would seeing repentance as an expression of God’s love change your response?
- How can you discern between shame (from the adversary) and godly sorrow (from God)? What does each voice sound like in your life?
- In what ways can you practice “affectionate obedience” this week—choosing Christ because you love Him, even while imperfect?
- What daily habits help you stay in a covenant relationship and keep turning to the Savior again and again?
- If you could hear Jesus Christ praying for you (3 Nephi 17), what do you think He would say to the Father about you today?
- How does remembering that your worth is constant—independent of performance—change the way you approach repentance and discipleship?
- What “degree of difficulty” are you carrying that the Savior understands? How can that awareness help you extend grace to yourself and others?
