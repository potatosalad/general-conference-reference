### Summary

Covenant discipleship binds us to the Father and the Son, deepens our trust in Their perfect love and justice, and endows us with spiritual power to experience Christ’s joy and an eternal perspective—even amid mortal trials—while turning outward to gather Israel.

### Haiku

Bind your heart to Christ  
Keep covenants, feel His joy  
Perspective grows bright

### Key Points

1) Know and trust God through Christ: The Father and the Son are perfectly aligned in love, justice, and mercy; Jesus is both our example and our enabling means to become like the Father.  
2) Covenants bring power and joy: As we keep commandments and abide in Christ’s love, we receive spiritual power, an expanded eternal view, and joy that endures suffering and defies mortal logic.  
3) Covenants turn us outward: Bound to Christ, we naturally join the gathering of Israel, helping others come to Him and rejoicing together in the “fruit of the vineyard.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 15:9–11 — Abide in Christ’s love by keeping His commandments; His purpose is our fulness of joy.  
- John 3:16 — The gift of the Son reveals the Father’s perfect love.  
- Mosiah 5:7 — Through covenant, we become the children of Christ.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 93:21 — Christ “was in the beginning… the Firstborn.”  
- Jacob 5:71–75 — Joy with the Lord in His vineyard as we labor to gather Israel.  
- Philippians 4:7 — Peace that “passeth all understanding,” linked with the Savior’s covenantal joy.  
- Gospel principles: Covenant path; justice and mercy are complementary; spiritual power through ordinances and obedience; eternal perspective through Christ’s Atonement; gathering Israel.

- Referenced teaching: President Russell M. Nelson, “Joy and Spiritual Survival” (Oct. 2016) — Joy in Christ amid trials.

### Questions to Ponder

- What specific commandments will help me more fully “continue in His love” this week?  
- How have my covenants given me spiritual power or joy during a challenging time?  
- In what ways can I deepen my trust in the Father’s perfect justice and mercy?  
- What is one step I can take to participate more actively in the gathering of Israel?  
- How does regular temple worship and sacrament observance expand my eternal perspective?  
- Where can I turn outward—at home, work, or church—to help someone feel the Savior’s covenant love today?  
- What changes would help me better live as a “child of Christ” in thought, word, and deed?
