### Summary

Elder Gary E. Stevenson teaches that disciples of Jesus Christ receive and share His light by talking of, centering on, and testifying of the living, resurrected Savior—especially through Christ-focused worship and a higher, holier observance of Easter—and affirming the literal reality of the Resurrection.

### Haiku

We talk of Christ’s light—  
He lives, the stone rolled away;  
Come, receive, then share.

### Key Points

1. We talk of Christ and share His light  
   - Disciples both receive and reflect the Light of Christ; everyday witness (symbols, words, acts) can open hearts and make the impersonal personal.

2. Prophetic invitations to center on Jesus Christ  
   - Recent adjustments focus us on the Savior: the correct name of the Church, Christ-centered art and youth themes, the Church symbol (Christ under the arch, the chief cornerstone), and elevating Easter as a season with home-centered worship.

3. The literal Resurrection and its hope  
   - Jesus’s Atonement, death, and Resurrection are real; all will rise because He broke the bands of death, bringing forgiveness, peace, and a sure hope in Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Nephi 25:26 — “We talk of Christ…”  
- Ephesians 2:20 — Jesus Christ, the chief cornerstone.  
- Matthew 17:5 — “Hear Him.”  
- Moroni 10:32 — “Come unto Christ.”  
- 1 Corinthians 15:20–22, 54–55 — In Christ all are made alive; death’s sting overcome.  
- Mosiah 16:7–9 — The grave has no victory through Christ.  
- Matthew 28:5–6 — “He is risen.”  
- John 15:13 — Greater love through His sacrifice.  
- 3 Nephi 11:10–11 — The risen Christ appears to the Nephites.  
- The Living Christ: The Testimony of the Apostles — A concise, authoritative witness of the Savior.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “The Correct Name of the Church” (Oct 2018) and introduction of the Church symbol (Apr 2020) — Centering the Church and our lives on Jesus Christ.  
- Gospel principles: Light of Christ; repentance and forgiveness through the Atonement; literal, universal Resurrection; home-centered, Church-supported worship (especially at Easter).

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I more naturally “talk of Christ” in daily conversations and settings this week?  
- What symbols and media in my home and on my devices help me remember Jesus—and what could I change or add?  
- How will I observe a higher and holier Easter season next year (Palm Sunday through Holy Week), starting with one simple tradition?  
- In what ways does belief in a literal Resurrection change how I face grief, mortality, and repentance today?  
- Which prophetic invitations (name usage, Christ-centered art/music, Easter focus) do I feel prompted to act on now?  
- Who in my circle could I invite to come unto Christ, and how can I lovingly extend that invitation?  
- When have I felt the Light of Christ soften a heart—mine or another’s—and how can I seek and share that light more deliberately?
