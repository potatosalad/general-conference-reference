### Summary

President Dallin H. Oaks conducted the sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers by common consent, invited members to manifest their support (or share concerns appropriately), expressed gratitude for released leaders, and announced new callings, reaffirming the Church’s order under living prophets, seers, and revelators.

### Haiku

Hands rise in one faith—  
Common consent binds our hearts;  
Christ orders His Church.

### Key Points

1. The law of common consent: Members were invited to sustain the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles as prophets, seers, and revelators, and to manifest support or share any opposition through proper priesthood channels (stake president).
2. Releases with gratitude: Several General Authority Seventies received emeritus status; the Young Men General Presidency and many Area Seventies were released, with heartfelt thanks for their devoted service.
3. New calls and continued order: New General Authority Seventies, a new Young Men General Presidency, and adjustments in leadership (including President Oaks as President of the Quorum of the Twelve and Elder Holland as Acting President) were sustained, reflecting the Lord’s ongoing, orderly guidance of His Church.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 26:2; 20:65–67 — The Church operates “by common consent” and leaders are called and sustained properly.
- Doctrine and Covenants 21:4–5; 1:38 — Heeding the prophet’s voice is heeding the Lord’s voice.
- Ephesians 4:11–13 — Apostles and prophets are given to perfect the Saints and unify the body of Christ.
- Mosiah 18:8–10 — Covenants to bear one another’s burdens inform how we sustain leaders and fellow Saints.
- Hebrews 13:17 — Be guided by those who watch over our souls.
- Articles of Faith 5 — Leaders are called of God by prophecy and laying on of hands.
- Gospel principles: Common consent; sustaining as a covenantal act of faith; gratitude for released servants; unity and order in the Lord’s Church.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean for me to sustain leaders beyond raising my hand?
- How can I seek and follow personal revelation as I support living prophets and apostles?
- If I have concerns, how can I address them faithfully and appropriately through priesthood channels?
- In what specific ways will I pray for, defend, and assist those newly called in my ward, stake, and the Church?
- How can I show gratitude and continued Christlike support to those who have been released?
- How does the principle of common consent strengthen unity in my family and congregation?
- What small, concrete action can I take this week to better sustain a leader or calling in my local unit?
