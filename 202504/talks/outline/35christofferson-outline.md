### Summary

Elder D. Todd Christofferson teaches that true worship includes devoted Sabbath and daily actions, a grateful heart that yields to God’s will, exclusive loyalty to the Father and the Son, and life-changing emulation of Them through covenants and the Holy Ghost.

### Haiku

Gathered, hearts made one,  
We yield our will, sing His praise—  
Worship changes us.

### Key Points

1. Worship as holy action, public and private  
   - We gather on the Lord’s day to pray, sing, learn, and partake of the sacrament; we also worship daily at home through prayer, scripture study, service, and “living in thanksgiving daily.”

2. Worship of the heart—gratitude, love, and submission  
   - True worship flows from loving God with all our heart and yielding our will to His, focusing on the Savior’s Atonement and sacrament; such intentional worship transforms us.

3. Exclusive devotion that leads to Christlike emulation  
   - We must have no other gods before Him; on the covenant path—through ordinances, temple worship, and repentance—we receive divine power to become more like the Father and the Son.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 2:1–2 — Wise men come to worship the newborn King.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 59:9, 12 — House of prayer; offer oblations and sacraments on the Lord’s day.  
- D&C 88:78 — Be “instructed more perfectly” in His gospel.  
- Exodus 31:13; Ezekiel 20:12, 20 — The Sabbath as a sign between God and His people.  
- Alma 34:26–27, 38 — Pray always; live in thanksgiving daily.  
- 3 Nephi 9:20 — Offer a broken heart and a contrite spirit.  
- Matthew 22:37 — Love God with all your heart, soul, and mind.  
- Matthew 26:39 — “Not as I will, but as thou wilt.”  
- Daniel 3:15–18, 28 — Shadrach, Meshach, and Abed-nego’s exclusive worship: “But if not...” faith.  
- John 17:3 — Know “the only true God, and Jesus Christ.”  
- D&C 84:20 — In ordinances, the power of godliness is manifest.  
- Elder Patrick Kearon, recent general conference — Worship that intentionally transforms us.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, teachings on the Sabbath as a sign and on covenant power (see “The Sabbath Is a Delight,” Apr. 2015; “Spiritual Treasures,” Oct. 2019).

### Questions to Ponder

- What “sign” am I giving God by how I keep the Sabbath?  
- How can my participation in the sacrament show deeper gratitude and love for the Savior this week?  
- In what ways has gathering with the Saints strengthened my discipleship compared to worshipping alone?  
- What “other gods” (habits, loyalties, causes) compete with my exclusive devotion to the Father and the Son?  
- Where do I need to say, “Nevertheless, not as I will, but as Thou wilt”?  
- How can I make repentance a daily act of worship that invites the Holy Ghost?  
- What attribute of the Father or the Son will I intentionally emulate this week?  
- How can I help my home become a house of prayer, learning, and “thanksgiving daily”?
