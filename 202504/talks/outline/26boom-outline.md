### Summary

Elder Hans T. Boom teaches the rising generation to draw strength from the faith and sacrifices of parents and pioneers, to make and keep covenants through trials, and to “carry on” by serving Christ—especially through missionary service—and building their lives on His rock.

### Haiku

Parents’ faith a flame,  
Carry on through hard trials,  
Built on Christ, the Rock.

### Key Points

1. Honor the legacy of faith
   - The stories of pioneers, parents, and first-generation converts—like Mary Wanlass and Elder Boom’s family—show that faithful endurance blesses future generations and invites us to “just carry on.”
2. Covenants, sacrifice, and endurance deepen testimony
   - Trusting Christ through hardship and choosing sacrifice for His Church bring heaven’s blessings; staying true when misunderstood or mistreated refines conviction.
3. Accept the call to serve and pass the torch
   - President Nelson invites every worthy, able young man (and able young women, optionally) to serve; service helps us know the Master and equips us to become “goodly parents” and mentors who pass on a living legacy of discipleship.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 36:3, 27 — The Lord supports those who trust Him in trials and troubles of every kind.
- Mosiah 5:13 — We come to know the Master by serving Him.
- Helaman 5:12 — Build on the rock of our Redeemer to withstand life’s storms.
- Joseph Smith—History 1:25 — “I knew it, and I could not deny it.”
- Hymns and songs:
  - “We’ll Bring the World His Truth” (Children’s Songbook): identity and mission of the rising generation.
  - “True to the Faith” (Hymn 254): covenant loyalty amid opposition.
  - “Praise to the Man”: “Sacrifice brings forth the blessings of heaven.”
- President Russell M. Nelson’s invitation to missionary service for all worthy, able young men (and optional but powerful for young women).
- Gospel principles: covenant fidelity, sacrifice, enduring to the end, honoring spiritual heritage, becoming first-generation pioneers, service that leads to knowing Christ.

### Questions to Ponder

- What stories of faith in my family line or ward can strengthen me today? What story am I creating for those who follow me?
- What sacrifice is the Lord inviting me to make now to deepen my discipleship?
- How can I honor my parents’ or ancestors’ faith—even if their example was imperfect—or become a “goodly parent” for future generations?
- How do I respond when I’m misunderstood or wronged at church? What does being “true to the faith” look like then?
- In what daily, practical ways am I building on the rock of Jesus Christ?
- How will I seek and act on prophetic counsel about missionary service (teaching or service missions)? If I have already served, how can I keep a missionary heart?
- Which covenant-centered habits can I model in my home to help the next generation “carry on” in Christ?
