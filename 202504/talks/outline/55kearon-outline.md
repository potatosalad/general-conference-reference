### Summary

Elder Patrick Kearon teaches that our Heavenly Father—the ultimate Giver—offers His greatest gift, Jesus Christ, and invites us to actively receive the transformative truth that we are God’s children, which unlocks every other divine gift.

### Haiku

Open heart, receive  
Father of lights gives His Son—  
Know you are His child.

### Key Points

1) God is the Giver; Christ is the greatest gift  
All good gifts “come down from the Father of lights,” and every blessing flows through the Atonement and Resurrection of Jesus Christ—the supreme gift that makes all other gifts possible.

2) Accepting vs. receiving  
Beyond accepting and opening, true receiving means valuing, using, and remembering the gift and the Giver; the core gift to receive is the soul-deep knowledge that we are literally children of God, which reframes our identity, trials, and hope.

3) How to receive more fully  
We “let it in” by grace—seeking the Spirit’s witness and nurturing it through scripture, prayer, covenants and temple worship, service, gratitude, and trust in Christ—thus “laying hold upon every good gift” with confidence in His loving-kindness.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- James 1:17 — Every good and perfect gift is from the Father of lights.  
- John 3:16 — God’s greatest gift is His Only Begotten Son.  
- D&C 88:33 — A gift not received brings neither joy in the gift nor in the Giver.  
- 2 Nephi 2:8 — We receive blessings only through the “merits, and mercy, and grace of the Holy Messiah.”  
- 2 Nephi 2:25 — We are that we might have joy.  
- Romans 8:16–17; Acts 17:28–29 — We are children/offspring of God; heirs with Christ.  
- Moroni 10:8, 30 — Deny not the gifts of God; come unto Christ and lay hold upon every good gift.  
- Gospel principles: Divine identity as sons and daughters of God; covenant relationship with the Father through Christ; receiving vs. merely accepting; grace-centered discipleship; seeking and recognizing the Spirit.

### Questions to Ponder

- What is the difference in your life between “accepting/opening” a gift from God and truly “receiving” it?  
- How would your daily choices change if you fully internalized, “I am a beloved child of God”?  
- What thoughts or traditions make it harder for you to “let in” this gift (e.g., feelings of being undeserving), and how can you replace them with truth through Christ’s grace?  
- When have you recognized a “gift of God” recently, and how did remembering the Giver deepen your gratitude?  
- Which practices this week (scripture, temple, prayer, service, gratitude) will help you receive this truth more deeply?  
- How do your covenants help you come unto Christ and “lay hold upon every good gift”?  
- Whom can you gently help remember their divine identity, and how might you do it in Christlike ways?
