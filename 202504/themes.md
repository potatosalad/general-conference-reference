### Summary

This conference centered on Jesus Christ—His Atonement as our ultimate rescue and gift—and invited us to draw near to Him through humble, childlike faith, daily repentance, and covenant discipleship. Leaders repeatedly emphasized:
- Believing, belonging, and becoming in Christ: nurturing faith in homes and Primary; belonging through covenants and Church participation; becoming like Him through charity and service.
- Covenant worship: elevating the sacrament, making temple worship a priority, and living a holier Sabbath and a more Christ-focused Easter.
- Charity as the defining sign of true discipleship; letting love bridge differences and witness to the world that we are His.
- Personal preparation to meet the Savior: taking the Holy Ghost as our guide, softening our hearts, avoiding deception, and joyfully engaging in callings, missionary work, and ministering.
- Trust in God’s “plan of mercy”: opposition as a tutor, compensating blessings amid unmet righteous desires, and hope for those returning to the covenant path.
- Reliance on divine helps: Jesus Christ’s Atonement, the Light of Christ, commandments and covenants, and the Holy Ghost.
- Anchoring in the Restoration: priesthood authority and keys, worldwide temple growth, and the Lord hastening His work—under prophetic direction with provident, transparent stewardship.

The call was clear: receive the Father’s greatest Gift—His Son—bind yourself to Him through covenants, let His love transform you into a true disciple, and participate with joy in gathering Israel as you prepare to meet Him.
