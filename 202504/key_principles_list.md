### Key Principles

1. Common consent unites us in covenant loyalty to living prophets and the Lord’s house of order. ([&ldquo;Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers&rdquo; - Presented by President Dallin H. Oaks](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/11oaks?lang=eng))
2. Faithful, provident stewardship of sacred funds reflects consecration, integrity, and trust in God’s revealed pattern. ([&ldquo;Church Auditing Department Report, 2024&rdquo; - Presented by Jared B. Larson](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/12larson?lang=eng))
3. Childlike humility, purity, and trust anchor discipleship and priesthood service in Christlike love. ([&ldquo;As a Little Child&rdquo; - President Jeffrey R. Holland](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/13holland?lang=eng))
4. Through faith, repentance, and covenants, Christ grants spiritual wholeness now while we patiently await full healing. ([&ldquo;Spiritually Whole in Him&rdquo; - President Camille N. Johnson](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/14johnson?lang=eng))
5. As the Lord hastens His work in temples and missions, we hasten our holiness by worshiping and trusting Jesus Christ. ([&ldquo;Right Before Our Eyes&rdquo; - Elder Ronald A. Rasband](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/15rasband?lang=eng))
6. The Savior’s Atonement is our refuge and model, moving us to rescue others and remember Him in sacrament and temple. ([&ldquo;The Atonement of Jesus Christ Provides the Ultimate Rescue&rdquo; - Elder Quentin L. Cook](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/16cook?lang=eng))
7. Love for Jesus Christ is the covenant-centered motive that turns prophetic invitations into joyful discipleship. ([&ldquo;My Love for the Savior Is My “Why”&rdquo; - Elder Ricardo P. Giménez](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/17gimenez?lang=eng))
8. Daily prayer, scripture, sacrament, and service invite the Spirit and draw us closer to the Savior in our homes and families. ([&ldquo;“Draw Near unto Me”&rdquo; - President Henry B. Eyring](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/18eyring?lang=eng))
9. Disciples honor the sanctity of life with Christlike compassion, moral courage, and trust in the Savior’s healing Atonement. ([&ldquo;Cherishing Life&rdquo; - Elder Neil L. Andersen](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/21andersen?lang=eng))
10. Aaronic Priesthood authority prepares young men to bless the Church through sacred ordinances—especially the sacrament—in covenant power. ([&ldquo;Divine Authority, Sublime Young Men&rdquo; - President Steven J. Lund](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/22lund?lang=eng))
11. However far we’ve strayed, Christ lovingly welcomes our return through repentance, ordinances, and nurturing fellowship. ([&ldquo;“Return unto Me … That I May Heal You”&rdquo; - Elder S. Mark Palmer](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/23palmer?lang=eng))
12. Faith matures from trusting Christ to loyal covenant keeping that invites His power and miracles in God’s time. ([&ldquo;Faith: A Bond of Trust and Loyalty&rdquo; - Elder Sandino Roman](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/24roman?lang=eng))
13. Simple obedience, Spirit-guided living, and service to “the least of these” calm fear and prepare us for His coming. ([&ldquo;Personal Preparation to Meet the Savior&rdquo; - Elder Dale G. Renlund](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/25renlund?lang=eng))
14. Drawing strength from faithful forebears, we build on Christ’s rock, keep covenants, and join the gathering through missionary service. ([&ldquo;True to the Faith That Our Parents Have Cherished&rdquo; - Elder Hans T. Boom](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/26boom?lang=eng))
15. Charity-fueled belonging and unity are the Church’s living witness that we are Christ’s disciples. ([&ldquo;“By This All Will Know That You Are My Disciples”&rdquo; - Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/27uchtdorf?lang=eng))
16. A Christ-centered life speaks of, worships, and testifies of the living, resurrected Lord—especially in a holier Easter. ([&ldquo;And We Talk of Christ&rdquo; - Elder Gary E. Stevenson](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/31stevenson?lang=eng))
17. In home and Primary, we help children believe, belong through covenants, and become like Jesus Christ. ([&ldquo;Thou Art the Christ&rdquo; - Sister Amy A. Wright](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/32wright?lang=eng))
18. God’s plan is a plan of mercy accessed through temples, repentance, and the Book of Mormon’s witness of Christ. ([&ldquo;The Plan of Mercy&rdquo; - Elder James R. Rasband](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/33rasband?lang=eng))
19. The Light of Christ and covenants steady our course so we endure—and rejoice—to the end in Jesus. ([&ldquo;Our Heavenly Guidance System&rdquo; - Elder Sergio R. Vargas](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/34vargas?lang=eng))
20. True worship yields our hearts to the Father and the Son, shaping daily discipleship through covenants and the Holy Ghost. ([&ldquo;Worship&rdquo; - Elder D. Todd Christofferson](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/35christofferson?lang=eng))
21. The Restoration’s divine sequence—First Vision to priesthood keys—summons us to seek personal witness and build Zion. ([&ldquo;The Times of Restitution of All Things&rdquo; - Elder David A. Bednar](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/41bednar?lang=eng))
22. Saying yes to callings and service is the Lord’s way to steadily prepare us and our families to meet Him. ([&ldquo;Participate to Prepare for Christ’s Return&rdquo; - Elder Steven D. Shumway](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/42shumway?lang=eng))
23. Repentance is a hopeful gift that affirms our unchanging worth and deepens our covenant bond with Christ. ([&ldquo;Your Repentance Doesn’t Burden Jesus Christ; It Brightens His Joy&rdquo; - Sister Tamara W. Runia](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/43runia?lang=eng))
24. When righteous desires are blocked, the Lord—through His love and Atonement—provides compensating blessings as we consecrate all we can. ([&ldquo;Compensating Blessings&rdquo; - Bishop Gérald Caussé](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/44causse?lang=eng))
25. In Jesus Christ we find peace, becoming, and belonging that gather us into covenant families and His restored Church. ([&ldquo;Eternity’s Great Gifts: Jesus Christ’s Atonement, Resurrection, Restoration&rdquo; - Elder Gerrit W. Gong](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/45gong?lang=eng))
26. Binding ourselves to Christ through covenants brings power, joy, and an eternal perspective that turns us outward to gather Israel. ([&ldquo;Joy Through Covenant Discipleship&rdquo; - Elder John A. McCune](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/46mccune?lang=eng))
27. Through Christ’s Atonement, the Light of Christ, commandments, covenants, and the Holy Ghost, God equips us to grow through opposition. ([&ldquo;Divine Helps for Mortality&rdquo; - President Dallin H. Oaks](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/47oaks?lang=eng))
28. Reverence unlocks revelation, deepens joy, safeguards covenants, and elevates worship at home, in sacrament, and in the temple. ([&ldquo;Reverence for Sacred Things&rdquo; - Elder Ulisses Soares](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/51soares?lang=eng))
29. Becoming like Christ is measured by active charity that notices needs and lifts others along the covenant path. ([&ldquo;Charity—a Sign of True Discipleship&rdquo; - Elder Michael B. Strong](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/52strong?lang=eng))
30. After sin, healing comes as we run to Christ and His covenant community rather than hiding in shame. ([&ldquo;Beware the Second Temptation&rdquo; - Elder Scott D. Whiting](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/53whiting?lang=eng))
31. Daily repentance and humility soften our hearts to receive the Spirit’s light and understanding. ([&ldquo;Harden Not Your Heart&rdquo; - Elder Christopher H. Kim](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/54kim?lang=eng))
32. Embracing our divine identity and receiving the Father’s supreme Gift—His Son—opens the way to every other heavenly gift. ([&ldquo;Receive His Gift&rdquo; - Elder Patrick Kearon](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/55kearon?lang=eng))
33. Christ embodies the Father’s perfect, personal, perpetual love, felt through faith, repentance, covenants, and Spirit-led service. ([&ldquo;The Love of God&rdquo; - Elder Benjamin M. Z. Tai](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/56tai?lang=eng))
34. Charity, virtue, and temple-centered covenant living cause our confidence before God to wax strong now and eternally. ([&ldquo;Confidence in the Presence of God&rdquo; - President Russell M. Nelson](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/04/57nelson?lang=eng))
