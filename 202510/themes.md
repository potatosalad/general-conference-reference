### Summary

In a time of tender transition following President Russell M. Nelson’s passing, the Lord’s work went forward in order and with faith. A unifying message rang through every session: turn your heart to Jesus Christ and let His covenants bind you to Him and to each other. Speakers invited us to reconcile ourselves to God through the Savior’s Atonement, receive “new beginnings,” and become through steady, simple, covenant-keeping discipleship.

Repeated themes included:
- Jesus Christ at the center: His atoning love heals, forgives, empowers, and gives spiritual sight. He offers as many fresh starts as we need as we repent, remember Him, and follow Him daily.
- Covenants and the temple: Sacrament tables and temple altars bind us to the Lord, refine us in proving moments, and enable us to take upon us His name, align with His work, and receive power to become. President Oaks emphasized a family-centered, temple-anchored gospel and noted a pause in new temple announcements to focus on providing ordinances worldwide.
- Identity and agency: Knowing we are beloved children of Heavenly Father changes everything. Through agency, we choose Christ repeatedly; the Final Judgment becomes “pleasing” as we acknowledge what we have become in Him.
- Belonging and ministering: Zion is built “one by one.” We count, account, and care by name so that no one sits alone—at church, at home, or online. Service (including by service missionaries), peacemaking, and temperance shape a covenant culture of kindness, inclusion, and self-mastery.
- Home-centered formation: Primary music, simple Christlike habits, and the Book of Mormon deepen conversion, teach pure doctrine, and invite the Holy Ghost to tutor hearts—especially the young.
- Family and prophets: The Family: A Proclamation to the World was reaffirmed as revelatory and timeless. We were invited to live as equal partners, welcome those who feel they “don’t fit,” and sustain living prophets and apostles as we prepare for the Savior’s return.
- Hastening the work: As the Lord accelerates His gathering, we love, nurture, and integrate new and returning members, trusting that small and simple, faithful steps—taken with all our hearts—bring great things to pass.

Across it all, the Lord asked for our hearts. In Christ, through covenants, we find healing, holiness, and a place in His family—now and forever.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean for me to take upon myself the name of Christ today? (Mosiah 5:7–12; Moroni 4–5; Elder Dale G. Renlund; Elder B. Corey Cuvelier)
- How will I turn my heart fully to God and let Him give me a new heart? (1 Samuel 16:7; Ezekiel 36:26; Alma 5; Elder Ronald M. Barcellos)
- In what daily ways can I be reconciled to God through Christ? (2 Corinthians 5:18–20; Enos; Elder Kelly R. Johnson)
- How can I ensure “no one sits alone” in my ward or branch? Whom will I count, account for, and lift this week? (Luke 15; Mosiah 18:8–10; Elder Gerrit W. Gong; Elder William K. Jackson)
- Where can I practice peacemaking—especially at home and online? (Matthew 5:9; 3 Nephi 11:29; Elder Gary E. Stevenson)
- How does temperance strengthen my spiritual foundation and harmonize my other virtues? (Galatians 5:22–23; Elder Ulisses Soares)
- How can Primary music and the Book of Mormon invite the Spirit more powerfully into my home? (Doctrine and Covenants 25:12; 2 Nephi 32:3; Sister Tracy Y. Browning; Elder Jeffrey R. Holland; Elder Ozani Farias)
- What covenant-focused, “small and simple” habits will I choose to add more of Jesus Christ into my life? (Alma 37:6–7; Elder Michael Cziesla; Elder John D. Amos; Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf)
- How will I welcome, nurture, and involve new and returning members as the work hastens? (Moroni 6:4–6; 3 Nephi 18:22–25; Elder Quentin L. Cook)
- How does Christ’s Atonement both forgive my sins and heal wounds caused by others? (Alma 7:11–13; Elder Neil L. Andersen)
- How can I make the Final Judgment “pleasing” by choosing to become in Christ now? (2 Nephi 2; Alma 41; Elder David A. Bednar)
- What will I do to sustain living prophets and apply the Family Proclamation in my home today? (Doctrine and Covenants 1:38; Amos 3:7; The Family: A Proclamation to the World; Sister Andrea Muñoz Spannaus; Elder Ronald A. Rasband; President Dallin H. Oaks)
