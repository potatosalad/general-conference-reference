## 1. Introduction - President Dallin H. Oaks

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/19oaks?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/19oaks?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Dallin H. Oaks explains that, following President Russell M. Nelson’s passing, general conference will proceed as President Nelson planned, funeral services will follow soon after, tributes in conference will be brief, and we honor the prophet by sustaining the Lord’s work with order and faith in Christ.

### Haiku

Prophet’s voice still guides;  
In ordered grief we press on—  
Christ leads; we sustain.

### Key Points

1. Proceeding as planned: Out of respect for President Nelson’s months of preparation, conference continues with the speakers and music he assigned; President Oaks will speak in the closing session in President Nelson’s place.
2. Doing all things in order: Though the timing is rare, leadership meetings and funeral arrangements are being carried out promptly and properly, with the approval of President Nelson’s family and the Quorum of the Twelve.
3. Honoring the prophet: Conference tributes will be brief, with fuller remembrances at the funeral; President Oaks offers personal love and witness of President Nelson as a model disciple of Jesus Christ.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 1:38 — The Lord speaks through His servants; sustaining prophetic direction.
- 1 Corinthians 14:40 — “Let all things be done decently and in order”; the Lord’s pattern in Church governance.
- Mosiah 18:9 — Mourn with those that mourn; comfort and covenant belonging.
- 1 Thessalonians 4:13 — We sorrow with hope in Christ amid loss.
- Doctrine and Covenants 107:22–24 — The role and authority of the Quorum of the Twelve; orderly succession.
- Gospel principles to study: sustaining living prophets, the purpose and power of general conference, unity and order in the Church, discipleship centered in Jesus Christ.
- Related messages from President Nelson: “Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives” (Apr 2018); “Pure Truth, Pure Doctrine, and Pure Revelation” (Oct 2021).

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I best prepare to receive the direction President Nelson anticipated for this conference?
- What does it mean to sustain the First Presidency and the Quorum of the Twelve, especially during a transition?
- Which qualities from President Nelson’s discipleship should I more fully emulate?
- How does the Lord’s pattern of “doing all things in order” bring peace and clarity in times of grief?
- In what ways can I “mourn with those that mourn” while keeping my focus on the Savior?

## 2. Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers - Presented by Elder Henry B. Eyring

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/11eyring?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/11eyring?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Henry B. Eyring presented the sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers by common consent, affirming the Apostles as prophets, seers, and revelators and noting newly sustained Area Seventies.

### Haiku

Hands raised in consent,  
Hearts knit to serve the Savior—  
We sustain and act.

### Key Points

1) Common consent in the Church  
Members were invited to sustain Church leaders “in the usual way,” with any opposition to be shared through proper priesthood channels (stake president), reflecting order, unity, and accountability in the Lord’s Church.

2) Sustaining prophetic leadership  
The President of the Quorum of the Twelve and the Twelve were presented for sustaining as prophets, seers, and revelators—signifying our covenant willingness to support, follow, and pray for those called by God.

3) Organizational updates and continuity  
Two new Area Seventies—Bhanu K. Hiranandani and Fernando Vivas—were sustained, and the body of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers was sustained as presently constituted, affirming continuity in Church leadership.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- D&C 26:2 — All things to be done by common consent, by much prayer and faith.  
- D&C 20:65 — Ordinations and callings to occur with the vote of the Church.  
- Articles of Faith 1:5 — Called of God by prophecy and by the laying on of hands.  
- D&C 107:22–24 — Governance and roles of the First Presidency and the Twelve.  
- Ephesians 4:11–13 — The Lord gives Apostles and prophets to perfect the Saints and unify the Church.  
- Mosiah 18:21 — Hearts knit together in unity and love.  
- Henry B. Eyring, “The Power of Sustaining Faith” (Apr. 2019).  
- Russell M. Nelson, “Sustaining the Prophets” (Oct. 2014).

### Questions to Ponder

## 3. Blessed Are the Peacemakers - Elder Gary E. Stevenson

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/12stevenson?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/12stevenson?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Gary E. Stevenson teaches that peacemaking is a Christlike, courageous discipline that begins in our hearts, extends to our homes, and radiates into our communities—where we choose kindness, forgiveness, and bridge-building, including online.

### Haiku

Peace begins within,  
Kindness mends homes and divides—  
Children show the way.

### Key Points

1. Peacemaking in our hearts: We cultivate Christlike qualities—kindness, forgiveness, meekness—following the simple, pure example of children and the Savior’s Sermon on the Mount.
2. Peacemaking at home: We influence family relationships by the Lord’s pattern—persuasion, long-suffering, gentleness, kindness, meekness, and love unfeigned—choosing to forgive and deliberately build others up.
3. Peacemaking in our communities: We see others as children of God, reject contention, and become bridge-builders; Elder Stevenson invites a one-week plan: create a contention-free home zone, build digital bridges (don’t post contention), and repair strained relationships.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 5:9 — “Blessed are the peacemakers.”  
- Matthew 5:16 — Let your light so shine before others.  
- Matthew 5:39, 44 — Turn the other cheek; love your enemies.  
- John 14:27 — “My peace I give unto you… let not your heart be troubled.”  
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:41–42 — Lead by persuasion, gentleness, meekness, love unfeigned.  
- Mosiah 4:13 — “Not have a mind to injure one another, but to live peaceably.”  
- 3 Nephi 12:9 — Book of Mormon witness of the Beatitude on peacemaking.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Peacemakers Needed” (Apr 2023) — Disagree without being disagreeable; reject contention.  
- Gospel principles: Forgiveness, meekness, charity, agency to de-escalate, seeing all as children of God, courageous compassion without sacrificing truth.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where does contention most easily enter my heart, and what Christlike habit could replace it this week?  
- How can I better apply the Lord’s pattern in D&C 121 at home when tensions rise?  
- What is one online behavior I will change to “publish peace” instead of perpetuating conflict?  
- Whom could I forgive or reconcile with to repair and reunite a strained relationship?  
- How do I demonstrate strength without harshness—holding to truth while extending compassion?  
- What do the children around me teach about simple, everyday peacemaking?  
- How can my quorum/class help create “peacemaker zones” at church, school, and in our neighborhoods?

## 4. Tune Your Heart to Jesus Christ: The Sacred Gift of Primary Music - Sister Tracy Y. Browning

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/13browning?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/13browning?lang=eng)

### Summary

Primary music is a sacred, Spirit-filled tool that teaches doctrine, imprints truth on tender hearts, and helps children remember covenants and testify of Jesus Christ throughout their lives.

### Haiku

Childlike songs take root,  
Writing His law on soft hearts—  
Christ tunes us to Him.

### Key Points

1. Primary music teaches doctrine and bears testimony: Simple, memorable songs convey clear truths about Jesus Christ and His Atonement, invite the Spirit, and give children a natural language to testify.
2. Songs imprint truth for a lifetime: Music helps the Holy Ghost “bring all things to [our] remembrance,” returning in times of choice and trial to strengthen faith and shape covenant living.
3. Music prepares for ordinances and covenant renewal: Joyful, intentional teaching through song helps children feel the Spirit, remember the Savior, and prepare for baptism, confirmation, and weekly sacrament worship.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 25:12 — “The song of the righteous is a prayer unto me.”
- John 14:26 — The Comforter will teach and bring truth to remembrance.
- Matthew 26:30 (see also Mark 14:26) — The Savior and His disciples sang a hymn before Gethsemane.
- Hebrews 6:17–19 — God’s promises are sure; hope as an “anchor” to the soul.
- Jeremiah 31:33; Hebrews 8:10 — God will write His law in our hearts.
- Alma 32:27–43 — Planting and nourishing the seed of faith.
- Moroni 4–5; Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79 — Sacrament prayers: take His name, always remember Him, keep His commandments.
- 1 Corinthians 14:15 — Sing with the spirit and with understanding.
- Teachings referenced:
  - President Dallin H. Oaks, Worship through Music (on hymns teaching doctrine).
  - President Russell M. Nelson, teachings on music’s power to heal, nourish, and facilitate worship.
  - Elder Henry B. Eyring, teachings on recognizing the Spirit as evidence of the Savior’s Atonement working in our lives.
- Gospel principles: Teaching in the home and Church, discipleship and testimony, covenant memory, the role of the Holy Ghost, preparation for ordinances, worship through sacred music.

### Questions to Ponder

- What Primary song first helped write a gospel truth on your heart, and how does it still shape your discipleship?
- How can I more intentionally teach doctrine during music time—helping children notice and name what they feel from the Holy Ghost?
- In what ways can Primary music prepare children for baptism, confirmation, and weekly sacrament worship?
- How can families use Primary songs at home (prayer, scripture time, car rides) to “always remember” the Savior?
- How might we include every child—new members, multilingual families, neurodiverse learners—so each can feel the Spirit through music?
- When facing decisions or discouragement, which song could I “tune my heart” with to anchor my hope in Jesus Christ?

## 5. The Lord Looketh on the Heart - Elder Ronald M. Barcellos

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/14barcellos?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/14barcellos?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Ronald M. Barcellos teaches that the Lord looks on the heart—our desires and motives—and invites us to turn our hearts fully to Jesus Christ through sincere discipleship, self-assessment, and covenant living so He can give us a new heart.

### Haiku

Not height nor bright eyes,  
The Lord looks on the soft heart—  
Yield, and He makes new.

### Key Points

1. God judges the heart, not appearances.  
   The Lord values our desires and motives as much as our works; He requires a broken heart, a willing mind, and love for Him above all else.

2. Assess your spiritual heart.  
   Evaluate focus and motives, willingness to obey, diligence in scripture study and revelation, and the purity of thoughts and words.

3. Let Christ change your heart.  
   Draw near through daily scripture, prayer, and fasting; align your will by making and keeping covenants; and serve God and others with real intent.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 1 Samuel 16:7 — The Lord looks on the heart.  
- Matthew 22:37 — Love God with all your heart.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 64:34 — The Lord requires the heart and a willing mind.  
- 3 Nephi 9:20 — Offer a broken heart and contrite spirit.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 64:33; Ether 12:27 — Small things become great; weak things become strong in Christ.  
- Matthew 5:8 — Blessed are the pure in heart.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 137:9 — Judged by works and the desires of our hearts.  
- Matthew 6:21; Doctrine and Covenants 121:34–35 — Where your treasure is; hearts set on the world vs. being chosen.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–29 — Don’t wait to be commanded; avoid a doubtful heart and slothfulness.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 6:20; Mosiah 12:27 — Treasure up the word; apply your heart to understanding.  
- Matthew 15:19; Matthew 12:34 — Thoughts and words reveal the heart.  
- Ezekiel 36:26 — A new heart and a new spirit from the Lord.  
- 1 Nephi 2:16; Helaman 3:35 — The Lord can soften and sanctify hearts as we fast and pray.  
- John 14:15; 2 Nephi 31:13 — If ye love me, keep my commandments; receive the Holy Ghost with full purpose of heart.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 4:2 — Serve Him with all your heart, might, mind, and strength.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where is my treasure, and what does that reveal about my heart’s true priorities?  
- In what ways am I obeying proactively rather than waiting to be compelled or commanded?  
- How can I increase my daily “treasuring up” of the word so my understanding sinks into my heart?  
- What recent thoughts or words of mine reveal a need for greater purity, charity, or humility?  
- Which covenant do I most need to honor this week with fuller purpose of heart?  
- What specific practice (prayer, fasting, temple worship, service) will I use to strengthen my relationship with Christ?  
- Where do I still resist God’s will, and how can I yield that part of my heart to Him today?  
- Whom can I serve this week with real intent so that my heart grows in love?

## 6. Know Who You Really Are - Elder Brik V. Eyre

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/15eyre?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/15eyre?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Brik V. Eyre teaches that truly knowing we are beloved children of Heavenly Father—validated by personal prayer and a personal relationship with Jesus Christ—changes everything about how we face trials, repent, and move forward in discipleship.

### Haiku

Child of God, stand firm—  
seek heaven’s voice in the quiet;  
Christ is mighty, yours.

### Key Points

1) Know who you really are: You are a child of God  
- Our core identity is divine; Heavenly Father names us His children while the adversary tries to label us by weaknesses. Seek validation vertically from God, not horizontally from the world.

2) Deepen identity through real communion with God  
- “Be with God” in prayer by preparing, pondering, and seeking a quiet place; increase frequency and intent. Daily Book of Mormon study and temple worship tune us to revelation and the still small voice.

3) Come to know Jesus Christ as your personal Savior  
- The Atonement is individually applied—He is “mighty to save” you. Knowing Him brings the “song of redeeming love,” assures forgiveness, and anchors us in the Father’s plan to bring us home.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Moses 1:4, 6, 12–13 — God declares Moses His son; Satan counters with lesser labels.  
- 3 Nephi 13:6 (cf. Matthew 6:6) — Pray in secret; “be with God.”  
- Enos 1:4 — A soul that hungers and prays after pondering.  
- Joseph Smith—History 1:11–13 — Preparation and prayer precede revelatory experience.  
- 1 Kings 19:12; 1 Nephi 17:45 — Hear and heed the “still small voice.”  
- Alma 5:26 — The “song of redeeming love.”  
- Isaiah 63:1 — The Messiah is “mighty to save.”  
- D&C 20:77, 79; Moroni 4–5 — Sacrament prayers: “God, the Eternal Father.”  
- Romans 8:16–17 — Children and heirs of God.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Choices for Eternity” — Identity triad: child of God, child of the covenant, disciple of Jesus Christ.  
- Elder Patrick Kearon — God’s “fabulous plan,” His relentless pursuit to bring us home.  
- Elder Jeffrey R. Holland — “Pray more; pray harder.”

### Questions to Ponder

- When the adversary tries to define me by my weaknesses, how can I answer as Moses did: “I am a child of God”?  
- What small changes can I make so my prayers move from “saying prayers” to truly “being with God”?  
- Where and when in my day can I consistently find a “closet” for quiet communion?  
- How has the Savior been my personal Redeemer this week? Where have I felt the “song of redeeming love”?  
- How do the sacrament prayers remind me of my relationship with “God, the Eternal Father”?  
- In what ways am I seeking validation vertically from God rather than horizontally from peers or culture?  
- How can I help someone else remember their divine identity in a moment of crisis or sorrow?

## 7. Be Reconciled to God - Elder Kelly R. Johnson

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/17johnson?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/17johnson?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Kelly R. Johnson teaches that reconciliation with God—made possible through Jesus Christ’s Atonement and sought by daily repentance, covenants, revelation, and heeding living prophets—aligns our will with His and results in unshakable faith.

### Haiku

Draw near, be made whole;  
Through Christ, repent day by day—  
Faith stands unshaken.

### Key Points

1) Reconciliation means alignment with God: Like reconciling accounts, we realign our will with God’s will; we choose to draw near to Him, and reconciliation is only possible through the mercy of Jesus Christ’s Atonement.

2) Repentance brings peace and unshaken faith: Enos’s story shows that humble, focused, determined repentance removes guilt, restores peace with God and others, and yields unshaken faith in the Lord.

3) Stay firmly connected to Christ: Disobedience, doubts, and fears can weaken our link to the “stem of Jesse,” but daily repentance, prayer, covenants, revelation, and heeding prophets strengthen that connection so we are not “ripe for destruction.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Corinthians 5:20 — “Be ye reconciled to God.”
- 2 Nephi 2:27 — Agency to choose eternal life or death.
- Jacob 4:11; Jacob 4:6 — Reconciled through Christ’s Atonement; searching prophets leads to hope and unshaken faith.
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:63 — “Draw near unto me… seek, ask, knock.”
- Enos 1:4–8, 11 — Forgiveness, guilt swept away, faith becomes unshaken.
- Isaiah 11:1; D&C 113:1–2 — The “stem of Jesse” as a title for the Messiah.
- D&C 20:32–34 — Even the sanctified must “take heed and pray always.”
- Helaman 13:14; Ether 2:9 — “Ripe for destruction” and the danger of hardening the will.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Let God Prevail” (Oct 2020) — Aligning our will with God’s.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “We Can Do Better and Be Better” (Apr 2019) — Daily repentance is liberating and essential.
- Gospel principles: Daily repentance, covenant keeping, continual seeking of revelation, acting on prophetic counsel, and praying always.

### Questions to Ponder

- In what specific ways is my will out of alignment with God’s, and what would it look like for me to “let God prevail” this week?
- How can I make daily repentance more specific, prompt, and joyful so it strengthens my bond with the Savior?
- When have I felt guilt “swept away,” and how did that experience change my faith and discipleship?
- What currently weakens my connection to Jesus Christ (disobedience, doubts, fear), and what practices most strengthen it (scripture, prayer, covenants, service)?
- How can I better “draw near” to the Lord—what will I ask, seek, or knock for in the coming days?
- Which prophetic invitations have I not yet acted on, and how might acting now increase my hope and make my faith more unshaken?
- How do my covenants practically anchor me during life’s “shaking,” and what covenant promises am I relying on right now?

## 8. Do Your Part with All Your Heart - Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/16uchtdorf?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/16uchtdorf?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf teaches that discipleship requires steady, disciplined effort to use and magnify God-given gifts while trusting Jesus Christ to perfect us—one faithful step at a time.

### Haiku

Daily, hearts align  
Use gifts, nourish living faith—  
Christ perfects our steps.

### Key Points

1) Discipleship takes discipline  
   Faith is a daily, deliberate choice that must be nourished through action; like the parable of the talents, unused gifts and testimonies diminish, while exercised faith grows.

2) You are gifted  
   Every person receives spiritual gifts—often quiet and simple—that matter profoundly to the Lord’s work; recognize and use them to bless others.

3) Do your little part—consistently  
   Growth in Christ is gradual but relentless; turn from Babylon to Zion, act today, and trust the Savior’s grace to refine and ultimately perfect you in Him.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 25:14–30 — Parable of the talents: magnify what God entrusts to you.
- Doctrine and Covenants 46:11–12 — “To every person is given a gift by the Spirit of God.”
- Moroni 10:8, 32 — “Deny not the gifts of God” and be “perfected in Christ.”
- Alma 37:6–7 — “By small and simple things are great things brought to pass.”
- Alma 32:27–43 — Nourishing the word/faith so it grows.
- Ether 12:4 — Hope in Christ as an anchor.
- Doctrine and Covenants 4:2 — Serve with all your “heart, might, mind and strength.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 133:5, 14 — “Go ye out from Babylon” toward Zion.
- Gospel principles: Daily discipleship, spiritual gifts, incremental sanctification, grace in Christ, covenant loyalty.

### Questions to Ponder

- What daily practices best nourish my faith so it does not “cool” with time?  
- Which spiritual gifts has the Lord already given me, including simple or quiet ones, and how am I using them?  
- Where have I “buried” a talent out of fear or comparison, and what is one small act I can take to put it to work?  
- How can I better turn from Babylon (distractions, pride, worldliness) and set my heart toward Zion this week?  
- When my faith feels small, what does “begin with a hope in Christ” look like in action?  
- How can I balance diligent effort with trusting the Savior’s grace rather than perfectionism or discouragement?  
- Who in my family, quorum/class, or neighborhood could be blessed by my specific gifts this week?  
- What consistent, simple habit (scripture study, prayer, service, temple or family history, ministering) will I recommit to today?

## 9. The Family Proclamation—Words from God - Elder Ronald A. Rasband

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/21rasband?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/21rasband?lang=eng)

### Summary

Elder Rasband testifies that The Family: A Proclamation to the World is revelation from God, reaffirms its doctrinal authority and timeless principles, and invites all—especially those who feel they don’t fit—to turn to Jesus Christ, live as equal partners in families, and find peace on the covenant path.

### Haiku

Words from God guide home  
Family, faith, equal hearts—  
Peace beyond the world.

### Key Points

1) Divine origin and authority of the Proclamation  
   - The Proclamation was prepared by Apostles under revelation, introduced by President Hinckley, and stands as doctrine that “the family is ordained of God.”

2) Christ-centered patterns for happy families  
   - Happiness is “most likely” when family life is built on Christ’s teachings: faith, prayer, repentance, forgiveness, respect, love, compassion, work, and wholesome recreation; parents have sacred duties, and fathers and mothers serve as equal partners.

3) A compassionate invitation to all God’s children  
   - For those who feel they don’t fit or struggle with the Proclamation, Elder Rasband urges trust in Heavenly Parents and the Savior’s atoning love; stay with the Church, seek the Spirit, and receive the Lord’s peace that “passeth all understanding.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 1:38 — “Whether by mine own voice or by the voice of my servants, it is the same.” Authority of prophetic declarations.  
- 1 Nephi 1:1 — “Born of goodly parents.” Parental duty to rear children in love and righteousness.  
- 1 Nephi 11:22–23 — The love of God “sheddeth itself abroad … most desirable … most joyous.” The Savior’s love heals and anchors families.  
- Philippians 4:7 — “The peace of God, which passeth all understanding.” Promise for disciples who turn to Christ.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:67 — “Eye … single to the glory of God.” Focus that brings light and stability to homes.  
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World — Core doctrines: divine identity as spirit children of Heavenly Parents; marriage between a man and a woman; sacred parental responsibilities; equal partnership; family happiness through Christlike living.  
- Gospel principles highlighted: prophetic revelation; “words matter”; covenant path; equal partnership (preside ≠ dominate; nurture ≠ secondary); do the very best you can.

### Questions to Ponder

- How does accepting the Proclamation as revealed doctrine change the way I speak about and prioritize family in my home?  
- Which of Christ’s teachings (faith, prayer, repentance, forgiveness, respect, love, compassion, work, wholesome recreation) could most bless our family this month, and what is one step we will take?  
- In what ways can husbands and wives more fully practice equal partnership while honoring distinct, complementary responsibilities?  
- How can we better minister to those who feel they “don’t fit” the Proclamation and help them feel the Savior’s love and belonging?  
- When my views conflict with shifting world standards, how will I anchor my words and choices to prophetic counsel and the covenant path?  
- What practices help our home be guided by the Spirit so we experience the Lord’s promised peace “that passeth all understanding”?

## 10. That All May Be Edified - Brother Chad H Webb

[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/22webb?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2025/10/22webb?lang=eng)

### Summary

Brother Chad H. Webb teaches that when we focus on Jesus Christ, teach true doctrine, and come as diligent learners, we invite the Holy Ghost—the perfect Tutor—to deepen faith and edify all.

### Haiku

Spirit is our guide  
Learn, teach truth, centered on Christ—  
All are edified

### Key Points

1) Invite diligent learning: Shift from teacher-centered delivery to learner-driven discipleship—prepare beforehand, seek personal revelation, share insights, and act in faith so the Spirit can confirm truth.

2) Teach true doctrine: Anchor discussions in the scriptures and the words of living prophets, avoid speculation or sensationalism, and trust that God’s word answers questions of the soul.

3) Focus on Jesus Christ: Center every lesson on the Savior—His titles, roles, attributes, and Atonement—seeing Him in all scripture and viewing life through the Father’s plan, that we may always have His Spirit.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 14:26 — The Comforter “shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 42:14 — We teach by the Spirit, “given by the prayer of faith.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 50:22; 88:122 — “That all may be edified of all”; teacher and learner rejoice together.
- John 7:16 — “My doctrine is not mine, but his that sent me.”
- 1 Nephi 17–18 — Nephi’s shipbuilding points to God’s power to strengthen and deliver.
- Alma 34:15 — “Faith unto repentance” through the atoning mercy of Christ.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79 — Sacrament prayers: that we may always have His Spirit to be with us.
- Teaching in the Savior’s Way (Church manual) — Principles that invite the Holy Ghost in learning and teaching.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives” (Apr 2018) — Increasing capacity to receive personal revelation.

### Questions to Ponder

- How will I prepare before Sunday so I come as a diligent learner ready to receive revelation?
- What questions am I bringing to my scripture study this week, and how will I record and act on the promptings I receive?
- In my teaching, how can I better root discussion in scripture and living prophets and avoid speculation?
- Where do I see Jesus Christ—His attributes, titles, and roles—in the passages I’m studying now?
- Which covenant habits help me “always have his Spirit to be with [me],” and how can I strengthen them?
- When have I been “edified” both in teaching and in receiving (D&C 50:22), and how can I foster that in my home or class?
- What principle am I currently applying so the Spirit can reconfirm its truth in my life?
- What is one specific way I can help someone else feel seen, loved, and needed in our learning setting this week?

## 11. Humble Souls at Altars Kneel - Elder Jeremy R. Jaggi

### Summary

President Oaks teaches that the gospel of Jesus Christ is family-centered and temple-anchored, calling Latter-day Saints to resist societal declines in marriage and childbearing by teaching, bonding, and serving within their families, while noting a pause in new temple announcements to focus on providing ordinances worldwide.

### Haiku

Temples bind our hearts  
Families learn Christ at home  
Eternity grows

1) Eternal families and temples: The family is central to God’s plan, and temple ordinances and sealings enable us to return to Heavenly Father as eternal families; at this conference, no new temples are being announced so the Church can prioritize providing ordinances and moving forward wisely.

2) Resist cultural headwinds with covenant focus: Despite societal declines in marriage and childbearing and diluted parental influence, Latter-day Saints should strengthen—not mirror—these trends by embracing the family proclamation, pursuing temple marriage and children where possible, and relying on the Atonement and repentance (including beyond the veil) for loved ones who struggle.

3) Build family unity through Christlike living and deliberate practices: Follow the Savior by serving one another; teach doctrine and practical life skills; turn off technology to turn on family; pray and worship together; hold family councils, share family stories, build traditions, and involve grandparents and extended family to fill gaps and bind hearts to the temple.

- Doctrine and Covenants 68:25 — Parents’ duty to teach children the doctrine of Christ.

- Doctrine and Covenants 138:57–59 — Repentance and growth continue in the spirit world.
- Doctrine and Covenants 110:13–16 — Restoration of sealing keys in the Kirtland Temple.
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World — The family is ordained of God; sacred powers of procreation belong to marriage between a man and a woman.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Salvation and Exaltation” — Salvation is an individual matter; exaltation is a family matter.
- President Spencer W. Kimball on family stories as powerful teaching tools.
- Gospel principles: covenant path; temple ordinances and sealings; parental stewardship; family councils; home-centered, Church-supported worship; Christlike service and unity.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can we encourage righteous marriage and welcoming children amid today’s cultural pressures?
