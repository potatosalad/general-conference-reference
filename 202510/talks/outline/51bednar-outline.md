### Summary

Elder Bednar teaches that because of the Father’s plan, our moral agency, and the Savior’s Atonement, the Final Judgment can be “pleasing” as we become righteous through faithful choices, covenants, repentance, and godly fear—ultimately acknowledging what we have chosen to become.

### Haiku

Choose God, love your kin,  
Act, become through Christ made new—  
Pleasing bar of God.

### Key Points

1) The Father’s plan and moral agency  
- God’s plan centers on our power to choose and act for ourselves—to love one another and to choose God. We are agents to act, not objects to be acted upon, and we are commanded to use agency morally (to “choose the right”), aligning with the two great commandments.

2) Doing and becoming  
- Final Judgment is not only a tally of deeds but an acknowledgment of what we have become through our choices and desires. Consistent righteous action shapes a Christlike nature, preparing us to stand before the Lord with confidence.

3) The Atonement and godly fear make Judgment “pleasing”  
- We are saved only through the mercy and grace of Jesus Christ as we exercise faith, make and keep covenants, repent, and endure. Godly fear (reverent awe and willing obedience) replaces worldly dread, so that in His presence we honestly recognize what we have chosen—and find the bar “pleasing” if our desires and works have been good.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 41 (esp. v. 7, 13–15): Restoration according to desires and works; “they are their own judges.”
- Moroni 10:32–34: Come unto Christ; Moroni’s “pleasing bar” language.
- 2 Nephi 2:14, 26–27: Things to act vs. things acted upon; agency and freedom to choose.
- D&C 58:26–28; 101:78: “Agents unto themselves”; moral agency and doing many good things of our own free will.
- Abraham 3:25; Moses 7:32–33: Mortal proving; God gives agency—choose Him and love one another.
- 1 Nephi 15:33; Mormon 9:14: Judgment according to what we have become.
- 2 Nephi 25:23; Article of Faith 3: Saved by the Atonement of Christ, “after all we can do,” through obedience to gospel laws and ordinances.
- Enos 1:27: “See his face with pleasure.”
- Jacob 4:13; 2 Nephi 9:14; Alma 12:14: Things as they really are; perfect knowledge and accountability at the bar of God.
- Gospel principles: Plan of Happiness; moral agency; covenants; repentance; becoming like Christ; godly fear vs. worldly fear; enduring to the end.

### Questions to Ponder

- How am I using my moral agency each day to love others and to choose God? Where could I realign my choices?
- In what ways are my habits and desires shaping what I am becoming, not just what I am doing?
- How do my covenants (baptism, sacrament, temple) help transform Judgment Day into a “pleasing” day?
- What does “godly fear” look and feel like in my discipleship compared to worldly fear?
- Where do I most need the Savior’s grace right now to repent, change, and become new?
- If I stood before the Lord today, what would my desires reveal about me? What would I hope to have different?
- How can I better act and not be acted upon amid today’s pressures, distractions, and temptations?
- What specific “good causes” will I engage in of my own free will this week to bring to pass more righteousness?
