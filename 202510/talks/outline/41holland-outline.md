### Summary

Elder Holland testifies that God manifests His truth and healing through simple, divinely appointed means—ordinances, priesthood authority, temple work for the living and the dead, and especially the Book of Mormon—which provide real evidence of the Restoration and give spiritual sight in Christ.

### Haiku

Spit and clay, plain things—  
wash, believe, the works of God;  
pages give me sight.

### Key Points

1) God works through simple means and ordinances  
- The healing of the man born blind teaches that the Lord’s “works” are made manifest through humble, even unexpected instruments; faith to act on plain instructions brings spiritual sight.

2) True authority and mercy through priesthood and temples  
- Priesthood power and authority come by the laying on of hands in an unbroken line to Jesus Christ; temples extend God’s covenants mercifully to the dead—a distinctive witness of His universal love.

3) The Book of Mormon as living evidence  
- “Translated by the gift and power of God,” the Book of Mormon has power to heal, guide, and reveal; personal experience with this book becomes compelling evidence of the Restoration and of the Savior amid opposition.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 9:1–11 — The man born blind; anointing and washing; “one thing I know… I was blind, now I see.”
- Luke 9:1–2 — Jesus gives the Twelve “power and authority.”
- 2 Kings 5:1–14 — Naaman and the Jordan: simple commandments, powerful outcomes.
- Numbers 21:4–9 — Looking to the brazen serpent: plain means, saving power.
- 1 Nephi 13:28–40 — “Plain and precious” truths restored.
- Mosiah 14:2 / Isaiah 53:2 — “No form nor comeliness”: God’s majesty often appears plain to the world.
- Title Page of the Book of Mormon — “Translated by the gift and power of God.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 138:58–60 — Redemption of the dead through vicarious ordinances.
- Gospel principles: Faith to act on simple instructions; ordinances and covenants; priesthood keys and line of authority; temple work for the living and the dead; personal revelation as evidence; the Restoration of the Church of Jesus Christ.

### Questions to Ponder

- What “spit and clay” experiences (simple, even unimpressive means) is the Lord asking me to trust and act on right now?
- How has the Book of Mormon given me spiritual sight? What specific passages have changed my life?
- In what ways do I prioritize evidence from lived discipleship and the Holy Ghost over arguments, assumptions, or cynicism?
- How do ordinances (anointing, washing, sacrament, temple worship) help the “works of God” be made manifest in me?
- Why does it matter that priesthood authority comes by laying on of hands in a line back to Christ? How does that affect my covenants?
- How can I participate more fully in temple work as a witness of God’s mercy to both the living and the dead?
- What personal evidences of the Restoration have I already received that I might be overlooking or under-valuing?
- When have I been able to say, like the healed man, “One thing I know…” and how can I strengthen that witness this week?
