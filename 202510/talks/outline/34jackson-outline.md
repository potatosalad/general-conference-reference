### Summary

Elder William K. Jackson teaches that Christlike ministering means counting and accounting by name so every soul is noticed, nourished, and led to the Savior through inspired councils, stewardships, and tools.

### Haiku

Names written with care  
Shepherds count, then go and find—  
No one left behind

### Key Points

1. The Savior’s pattern is personal. He is the Good Shepherd who knows His sheep by name and seeks the one; Moroni 6 models recording names, meeting often, and nourishing by the word so all are remembered and kept in the right way.
2. Counting and accounting are spiritual, not bureaucratic. Overlapping stewardships and inspired tools (reports, lists, councils) help us notice who is missing, who needs an ordinance or calling, and who needs love now—and then act promptly.
3. Lead with names, not programs. Effective leaders set goals tied to specific people, review rosters, counsel together, and follow up; when ministering centers on individuals, miracles happen and retention, conversion, and temple participation increase.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Moroni 6:4–5 — Numbered, remembered, nourished by the good word; meeting often and speaking of the welfare of souls.
- Luke 15:4–7 — The lost sheep; leaving the ninety and nine to find the one.
- John 10:11, 14 — Jesus Christ is the Good Shepherd who knows His sheep.
- Doctrine and Covenants 128:7–8 — Sacredness of records and accounting before heaven.
- Moses 1:39 — God’s work and glory are personal: to bring to pass immortality and eternal life.
- Gospel principles: Ministering by name; councils focused on people (not just activities); overlapping stewardships as “safety nets”; using reports and interviews to notice, remember, and act.

### Questions to Ponder

- Which one to three names will I write down, pray over, and visit this week?
- How can I use the tools available (ministering reports, Action and Interview List, temple recommend statuses, rosters) to notice who needs help right now?
- In our next council, how will we ensure our agenda prioritizes names, specific plans, and clear assignments with follow-up?
- What “safety nets” am I part of (bishopric, ministering, youth leadership, seminary, quorum/class), and how can I tighten my net for someone at risk?
- How can I make counting and accounting an expression of Christlike love rather than mere administration?
- Who in my stewardship needs a calling, an interview, a priesthood advancement, or a recommend renewal—and what is my next step?
- When someone misses sacrament meeting, what is our plan to notice within hours and lovingly reach out the same day?
