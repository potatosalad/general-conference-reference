### Summary

Elder Rasband testifies that The Family: A Proclamation to the World is revelation from God, reaffirms its doctrinal authority and timeless principles, and invites all—especially those who feel they don’t fit—to turn to Jesus Christ, live as equal partners in families, and find peace on the covenant path.

### Haiku

Words from God guide home  
Family, faith, equal hearts—  
Peace beyond the world.

### Key Points

1) Divine origin and authority of the Proclamation  
   - The Proclamation was prepared by Apostles under revelation, introduced by President Hinckley, and stands as doctrine that “the family is ordained of God.”

2) Christ-centered patterns for happy families  
   - Happiness is “most likely” when family life is built on Christ’s teachings: faith, prayer, repentance, forgiveness, respect, love, compassion, work, and wholesome recreation; parents have sacred duties, and fathers and mothers serve as equal partners.

3) A compassionate invitation to all God’s children  
   - For those who feel they don’t fit or struggle with the Proclamation, Elder Rasband urges trust in Heavenly Parents and the Savior’s atoning love; stay with the Church, seek the Spirit, and receive the Lord’s peace that “passeth all understanding.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 1:38 — “Whether by mine own voice or by the voice of my servants, it is the same.” Authority of prophetic declarations.  
- 1 Nephi 1:1 — “Born of goodly parents.” Parental duty to rear children in love and righteousness.  
- 1 Nephi 11:22–23 — The love of God “sheddeth itself abroad … most desirable … most joyous.” The Savior’s love heals and anchors families.  
- Philippians 4:7 — “The peace of God, which passeth all understanding.” Promise for disciples who turn to Christ.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:67 — “Eye … single to the glory of God.” Focus that brings light and stability to homes.  
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World — Core doctrines: divine identity as spirit children of Heavenly Parents; marriage between a man and a woman; sacred parental responsibilities; equal partnership; family happiness through Christlike living.  
- Gospel principles highlighted: prophetic revelation; “words matter”; covenant path; equal partnership (preside ≠ dominate; nurture ≠ secondary); do the very best you can.

### Questions to Ponder

- How does accepting the Proclamation as revealed doctrine change the way I speak about and prioritize family in my home?  
- Which of Christ’s teachings (faith, prayer, repentance, forgiveness, respect, love, compassion, work, wholesome recreation) could most bless our family this month, and what is one step we will take?  
- In what ways can husbands and wives more fully practice equal partnership while honoring distinct, complementary responsibilities?  
- How can we better minister to those who feel they “don’t fit” the Proclamation and help them feel the Savior’s love and belonging?  
- When my views conflict with shifting world standards, how will I anchor my words and choices to prophetic counsel and the covenant path?  
- What practices help our home be guided by the Spirit so we experience the Lord’s promised peace “that passeth all understanding”?
