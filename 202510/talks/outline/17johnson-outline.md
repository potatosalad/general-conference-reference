### Summary

Elder Kelly R. Johnson teaches that reconciliation with God—made possible through Jesus Christ’s Atonement and sought by daily repentance, covenants, revelation, and heeding living prophets—aligns our will with His and results in unshakable faith.

### Haiku

Draw near, be made whole;  
Through Christ, repent day by day—  
Faith stands unshaken.

### Key Points

1) Reconciliation means alignment with God: Like reconciling accounts, we realign our will with God’s will; we choose to draw near to Him, and reconciliation is only possible through the mercy of Jesus Christ’s Atonement.

2) Repentance brings peace and unshaken faith: Enos’s story shows that humble, focused, determined repentance removes guilt, restores peace with God and others, and yields unshaken faith in the Lord.

3) Stay firmly connected to Christ: Disobedience, doubts, and fears can weaken our link to the “stem of Jesse,” but daily repentance, prayer, covenants, revelation, and heeding prophets strengthen that connection so we are not “ripe for destruction.”

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Corinthians 5:20 — “Be ye reconciled to God.”
- 2 Nephi 2:27 — Agency to choose eternal life or death.
- Jacob 4:11; Jacob 4:6 — Reconciled through Christ’s Atonement; searching prophets leads to hope and unshaken faith.
- Doctrine and Covenants 88:63 — “Draw near unto me… seek, ask, knock.”
- Enos 1:4–8, 11 — Forgiveness, guilt swept away, faith becomes unshaken.
- Isaiah 11:1; D&C 113:1–2 — The “stem of Jesse” as a title for the Messiah.
- D&C 20:32–34 — Even the sanctified must “take heed and pray always.”
- Helaman 13:14; Ether 2:9 — “Ripe for destruction” and the danger of hardening the will.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Let God Prevail” (Oct 2020) — Aligning our will with God’s.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “We Can Do Better and Be Better” (Apr 2019) — Daily repentance is liberating and essential.
- Gospel principles: Daily repentance, covenant keeping, continual seeking of revelation, acting on prophetic counsel, and praying always.

### Questions to Ponder

- In what specific ways is my will out of alignment with God’s, and what would it look like for me to “let God prevail” this week?
- How can I make daily repentance more specific, prompt, and joyful so it strengthens my bond with the Savior?
- When have I felt guilt “swept away,” and how did that experience change my faith and discipleship?
- What currently weakens my connection to Jesus Christ (disobedience, doubts, fear), and what practices most strengthen it (scripture, prayer, covenants, service)?
- How can I better “draw near” to the Lord—what will I ask, seek, or knock for in the coming days?
- Which prophetic invitations have I not yet acted on, and how might acting now increase my hope and make my faith more unshaken?
- How do my covenants practically anchor me during life’s “shaking,” and what covenant promises am I relying on right now?
