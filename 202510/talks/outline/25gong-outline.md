### Summary

Elder Gerrit W. Gong teaches that the restored gospel establishes a covenant culture of belonging in which we actively gather, welcome, and minister so that no one sits alone—physically, emotionally, or spiritually—and all can find forgiveness and a place at the Lord’s table.

### Haiku

No one sits alone  
In His inn, hearts knit in love—  
Alike unto God

### Key Points

1. Gathered in Christ, beyond worldly culture  
   - The Lord is gathering His children from many lands and cultures (Jacob 5; parables of the feast), teaching that “God is no respecter of persons” (Acts 10). Gospel culture transcends national traditions and invites all to the Lord’s supper.

2. “Room in the inn”: No one sits alone  
   - The Church, as the Savior’s inn (Good Samaritan), is where we come to Him and to each other—welcoming, sitting with, and ministering so no one is left out or wounded unattended. Small acts (a first hello, choosing to sit with someone) build Zion and heal hearts.

3. Covenant belonging and conversion  
   - In Christ, all are alike unto God; covenant belonging asks us to relinquish customs contrary to God’s commandments and live gospel culture (chastity, Word of Wisdom, integrity, humble service). Through repentance and the Savior’s atonement, real forgiveness and hope are possible for everyone.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Jacob 5 (allegory of the olive tree; gathering and grafting of diverse branches)
- Luke 14:16–24; Matthew 22:1–14 (great supper and wedding feast—inviting all to come)
- Acts 10:34–35 (God is no respecter of persons; in every nation those who fear Him are accepted)
- Luke 10:25–37 (Good Samaritan; the Church as the “inn” where the wounded are cared for)
- Mosiah 18:21 (knit hearts together in unity and love)
- 2 Nephi 26:24, 33 (He doeth all things for our benefit; all are alike unto God)
- 1 Nephi 19:9 (the Savior “suffereth it”—His long-suffering toward our weakness)
- Doctrine and Covenants 58:8–11; 65:2 (the supper of the Lord; prepare the way for His kingdom)
- Matthew 25:40, 45 (how we treat “the least of these” is how we treat the Savior)
- Gospel principles: covenant belonging; the gathering of Israel; gospel culture vs. worldly traditions; ministering and inclusion; repentance, forgiveness, and hope in Christ; Word of Wisdom and law of chastity; humility in callings (service, not status).

### Questions to Ponder

- Who might be “sitting alone” (physically, emotionally, or spiritually) in my ward or class this week, and how will I sit with them?
- What traditions, habits, or assumptions from my culture should I lay aside to fully live gospel culture?
- How can my small “first hello” gestures help build Zion where hearts are knit together in love?
- When I have been hurt—or when I have hurt others—how can I come to Christ’s “inn” to seek healing, reconciliation, and forgiveness?
- In what ways can my ward better reflect our community and the Lord’s invitation to “bring in” all who will come?
- How does my identity as a child of God, child of the covenant, and disciple of Jesus Christ change how I see those who are different from me?
- What does “no respecter of persons” look like in my ministering routes, seating choices, and Sunday conversations?
- Where do I personally need the Savior’s mercy right now, and what is my next step toward Him (prayer, bishop, ordinance, restitution)?
