### Summary

Elder Kevin G. Brown teaches that God lovingly gives spiritual gifts, agency empowers us to choose Christ repeatedly, and a sure testimony—received through the Holy Ghost—must be consciously chosen, nurtured, and defended throughout life.

### Haiku

Father gives good gifts,  
Choose Christ—Spirit makes truth sure,  
Seeds rise into light.

### Key Points

1. God is our loving Heavenly Father  
   - He knows our past, present, and eternal destiny and delights to bless us; “think celestial” by trusting His eternal perspective and gifts.

2. Agency is a sacred stewardship  
   - Bought by the blood of Christ, agency is the power and responsibility to choose good, to choose Christ, and to choose eternal life—again and again.

3. Testimony comes by the Holy Ghost and endures  
   - The Spirit’s witness can be sudden, gradual, or intermittent; we must choose to believe, “take charge” of our testimony, and multiply it as a lifelong, eternal gift.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Agency and accountability: 2 Nephi 2:26–27; Doctrine and Covenants 58:26–28
- The Holy Ghost as the preeminent witness: John 15:26; 2 Nephi 32:5
- “What greater witness can you have than from God?”: Doctrine and Covenants 6:23
- The sacrament promise to always have His Spirit: Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79
- Growing a seed into sure knowledge: Alma 32:27–43
- Pray and gain a witness of the Book of Mormon: Moroni 10:3–5; James 1:5 (pattern seen in the First Vision)
- Stewardship of spiritual gifts (including testimony): Matthew 25:14–30 (parable of the talents)
- Living water, enduring testimony: John 4:14
- “I knew it… and I could not deny it”: Joseph Smith—History 1:25
- President Russell M. Nelson: “Think Celestial!” (Oct 2023); “Overcome the World and Find Rest” (Oct 2022—“take charge of your testimony”); “Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives” (Apr 2018—need for constant influence of the Holy Ghost)

### Questions to Ponder

- In what specific ways can I “take charge” of my testimony this week—work for it, own it, and feed it truth?
- How am I using my agency daily to choose Christ and eternal life, especially in small, repeated decisions?
- When have I felt the Holy Ghost’s witness (sudden, gradual, or in rays), and how can I invite that influence more consistently?
- What practices help me honor the sacrament promise to always have His Spirit to be with me?
- How can I treat my testimony as a sacred stewardship to multiply rather than something to bury or neglect?
- What “Sacred Grove” places, times, or habits can I create to hear the Lord more clearly?
- How will I respond in faith and charity when others question my beliefs or challenge my commitment?
- Whom can I lovingly invite (as that sister missionary did for Elder Brown) to read, pray, and seek a witness from God?
