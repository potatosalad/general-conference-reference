### Summary

Elder Steven C. Barlow teaches that God recognizes our love when we put Him first, love and minister to His children, live with gratitude, and choose willing, affectionate obedience—by doing so, we also more clearly perceive His love for us.

### Haiku

Lovest thou Me more?  
Feed My sheep, thank God, obey—  
Then feel His love more.

### Key Points

1) Put the Lord first above “these”
- The Savior’s question to Peter invites us to prioritize God over anything that competes for our hearts, time, or loyalty; discipleship means letting His voice and will prevail daily.

2) Love God by loving His children
- “Feed my sheep” means seeing others as God sees them and ministering without judgment—listening, lifting, and serving are direct expressions of love for the Lord.

3) Grateful, willing obedience
- We show love by confessing His hand in all things and keeping His commandments with “affectionate obedience,” choosing to follow Him because we love Him, not from compulsion.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 21:15–17 — “Lovest thou me?... Feed my sheep.”
- Matthew 22:37–39 — The great commandments: love God and neighbor.
- Luke 15:11–32 — Prodigal son: avoid elder-brother pride; rejoice in repentance.
- Doctrine and Covenants 59:21 — “Confess his hand in all things.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 76:94 — “They see as they are seen, and know as they are known.”
- 2 Nephi 31:3 — God speaks to us according to our understanding.
- John 3:16; 1 John 4:9–10 — The Atoning Son as the supreme manifestation of the Father’s love.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Let God Prevail” — Will we let God be the most important influence?
- Sister Tamara W. Runia — “Affectionate obedience.”
- Elder Dale G. Renlund — God’s goal is that we choose to do what is right, becoming like Him.

### Questions to Ponder

- What are the “these” in my life that compete with loving God first? What one change would most clearly put Him first this week?
- How does God uniquely communicate His love to me? How can I notice it more readily?
- Whom can I “feed” this week through listening, encouraging, or quiet service?
- Do those I love recognize my love in the ways I’m expressing it? How can I better express love in the ways they receive it?
- How can I more consistently “confess His hand” in daily prayers and conversations?
- Which commandment or covenant could I keep more “affectionately,” out of love rather than routine?
- How does focusing on showing love for God help me better recognize His love for me?
