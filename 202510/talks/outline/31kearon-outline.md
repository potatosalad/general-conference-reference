### Summary

Elder Kearon teaches that because of Jesus Christ’s infinite goodness, Atonement, and Resurrection, every one of us can receive as many new beginnings as we need through repentance, covenants, and His enabling grace.

### Haiku

Jesus doing good  
New beginnings bloom in grace—  
Turn, and live in Him.

### Key Points

1) Jesus Christ is the source of all goodness and new beginnings  
   His mortal ministry—healing, forgiving, teaching—shows He offers fresh starts to every person, regardless of status or spiritual starting point.

2) The covenant path enables continual renewal  
   Baptism, the gift of the Holy Ghost, and the sacrament allow us to be “born again,” walk “in newness of life,” receive a “new heart,” and become a “new creature” through His grace—again and again.

3) Repentance is joyful, daily progress  
   As taught by President Nelson, repentance is not punishment but a hopeful, ongoing gift; because of Christ’s Atonement and Resurrection, there is always another chance—press forward, leave deliberate sin, and keep turning to Him.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Acts 10:38; John 21:25 — Jesus “went about doing good”; His works exceed all record.  
- John 3:3–5; Romans 6:4 — Born again; walk in newness of life (baptism and the Holy Ghost).  
- 2 Corinthians 5:17; Ezekiel 36:26 — Become a new creature; receive a new heart and a new spirit.  
- Mark 2:5; Mark 1:41; Mark 5:41; Luke 8:48; John 8:11 — The Savior’s life-giving words that signal new beginnings (forgiveness, healing, rising).  
- John 8:29; Mosiah 15:7 — He always did the Father’s will and “suffered the will of the Father in all things.”  
- John 19:30; Hebrews 12:2; Psalm 23:6 — “It is finished”; Jesus is the Author and Finisher of our faith; His goodness and mercy follow us.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “We Can Do Better and Be Better” (Apr 2019) — The joy and power of daily repentance.  
- Gospel principles: Grace as enabling power; sacrament as weekly renewal; faithful endurance amid setbacks; the Church as a place of fresh starts.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where do I most need a new beginning right now, and what is the next faithful step I can take today?  
- How can I approach the sacrament each week as a true fresh start with Christ?  
- What changes in mindset or habit (not just sin) is the Lord inviting me to make through His grace?  
- Do I view repentance as joyful daily progress? If not, what belief about God’s character needs to change?  
- Which words of Jesus (“Be thou clean,” “Go, and sin no more,” etc.) do I most need to hear and act on this week?  
- How will I more intentionally “make Him my King” in my thoughts, choices, and relationships?  
- When I face repeated setbacks, how will I keep pressing forward without excusing deliberate sin?  
- Who around me needs encouragement to believe in their new beginning, and how can I minister like the Savior?
