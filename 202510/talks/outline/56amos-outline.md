### Summary

Elder John D. Amos teaches that the “Good News Recipe” for happy living is found in the scriptures, with Jesus Christ as the essential “secret ingredient,” and invites us to add more of Him through charity, small and simple actions, and personal revelation.

### Haiku

God’s recipes guide  
Add the Savior, heart by heart—  
Love endures all things.

### Key Points

1. God’s recipe for happiness: The scriptures are “God’s recipes for happy living,” and when life’s efforts fall short, the answer—and the essential “secret ingredient”—is always Jesus Christ.
2. Charity in action, one small step at a time: Applying Moroni 7:45 to relationships, starting “where you are” (the slope), and doing small and simple things invites the Lord’s power to heal, improve, and transform.
3. Hear Him through revelation: President Nelson’s pattern—seek quiet, humbly pray in Christ’s name, listen, record, and act—helps us find answers in the Savior’s life and teachings and grow into the principle of revelation.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Moroni 7:45 — Attributes of charity that transform relationships.
- Alma 37:6–7 — “By small and simple things are great things brought to pass.”
- 1 Corinthians 13 (esp. v. 8) — “Charity never faileth.”
- President Russell M. Nelson, Restoration Conversations video: “Scriptures Are God’s Recipes for Happy Living.”
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives” (Apr 2018) — Steps to “hear Him” and grow into revelation.
- Elder Clark G. Gilbert, “The Parable of the Slope” (Oct 2021) — Start where you are; rise with the Lord.
- Gospel principles: The Atonement of Jesus Christ, personal revelation, covenant living, daily discipleship, repentance and course correction, ministering with charity.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where in my life do I need to “add more of Christ” as the essential ingredient?
- Which attribute from Moroni 7:45 would most bless one relationship this week?
- What two “small and simple” actions will I take today to be kinder and more patient?
- How does the “parable of the slope” change the way I view my current starting point?
- When and where will I consistently seek quiet, listen, write promptings, and act?
- What specific question will I bring to the Savior in prayer this week?
- How can the scriptures become my daily “recipe” rather than an occasional ingredient?
- In what ways have I seen “charity never faileth” in my home, companionship, or ward?
