### Summary

Elder Quentin L. Cook testifies that the Lord is hastening His work worldwide and urges all Saints to lovingly welcome, nurture, and involve new and returning members as we unite in Zion and progress on the covenant path centered in temple ordinances and daily discipleship.

### Haiku

The Lord hastens work  
Hearts gather into Zion  
Covenants light paths

### Key Points

1. The Lord is hastening His work now
   - Despite turbulent times, the gospel is spreading across the earth; resources, priesthood keys, and temples make saving ordinances available worldwide, and many are entering the covenant path.

2. Welcoming and retaining converts is a sacred duty
   - Members are to create Zion by being of one heart and one mind, ensuring no one sits alone emotionally or spiritually; every convert needs a friend, a responsibility, and nourishing with the good word of God.

3. Counsel to new and returning members: focus on covenants and Christ
   - Be patient and prioritize receiving and keeping ordinances and covenants (especially temple covenants); study the Book of Mormon daily, learn the doctrine over time, pursue eternal marriage when possible, repent continually through the Atonement, and endure to the end.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I personally ensure that no one in my ward or class sits alone—emotionally or spiritually—this month?
- Which “friend, responsibility, and nourishing” actions can our quorum/class take to help a recent convert feel they truly belong?
- What is my next step on the covenant path, and how will I prepare to receive temple ordinances or renew my temple worship?
- In what ways can I be a peacemaker in a world “in commotion” and thereby foster unity in Zion?
- How can daily Book of Mormon study open heaven’s guidance for me right now, as promised by President Nelson?
- What adjustments can our ward make to better welcome and retain the growing number of converts the Lord is sending?
- How does remembering that the Savior focused on perfecting the Saints—rather than the politics of His day—reframe my discipleship?
