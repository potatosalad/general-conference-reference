### Summary

Elder Michael Cziesla teaches that the doctrine of Christ is beautifully simple—lived through small and simple, covenant-centered acts that focus us on Jesus Christ, bring daily joy, and, over time, unlock promised blessings.

### Haiku

Simple, holy steps,
Faith, repent, covenant path—
Small things make souls strong.

### Key Points

1) The simplicity that is in Christ  
   - The gospel is plain and precious: exercise faith in Jesus Christ, repent, be baptized, receive the Holy Ghost, and endure to the end; simplicity does not mean ease—discipleship requires steady effort, childlike trust, and continual change.

2) Small and simple practices carry great power  
   - Prayer, fasting, scripture study, daily repentance, the sacrament, and temple worship “work wonderfully,” bringing peace, guidance, and strength; prioritize people over processes, focus on what you can do, and remember that out of small things proceed great things.

3) A living example: Oma Cziesla  
   - In isolation and hardship, Oma kept covenants through daily, simple devotion (praying, reading, singing), which prepared the way for her family’s conversion years later—reminding us that blessings may come soon, late, or in heaven, but in Christ, they come.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- 2 Corinthians 11:3 — “the simplicity that is in Christ.”
- 1 Nephi 13 (plain and precious truths)  
- 2 Nephi 31–32; 3 Nephi 11:31–41 — the doctrine of Christ.
- Mosiah 3:19 — “put off the natural man” and become as a child.
- Proverbs 3:5–6 — trust in the Lord with all thine heart.
- 2 Kings 5:13 — Naaman and “do some great thing”: the power of simple obedience.
- Alma 37:6–7 — “by small and simple things.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 64:33 — “be not weary in well-doing... out of small things proceedeth that which is great.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 68:28 — teach children to pray and walk uprightly.
- Matthew 22:36–40; John 13:34 — love God and love neighbor.
- Titles of Christ to anchor our focus: Prince of Peace (Isaiah 9:6), Good Shepherd (John 10), Rock and Refuge (Psalm 18:2).
- President Russell M. Nelson: Joy independent of circumstance (“Joy and Spiritual Survival,” Oct 2016); “Pure truth, pure doctrine, and pure revelation” (Oct 2021); the covenant path.
- Elder Jeffrey R. Holland: “Some blessings come soon... but they come” (“An High Priest of Good Things to Come,” Oct 1999).
- Elder M. Russell Ballard: counsel to find peace and happiness in gospel simplicity.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where has complexity crept into my discipleship, and how can I realign with “the simplicity that is in Christ” this week?
- What one “small and simple” practice will I do daily to deepen faith in Jesus Christ?
- How can I better prioritize people over processes in my ministering, calling, or home?
- In what decisions can I ask, “What would the Lord Jesus Christ have me do?” and act on the answer?
- What can I do (rather than lament what I cannot) to move forward on the covenant path?
- How can I teach or model simple, home-centered discipleship—especially to children or new members?
- Where might steady spiritual practices combine with needed professional help to bring healing and hope?
- Which title of Christ (Prince of Peace, Good Shepherd, Rock and Refuge) do I need to hold to right now, and why?
