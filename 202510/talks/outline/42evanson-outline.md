### Summary

Elder James E. Evanson teaches that Christlike service—modeled powerfully by service missionaries—opens hearts to the gospel, enables everyone to minister regardless of circumstance, and brings the Savior’s healing power into our lives, inviting us to “go, and do thou likewise.”

### Haiku

Hands that lift the weak—  
Open hearts, heal weary eyes;  
Go and do likewise.

### Key Points

1) Service opens hearts to the gospel  
- Like Ammon with King Lamoni and Sister Bevan’s inspired help in a park, sincere service invites the Spirit, builds trust, and creates natural opportunities to share the message of Jesus Christ.

2) Service enables all to minister, whatever our circumstances  
- Service missions expand what “able” means; whether or not we wear a name tag, every disciple can bless others and present a “living sacrifice” to God through consistent ministering.

3) Service brings the power of Christ into our lives  
- Consecrated service draws the Savior’s healing into our homes and hearts; as we lift others, He lifts us, increasing joy, temple worship, and spiritual focus.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Mark 8:22–25 — The Savior’s compassionate “second touch” that brings clear sight.  
- Matthew 20:28 — Jesus “came not to be ministered unto, but to minister.”  
- Matthew 5:41–42 — Going the extra mile and giving to those who ask.  
- Luke 10:25–37 — The good Samaritan: “Go, and do thou likewise.”  
- Alma 17–19; 26:22 — Ammon’s service opens a nation’s heart to the gospel.  
- 1 Peter 3:15 — Be ready to share “a reason of the hope” within you.  
- Romans 12:1 — Present your bodies a “living sacrifice” through devoted service.  
- Mosiah 2:17 — When we serve others, we serve God.  
- Matthew 25:40 — “Inasmuch as ye have done it unto one of the least of these…”  
- Acts 10:38 — Christ “went about doing good.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson, teachings on missionary service and gathering Israel (e.g., Hope of Israel, 2018): Anytime we help someone make and keep covenants, we help gather Israel.

### Questions to Ponder

- Who is my “neighbor” this week, and what simple act of service can I offer today?  
- When have I seen a heart open to the gospel because someone first felt loved and served?  
- How is the Lord expanding my definition of “able” in ministering and missionary work?  
- What promptings—like Sister Bevan’s—am I receiving to return and bless someone I hesitated to approach?  
- How can my family more fully celebrate, support, or emulate service missionaries in our area?  
- In what ways is the Savior inviting me to be a “living sacrifice” through quiet, consistent service?  
- Where do I need the Savior’s “second touch,” and how might service invite His healing power into my life?
