### Summary

Elder Soares teaches that temperance—a Christlike, Spirit-given self-mastery—reinforces our spiritual foundation in Jesus Christ, harmonizes other virtues, and protects us from the erosion of anger, pride, and excess.

### Haiku

Gentle, bridled will—
tempered words, patient hearts stand
firm on Christ, our rock.

### Key Points

1. Temperance fortifies our foundation in Christ
   - Like strengthening the Salt Lake Temple’s base, temperance prevents spiritual erosion and stabilizes faith, testimony, and understanding.

2. What temperance looks like in discipleship
   - It refines desires, words, and actions; rejects being “selfwilled,” “soon angry,” or a “striker”; and grows alongside humility, faith, hope, and charity.

3. Christ’s perfect example and covenant power
   - Jesus modeled temperance amid suffering; as we follow Him and keep covenants, His grace enables patient, peaceable responses—especially in trials.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Doctrine and Covenants 12:8 — “Being temperate in all things”
- Philippians 4:13 — “I can do all things through Christ”
- Titus 1:7–8 — Qualifications emphasizing temperance (not selfwilled, not soon angry)
- 1 Samuel 2:3 — Hannah’s counsel against pride; the Lord weighs actions
- 1 Peter 2:23 — The Savior “reviled not again”
- Doctrine and Covenants 19:16–19 — “Glory be to the Father… I partook”
- 3 Nephi 11:29; 12:23–24 — Reject contention; reconcile with others
- Isaiah 54:10 — Covenant of peace not removed
- Related talk: President Russell M. Nelson, “The Temple and Your Spiritual Foundation” (Oct. 2021)
- Gospel principles: Temperance (self-mastery), meekness, humility, charity, covenant keeping, peacemaking, relying on the Holy Ghost

### Questions to Ponder

- Where do I notice “spiritual erosion” in my life, and what temperate practices could reinforce my foundation in Christ?
- What situations most often trigger impatience or anger in me, and how will I invite the Spirit to moderate my words and actions?
- In what ways might I be “selfwilled,” and how can I yield more fully to God’s will with meekness?
- Whom do I need to reconcile with, and what is a first, humble step I can take this week?
- How does keeping my covenants empower me to respond with restraint and compassion during conflict?
- What daily habits (prayer, scripture study, Sabbath worship, service) best cultivate temperance in my home and online?
- How can I let Christ’s example in suffering without retaliation shape my reactions in small, everyday trials?
