### Summary

Elder Carlos A. Godoy testifies that Saints in Africa exemplify Christ-centered joy and gratitude amid real hardship, showing that as we focus on Jesus Christ and His Atonement, He succors us and makes our burdens light.

### Haiku

Smiles in storm-worn roads,  
Hearts grateful, yoked with Jesus—  
Light burdens, bright hope.

### Key Points

1. Joy independent of circumstance: When our focus is on God’s plan and Jesus Christ, we can feel joy regardless of what is happening, responding to trials with gratitude and faith.
2. Faithful examples across Africa: From long walks to crowded, humble meeting places and rapid growth, members serve and worship without complaint—showing resilience, consecration, and gratitude; Church self-reliance and education efforts help transform lives.
3. The Savior’s perfect succor: Because Christ bore our pains and infirmities, He knows how to help; as we come unto Him and take His yoke, we find rest and strength in every season.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 7:11–12 — The Savior suffers our pains and knows how to succor His people.
- Matthew 11:28–30 — “Come unto me… my yoke is easy, and my burden is light.”
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Joy and Spiritual Survival” (Oct. 2016) — Joy comes from our focus on Christ, not circumstances.
- Elder David A. Bednar’s teachings on the enabling power of the Atonement — The Lord perfectly understands and strengthens us.
- Principle: Gratitude and covenant living (including tithing, service, and learning) invite Christ’s power and build spiritual resilience.
- President James E. Faust’s observation: The gospel helps ordinary people become great through discipleship.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where is my focus right now—on my circumstances or on Jesus Christ and His gospel?
- How can I cultivate visible gratitude in the middle of my current challenges?
- What does taking Christ’s yoke look like for me this week in practical, simple steps?
- Which covenant practices (tithing, ministering, Sabbath worship, service) can I deepen to access the Savior’s succor?
- How can my family or class emulate the African Saints’ cheerful consecration and willingness to serve?
- What learning or self-reliance step (education, skill-building, English, budgeting) could I pursue with faith to invite the Lord’s help?
