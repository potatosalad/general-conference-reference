### Summary

Elder B. Corey Cuvelier teaches that to be called by the name of Christ means centering our identity in Him by making and keeping covenants, remembering Him, standing as His witnesses, and quietly doing good that blesses generations.

### Haiku

Name written on hearts—  
Covenants, witness, and love;  
Fruit from simple good.

### Key Points

1. Take upon you the name of Christ
   - Salvation comes only in and through Christ; disciples remember Him, keep His commandments, and “retain His name written always” in their hearts, above all worldly labels.

2. Discipleship is covenantal and lifelong
   - Through faith, repentance, baptism, and the Holy Ghost, we become His people and witnesses; growth comes “line upon line” as we follow prophets and prioritize Christ amid competing voices.

3. Quiet service reveals true identity
   - Small, Christlike acts—especially toward those “in the church or out of the church”—can change lives and posterity; love of God moves disciples to bless the whole human family.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Mosiah 3:17; Mosiah 5:7–12 — No other name; take and retain the name of Christ.  
- Mosiah 18:8–10 — Willing to stand as witnesses of God at all times and in all things.  
- Acts 2:38; Acts 11:26 — Repentance, baptism, remission of sins; “called Christians.”  
- 2 Nephi 2:6 — Redemption comes in and through the Holy Messiah.  
- 2 Nephi 28:30 — Growth “line upon line, precept upon precept.”  
- Alma 1:30 — Do not send any away; be liberal to all, in and out of the Church.  
- John 1:41 — “We have found the Messias.”  
- Isaiah 43:1 — “I have called thee by thy name; thou art mine.”  
- President Russell M. Nelson — Our divine identities: children of God, children of the covenant, disciples of Jesus Christ.  
- Joseph Smith — Discipleship isn’t achieved in a moment; love of God seeks to bless the whole human race.

### Questions to Ponder

- Which names or labels shape my choices most—worldly titles or my identity in Christ?  
- How can I better “retain His name written always” in my heart this week?  
- What will I do to live my baptismal and sacrament covenants more intentionally each day?  
- In what ways can I stand with living prophets and apostles to share Christ’s message?  
- Who near me is hungry, thirsty, sick, lonely, or searching—inside or outside the Church—and how will I not “send them away”?  
- What simple act of Christlike service can I offer this week that might plant a seed for generations?  
- How do I hear and recognize the voice by which the Savior calls me?
