### Summary

Elder Jeremy R. Jaggi teaches that sacrament tables and temple altars point us to Jesus Christ’s Atonement; as we humbly make and keep covenants, we are bound to Him and receive His mercy, protection, sanctification, healing, and rest.

### Haiku

Altar light invites—  
broken hearts to meet His grace,  
rest in covenant.

### Key Points

1) Altars point to Christ and His Atonement  
- From Adam and Eve to Abraham and Lehi, scriptural altars teach that sacrifice is a similitude of the Only Begotten; today the sacrament table and temple altars symbolize Christ’s infinite sacrifice and invite us to approach Him with reconciled hearts.

2) Covenants bind us to the Savior and protect us  
- Regular worship and partaking of the sacrament, receiving temple ordinances, and honoring the endowment’s laws form a reciprocal relationship with the Lord, granting power to resist the adversary, spiritual protection, and increased capacity through His strengthening grace.

3) Kneeling brings reflection, sanctification, healing, and rest  
- Humble “check-ins” with God—at home, at the sacrament table, and in the temple—help us yield our will, retain a remission of sins through the Holy Ghost, lay our burdens on His altar, be healed through His bitter cup and cross, and find the soul-deep rest He promises.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Adam and Eve’s sacrifices taught as a similitude of Christ  
  - Moses 5:5–8  
- Reconcile before the altar  
  - Matthew 5:23–24  
- Abraham’s altar and “Here am I” faith  
  - Genesis 22:1–14  
- Lehi’s gratitude at the altar  
  - 1 Nephi 2:7; 5:9  
- We have an altar; sanctified through Jesus’ blood  
  - Hebrews 13:10–12  
- Sacrament covenants and retaining a remission of sins  
  - Moroni 4–5; 3 Nephi 18:1–12; 3 Nephi 27:20  
- Healing and returning to Christ  
  - 3 Nephi 9:13; 2 Nephi 4:21  
- The Savior’s “bitter cup” and suffering  
  - Doctrine and Covenants 19:18; Luke 22:41–44  
- Rest in Christ  
  - Matthew 11:28–30; Matthew 8:20 (cf. Luke 9:58)

### Questions to Ponder

- What does the altar (sacrament table or temple altar) help me remember about Jesus Christ personally?  
- Is there anyone I need to reconcile with before I approach the altar this week?  
- How can I better “check in” with God daily so the sacrament becomes a true renewal and not a routine?  
- Which covenant promises am I actively keeping, and how have I felt the Savior’s protecting and strengthening power as I do?  
- What specific burden, sin, or sorrow will I lay on the Lord’s altar to seek His healing?  
- How can I help a friend or family member experience the sacrament or prepare for the temple sooner, so they can access Christ’s mercy and rest?  
- Where do I feel “separated” by the adversary, and what worship habits will reconnect me to the Savior?
