### Summary

Elder Dale G. Renlund teaches that taking upon ourselves the name of Jesus Christ means identifying with Him, remembering Him, becoming like Him, aligning with His work, and thereby receiving empowering covenant blessings.

### Haiku

Bear His holy name  
Remember; become like Him—  
Power flows to serve.

### Key Points

1) Identify and remember Jesus Christ  
- Through baptism and the sacrament we link our name to His and promise to always remember Him; this steady remembrance shapes our thoughts, affections, and daily doings.

2) Emulate and align with Him  
- As disciples we seek Christlike attributes—especially charity—and intentionally join His work by loving, sharing, inviting, and ministering, with special care for the vulnerable.

3) Be empowered through covenants  
- Keeping covenants increases our access to the Savior’s power, bringing courage, peace, angelic help, a retained remission of sins, and greater capacity to share His gospel and endure trials.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Mosiah 5:7–12 — Taking upon us the name of Christ and retaining it.  
- D&C 20:77, 79 — Sacrament covenants to take His name and always remember Him.  
- Alma 37:36–37 — Let all your doings, thoughts, and affections be unto the Lord.  
- Moroni 7:47–48 — Charity, the pure love of Christ, as the goal of discipleship.  
- D&C 93:19 — Come unto the Father in Christ’s name and receive of His fulness.  
- D&C 84:88; Psalm 91:11 — The Lord’s glory round about us; angels have charge over us.  
- Mosiah 4:12, 26 — Retaining a remission of sins and caring for the needy.  
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Overcome the World and Find Rest,” Oct. 2022 — Increased access to the power of Jesus Christ through kept covenants.  
- Gospel principles: Covenant belonging; grace of Jesus Christ; ministering; spiritual gifts (integrity, patience, diligence); love, share, invite.

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it mean for me, personally and publicly, to “link my name” with Jesus Christ?  
- How will I use the sacrament this week to remember Him throughout each day?  
- Which Christlike attribute is the Spirit inviting me to cultivate now, and what will I do about it?  
- Where can I realign my plans with His work of loving, sharing, inviting, and ministering? Who specifically needs my care?  
- When I face fatigue, stress, or frustration, what would a Christlike “ethos” look like in my home, quorum/class, or workplace?  
- How have I felt increased access to His power as I have kept covenants? What next covenant step is the Lord inviting me to take?  
- What judgments or habits should I lay aside so charity can become more of my nature?  
- Who will I invite this week to come closer to Christ—through service, testimony, or an invitation to worship?
