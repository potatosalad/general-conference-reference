### Summary

Elder D. Todd Christofferson teaches that true healing, guidance, and flourishing—now and eternally—come as we look to God by turning to Jesus Christ, keeping covenants, heeding prophetic counsel, and making Him our highest priority.

### Haiku

Lifted serpent shines—  
Look to Christ and you will live;  
His light leads us home.

### Key Points

1) Look to Christ and live  
   - Scriptural types (the Liahona and the brass serpent) show that healing and direction come through simple, steadfast looking to the Savior. Turning to God in prayer, scripture, and sacred music brings real comfort amid tragedy and unanswered questions.

2) Prosper through covenant faithfulness  
   - The Book of Mormon promise—keep the commandments and “prosper in the land”—means enjoying the Lord’s presence, guidance, resilience, and capacity to bless others; the opposite is being cut off from His Spirit. Repentance and renewed covenants restore the gift of the Holy Ghost.

3) How to look to God today  
   - Actively keep commandments, pray continually, counsel with the Lord, and give thanks. Follow the scriptures, living prophets, the Family Proclamation, and For the Strength of Youth—especially honoring the law of chastity and the sacredness of the body. Make God not one priority but the highest priority in every decision.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 37:44–47 — Liahona as a type; “look to God and live.”  
- Numbers 21:8–9; Alma 33:19–22; Helaman 8:14–15 — Brass serpent as a symbol of Christ.  
- Hebrews 12:2 — Jesus Christ, “the author and finisher of our faith.”  
- Alma 7:11–13 — Christ succors us in every pain, worry, and burden.  
- 1 Nephi 2:20; 2 Nephi 1:20 — “Inasmuch as ye shall keep my commandments ye shall prosper.”  
- D&C 6:36 — “Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not.”  
- Isaiah 45:22 — “Look unto me, and be ye saved.”  
- 3 Nephi 15:9 — “Look unto me, and endure to the end, and ye shall live.”  
- Jacob 4:14; Ephesians 4:14 — Don’t look beyond the mark nor be tossed by every wind of doctrine.  
- Isaiah 1:16–18; D&C 58:42–43 — Repentance and complete forgiveness through Christ.  
- The Family: A Proclamation to the World — Divine design of the family and moral standards.  
- For the Strength of Youth — God’s standards; “Your Body Is Sacred,” law of chastity.  
- Gospel principles: Gift of the Holy Ghost and the Light of Christ; covenant obedience; chastity; gratitude; enduring to the end; making God our highest priority.

### Questions to Ponder

- Where am I “looking” first for comfort, answers, and identity—social voices or the Savior?  
- What does “prosper” mean in my discipleship, and how have I felt the Lord’s presence as I keep covenants?  
- In what decisions this week can I consciously “look unto [Christ] in every thought” before acting?  
- How can I better honor my body and the law of chastity as sacred trusts from God?  
- What habits help me counsel with the Lord daily (prayer, scripture, gratitude), and which habits pull my gaze away from Him?  
- When have I felt the loss or the return of the Holy Ghost’s influence, and what did I learn about repentance?  
- How can I help my family use modern “Liahonas” (scriptures, prophetic counsel, FSY) to point us to Christ?  
- What would making God my highest—not just one—priority change about my time, media, relationships, and goals this week?
