### Summary

Elder Neil L. Andersen testifies that the fulness of forgiveness for serious sins and the healing of wounds caused by others’ sins are found in the atoning love of Jesus Christ, accessed through faith in Him, honest and repairing repentance, patient trust in His timing, and earnest seeking of His comfort.

### Haiku

Wounds and sins find rest,  
In Christ’s atoning embrace—  
Grace mends, light returns.

### Key Points

1) Come unto Christ for forgiveness and healing  
- The Savior’s atoning love offers complete forgiveness for serious sins and real healing for those injured by others; both are received as we exercise faith in Him, turn from sin, and yield our hearts to God.

2) True repentance is honest, repairing, and guided  
- Repentance includes full honesty with God, self, those harmed, and priesthood leaders; godly sorrow leads to inspired restitution (not just financial) as we humbly seek to repair what we have broken.

3) Approval and healing often come “by degrees”  
- The Lord’s relief, approval, and peace usually unfold in His timing; as we persist in faith, He eases burdens, turns sorrow to joy, and through His grace can lift even crushing guilt, as illustrated in Elder Robert E. Wells’s experience.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Enos 1:7–8 — “Lord, how is it done? … Because of thy faith in Christ.”  
- Moroni 10:32 — Deny ungodliness, love God with all your might; His grace is sufficient.  
- 2 Corinthians 7:10 — Godly sorrow works repentance unto salvation.  
- Mosiah 27:35 — “Zealously striving to repair all the injuries” done to others.  
- Mosiah 21:14–16 — The Lord was slow to hear; then eased their burdens and they prospered “by degrees.”  
- 3 Nephi 9:20 — A broken heart and a contrite spirit.  
- James 4:10 — Humble yourselves, and He shall lift you up.  
- John 16:20, 22 — Your sorrow shall be turned into joy; your joy none can take.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 84:88 — Angels round about you, to bear you up.  
- President Dallin H. Oaks: The Savior comforts, heals, and strengthens those who seek Him and ask for His help (see James 4:10).  
- Principles: Forgiveness comes from Christ (not from leaders); confession to a bishop/branch president is part of repentance; restitution is Spirit-led; healing for victims is real through Christ’s grace; approval often comes “by degrees.”

### Questions to Ponder

- What does it look like for me, right now, to “come unto Christ” with a broken heart and contrite spirit?  
- Where might complete honesty (with God, myself, those harmed, and my priesthood leader) deepen my repentance?  
- What restitution—practical, emotional, spiritual—might the Lord invite me to make to those I have hurt?  
- How have I seen the Lord’s relief and approval come “by degrees,” and how can I better trust His timing?  
- If I carry wounds from another’s sin, how can I actively seek the Savior’s comfort and allow angels to “bear [me] up”?  
- In what ways can I partner with the Lord to transform sorrow into joy without rushing or resisting His process?  
- How can our ward or family better support both repentant seekers and those who are healing from others’ sins?
