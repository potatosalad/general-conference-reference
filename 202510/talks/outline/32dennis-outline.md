### Summary

Sister J. Anette Dennis teaches that only the Savior can fully judge our personal race, so true disciples create covenant belonging by cheering, lifting, and loving others without judgment.

### Haiku

Old runner shuffles;  
crowds lift with gentle cheering—  
Christ knows our true pace.

### Key Points

1) Build covenant belonging through charity and unity  
- The Church and our homes should be cheering sections where Christlike love, encouragement, and unity strengthen every participant in the race of discipleship.

2) Withhold judgment—only the Lord sees the whole story  
- We seldom know others’ burdens, trauma, or capacity; the Savior alone knows the “degree of difficulty,” so we minister with empathy instead of labels or criticism.

3) Discipleship is becoming like Christ, not checking boxes  
- We follow the Golden Rule, love across differences, and develop Christlike attributes as we help the Lord heal and transform His people into a Zion community.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 13:34–35 — Disciples are known by their love.  
- Matthew 7:12 (Luke 6:31) — The Golden Rule.  
- Mosiah 18:8–9 — “Bear one another’s burdens.”  
- Moroni 7:45–48 — Charity, the pure love of Christ.  
- 1 Corinthians 12:12–27 — Members of the body of Christ.  
- Doctrine and Covenants 38:27 — Be one; unity in Zion.  
- Matthew 25:40 — “Unto the least of these.”  
- 1 Nephi 15:23–24; 8:19, 24, 30 — The iron rod: hold to the word of God.  
- Moses 7:18 — Zion people: of one heart and one mind.  
- Gospel principles: covenant belonging; ministering with empathy; avoiding unrighteous judgment; the gospel of becoming; unity vs. division; Christ as our compassionate Judge.

### Questions to Ponder

- Who in my ward, class, or family might feel “last on the track,” and how can I quietly cheer them on this week?  
- When have I felt judged or misunderstood, and how does that experience invite me to withhold judgment of others?  
- What small, specific actions (a seat saved, a text, sitting with someone new) could help create belonging on Sundays?  
- How can I better live the Golden Rule in conversations—especially with those who differ in culture, politics, or faith questions?  
- In what ways am I relying on checklists rather than seeking to become more like the Savior?  
- How can I recognize hidden burdens without prying and offer support that feels like charity rather than a project?  
- What divides exist in my sphere (family, quorum/class, ward), and what will I do to build unity this month?  
- How does remembering that Christ is the Judge—and He knows our “degree of difficulty”—change how I view myself and others?
