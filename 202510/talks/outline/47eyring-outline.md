### Summary

Elder Henry B. Eyring teaches that trials are loving “proving” moments from the Lord that, through faith in Jesus Christ, refine and strengthen us to bear the weight of eternal life.

### Haiku

Heat, weight, pressure press—
In Christ, the metal of faith
Hardens into trust.

### Key Points

1) Proving strengthens, not just tests: Like steel under strain, the Lord’s proving increases our spiritual capacity; if we keep working in faith, we can “do all things through Christ” who strengthens us.

2) Scriptural witnesses of refined faith: Moroni, Jacob, and Joseph Smith were each proved through loneliness, affliction, and imprisonment; enduring well invited the Holy Ghost, consecrated their afflictions for gain, and deepened their witness of Christ.

3) Christ, our perfect exemplar and strength: The Savior’s submissive Atonement in Gethsemane enables Him to succor us; as we trust Him and remain faithful, our trials become evidence of His love and shape us to “fight a good fight” and finish our course.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Philippians 4:13 — Strength through Christ in all things
- Moroni 10:3–5 — Remember God’s mercy; seek truth by the Holy Ghost
- 2 Nephi 2:1–3 — The Lord consecrates afflictions for our gain
- Doctrine and Covenants 121:1–8 — “O God, where art thou?” and “If thou endure it well…”
- Alma 7:11–12 — Christ knows how to succor His people
- Matthew 26:39; Luke 22:42 — “Nevertheless not my will, but thine, be done”
- 2 Timothy 4:7 — “I have fought a good fight… kept the faith”
- Gospel principles: Proving and sanctification; enduring well; submission to God’s will; seeking strength in Christ; personal revelation in trials; consecration of afflictions

### Questions to Ponder

- What current challenge might be a “proving” moment the Lord is using to strengthen me?
- How can I better “go to work” in faith when I feel overwhelmed or tempted to quit?
- In what ways have I felt the Savior’s succor during my Gethsemane-like moments?
- How can I let the Lord consecrate my afflictions for my gain rather than grow bitter?
- Which example—Moroni, Jacob, or Joseph in Liberty Jail—most guides me right now, and why?
- What practices (prayer, scripture, covenants, service) help me endure trials well?
- How can I recognize the Holy Ghost’s witness and comfort more readily during adversity?
- Who around me is being proved, and how can I minister to strengthen their faith in Christ?
