### Summary

Brother Chad H. Webb teaches that when we focus on Jesus Christ, teach true doctrine, and come as diligent learners, we invite the Holy Ghost—the perfect Tutor—to deepen faith and edify all.

### Haiku

Spirit is our guide  
Learn, teach truth, centered on Christ—  
All are edified

### Key Points

1) Invite diligent learning: Shift from teacher-centered delivery to learner-driven discipleship—prepare beforehand, seek personal revelation, share insights, and act in faith so the Spirit can confirm truth.

2) Teach true doctrine: Anchor discussions in the scriptures and the words of living prophets, avoid speculation or sensationalism, and trust that God’s word answers questions of the soul.

3) Focus on Jesus Christ: Center every lesson on the Savior—His titles, roles, attributes, and Atonement—seeing Him in all scripture and viewing life through the Father’s plan, that we may always have His Spirit.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 14:26 — The Comforter “shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 42:14 — We teach by the Spirit, “given by the prayer of faith.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 50:22; 88:122 — “That all may be edified of all”; teacher and learner rejoice together.
- John 7:16 — “My doctrine is not mine, but his that sent me.”
- 1 Nephi 17–18 — Nephi’s shipbuilding points to God’s power to strengthen and deliver.
- Alma 34:15 — “Faith unto repentance” through the atoning mercy of Christ.
- Doctrine and Covenants 20:77, 79 — Sacrament prayers: that we may always have His Spirit to be with us.
- Teaching in the Savior’s Way (Church manual) — Principles that invite the Holy Ghost in learning and teaching.
- President Russell M. Nelson, “Revelation for the Church, Revelation for Our Lives” (Apr 2018) — Increasing capacity to receive personal revelation.

### Questions to Ponder

- How will I prepare before Sunday so I come as a diligent learner ready to receive revelation?
- What questions am I bringing to my scripture study this week, and how will I record and act on the promptings I receive?
- In my teaching, how can I better root discussion in scripture and living prophets and avoid speculation?
- Where do I see Jesus Christ—His attributes, titles, and roles—in the passages I’m studying now?
- Which covenant habits help me “always have his Spirit to be with [me],” and how can I strengthen them?
- When have I been “edified” both in teaching and in receiving (D&C 50:22), and how can I foster that in my home or class?
- What principle am I currently applying so the Spirit can reconfirm its truth in my life?
- What is one specific way I can help someone else feel seen, loved, and needed in our learning setting this week?
