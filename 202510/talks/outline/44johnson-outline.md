### Summary

Elder Johnson teaches that ministering to the one with Christlike love—without judgment—invites individuals to come unto Christ and to the temple, and as we live the doctrine of Christ, remember our spiritual witnesses, and rely on sacred ordinances, we gain power to become His devoted disciples.

### Haiku

One heart at a time,  
Living water draws us home—  
Temple light restores.

### Key Points

1. Minister in the Savior’s way leads to the temple: The Lord’s pattern is personal, compassionate, and nonjudgmental; like the Samaritan woman and the man healed by Peter and John, true ministering helps the one come unto Christ and worship in His house.
2. Remember your witness amid questions: When we feel unseen or unsettled, turning to prayer and scripture allows the Spirit to help us remember prior confirmations (D&C 6:21–23), restoring peace and anchoring us in Christ.
3. Live the doctrine of Christ to gain spiritual momentum: Daily Book of Mormon study, weekly sacrament, and temple worship increase charity, humility, and divine power, enabling us to love, lift, and invite others to the Lord.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 4:10–14 — “Living water” to the woman of Samaria; Christ sees and redeems the one.  
- Acts 3:1–10 — Peter and John heal the lame man and lead him into the temple.  
- D&C 6:21–23 — Remembering spiritual witnesses brings peace.  
- John 14:1; 16:33 — “Let not your heart be troubled… be of good cheer.”  
- D&C 84:20 — In ordinances, the power of godliness is manifest.  
- 2 Nephi 31 — The doctrine of Christ (faith, repentance, baptism, Holy Ghost, enduring).  
- Gospel principles: Ministering in love without judgment; covenant belonging; spiritual momentum; the role of the Holy Ghost; the temple as a place to know Christ; the Savior heals—our job is to love.  
- Related counsel: President Russell M. Nelson on temple worship and spiritual momentum; Elder Dale G. Renlund on Christ healing and our charge to love and minister.

### Questions to Ponder

- Who is “the one” the Lord is placing on my path right now, and how can I minister without judgment?  
- How can my ministering naturally invite someone to worship in the temple (e.g., family history help, a temple open house, sharing personal temple blessings)?  
- When have I felt the Spirit speak peace to my mind? How can I better remember and record those witnesses during times of doubt?  
- What specific changes to my daily Book of Mormon study and weekly sacrament worship would increase my capacity to love and lift?  
- How does knowing “the Savior heals; our job is to love” shape my expectations and patience in ministering?  
- What compassionate questions could I ask someone who feels they don’t belong, so they feel seen and valued in Christ?
