### Summary

Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf teaches that discipleship requires steady, disciplined effort to use and magnify God-given gifts while trusting Jesus Christ to perfect us—one faithful step at a time.

### Haiku

Daily, hearts align  
Use gifts, nourish living faith—  
Christ perfects our steps.

### Key Points

1) Discipleship takes discipline  
   Faith is a daily, deliberate choice that must be nourished through action; like the parable of the talents, unused gifts and testimonies diminish, while exercised faith grows.

2) You are gifted  
   Every person receives spiritual gifts—often quiet and simple—that matter profoundly to the Lord’s work; recognize and use them to bless others.

3) Do your little part—consistently  
   Growth in Christ is gradual but relentless; turn from Babylon to Zion, act today, and trust the Savior’s grace to refine and ultimately perfect you in Him.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 25:14–30 — Parable of the talents: magnify what God entrusts to you.
- Doctrine and Covenants 46:11–12 — “To every person is given a gift by the Spirit of God.”
- Moroni 10:8, 32 — “Deny not the gifts of God” and be “perfected in Christ.”
- Alma 37:6–7 — “By small and simple things are great things brought to pass.”
- Alma 32:27–43 — Nourishing the word/faith so it grows.
- Ether 12:4 — Hope in Christ as an anchor.
- Doctrine and Covenants 4:2 — Serve with all your “heart, might, mind and strength.”
- Doctrine and Covenants 133:5, 14 — “Go ye out from Babylon” toward Zion.
- Gospel principles: Daily discipleship, spiritual gifts, incremental sanctification, grace in Christ, covenant loyalty.

### Questions to Ponder

- What daily practices best nourish my faith so it does not “cool” with time?  
- Which spiritual gifts has the Lord already given me, including simple or quiet ones, and how am I using them?  
- Where have I “buried” a talent out of fear or comparison, and what is one small act I can take to put it to work?  
- How can I better turn from Babylon (distractions, pride, worldliness) and set my heart toward Zion this week?  
- When my faith feels small, what does “begin with a hope in Christ” look like in action?  
- How can I balance diligent effort with trusting the Savior’s grace rather than perfectionism or discouragement?  
- Who in my family, quorum/class, or neighborhood could be blessed by my specific gifts this week?  
- What consistent, simple habit (scripture study, prayer, service, temple or family history, ministering) will I recommit to today?
