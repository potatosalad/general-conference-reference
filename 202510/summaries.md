1. Introduction - President Dallin H. Oaks

We now go forward with this semiannual general conference, proceeding as planned and with speakers and music assigned by President Nelson after months of preparation.

President Dallin H. Oaks explains that, following President Russell M. Nelson’s passing, general conference will proceed as President Nelson planned, funeral services will follow soon after, tributes in conference will be brief, and we honor the prophet by sustaining the Lord’s work with order and faith in Christ.

2. Sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers - Presented by Elder Henry B. Eyring



Elder Henry B. Eyring presented the sustaining of General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers by common consent, affirming the Apostles as prophets, seers, and revelators and noting newly sustained Area Seventies.

3. Blessed Are the Peacemakers - Elder Gary E. Stevenson

Peacemaking still begins in the most basic place—in our hearts. Then in homes and families.

Elder Gary E. Stevenson teaches that peacemaking is a Christlike, courageous discipline that begins in our hearts, extends to our homes, and radiates into our communities—where we choose kindness, forgiveness, and bridge-building, including online.

4. Tune Your Heart to Jesus Christ: The Sacred Gift of Primary Music - Sister Tracy Y. Browning

Primary songs are sermons for disciples of Jesus Christ, testimonies to the truthfulness of the restored gospel, and prayers set to music.

Primary music is a sacred, Spirit-filled tool that teaches doctrine, imprints truth on tender hearts, and helps children remember covenants and testify of Jesus Christ throughout their lives.

5. The Lord Looketh on the Heart - Elder Ronald M. Barcellos

All God needs to accomplish His purposes and to help us become who He wants is for each of us to turn our hearts fully to Him.

Elder Ronald M. Barcellos teaches that the Lord looks on the heart—our desires and motives—and invites us to turn our hearts fully to Jesus Christ through sincere discipleship, self-assessment, and covenant living so He can give us a new heart.

6. Know Who You Really Are - Elder Brik V. Eyre

Regardless of where we are on our path of discipleship, our lives will fundamentally change if we better understand who we really are.

Elder Brik V. Eyre teaches that truly knowing we are beloved children of Heavenly Father—validated by personal prayer and a personal relationship with Jesus Christ—changes everything about how we face trials, repent, and move forward in discipleship.

7. Be Reconciled to God - Elder Kelly R. Johnson

Reconciliation to God, through Jesus Christ’s Atonement, leads to unshakable faith.

Elder Kelly R. Johnson teaches that reconciliation with God—made possible through Jesus Christ’s Atonement and sought by daily repentance, covenants, revelation, and heeding living prophets—aligns our will with His and results in unshakable faith.

8. Do Your Part with All Your Heart - Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf

Trust the Savior and engage, patiently and diligently, in doing your part with all your heart.

Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf teaches that discipleship requires steady, disciplined effort to use and magnify God-given gifts while trusting Jesus Christ to perfect us—one faithful step at a time.

9. The Family Proclamation—Words from God - Elder Ronald A. Rasband

The proclamation has a divine origin, so we must treat it with the reverence deserving words from God.

Elder Rasband testifies that The Family: A Proclamation to the World is revelation from God, reaffirms its doctrinal authority and timeless principles, and invites all—especially those who feel they don’t fit—to turn to Jesus Christ, live as equal partners in families, and find peace on the covenant path.

10. That All May Be Edified - Brother Chad H Webb

As we focus on Jesus Christ, teach His doctrine, and learn diligently, we invite the Holy Ghost to deepen our faith in Jesus Christ and help us become more like Him.

Brother Chad H. Webb teaches that when we focus on Jesus Christ, teach true doctrine, and come as diligent learners, we invite the Holy Ghost—the perfect Tutor—to deepen faith and edify all.

11. Humble Souls at Altars Kneel - Elder Jeremy R. Jaggi

As we make and honor our covenants, we bind ourselves to the Savior, gaining greater access to His mercy, protection, sanctification, healing, and rest.

Elder Jeremy R. Jaggi teaches that sacrament tables and temple altars point us to Jesus Christ’s Atonement; as we humbly make and keep covenants, we are bound to Him and receive His mercy, protection, sanctification, healing, and rest.

12. The Eternal Gift of Testimony - Elder Kevin G. Brown

Every son or daughter of God can gain a deeper, firmer, and surer knowledge for themselves.

Elder Kevin G. Brown teaches that God lovingly gives spiritual gifts, agency empowers us to choose Christ repeatedly, and a sure testimony—received through the Holy Ghost—must be consciously chosen, nurtured, and defended throughout life.

13. No One Sits Alone - Elder Gerrit W. Gong

Living the gospel of Jesus Christ includes making room for all in His restored Church.

Elder Gerrit W. Gong teaches that the restored gospel establishes a covenant culture of belonging in which we actively gather, welcome, and minister so that no one sits alone—physically, emotionally, or spiritually—and all can find forgiveness and a place at the Lord’s table.

14. Simplicity in Christ - Elder Michael Cziesla

Applying the doctrine of Christ in a simplified and focused way will help us to find joy in our daily lives.

Elder Michael Cziesla teaches that the doctrine of Christ is beautifully simple—lived through small and simple, covenant-centered acts that focus us on Jesus Christ, bring daily joy, and, over time, unlock promised blessings.

15. The Lord Is Hastening His Work - Elder Quentin L. Cook

As the Lord hastens His work, we should love, nurture, and serve those who accept His gospel.

Elder Quentin L. Cook testifies that the Lord is hastening His work worldwide and urges all Saints to lovingly welcome, nurture, and involve new and returning members as we unite in Zion and progress on the covenant path centered in temple ordinances and daily discipleship.

16. Jesus Christ and Your New Beginning - Elder Patrick Kearon

All of us can have a new beginning through, and because of, Jesus Christ. Even you.

Elder Kearon teaches that because of Jesus Christ’s infinite goodness, Atonement, and Resurrection, every one of us can receive as many new beginnings as we need through repentance, covenants, and His enabling grace.

17. Cheering Each Other On - Sister J. Anette Dennis

Only the Lord fully knows our individual limitations and capacity, and because of that, He is the only one fully qualified to judge our performance.

Sister J. Anette Dennis teaches that only the Savior can fully judge our personal race, so true disciples create covenant belonging by cheering, lifting, and loving others without judgment.

18. “Lovest Thou Me?” - Elder Steven C. Barlow

If we want to show our love for God, we should understand how He recognizes our love.

Elder Steven C. Barlow teaches that God recognizes our love when we put Him first, love and minister to His children, live with gratitude, and choose willing, affectionate obedience—by doing so, we also more clearly perceive His love for us.

19. Remembering the Sheep - Elder William K. Jackson

The principle of counting and accounting works. It is the Lord’s way.

Elder William K. Jackson teaches that Christlike ministering means counting and accounting by name so every soul is noticed, nourished, and led to the Savior through inspired councils, stewardships, and tools.

20. The Atoning Love of Jesus Christ - Elder Neil L. Andersen

Healing and forgiveness are each found in their fulness in the atoning love of Jesus Christ.

Elder Neil L. Andersen testifies that the fulness of forgiveness for serious sins and the healing of wounds caused by others’ sins are found in the atoning love of Jesus Christ, accessed through faith in Him, honest and repairing repentance, patient trust in His timing, and earnest seeking of His comfort.

21. And Now I See - Elder Jeffrey R. Holland

The impact of the Book of Mormon in my life is no less miraculous than was the application of spit and dirt placed on the blind man’s eyes.

Elder Holland testifies that God manifests His truth and healing through simple, divinely appointed means—ordinances, priesthood authority, temple work for the living and the dead, and especially the Book of Mormon—which provide real evidence of the Restoration and give spiritual sight in Christ.

22. Go and Do Likewise - Elder James E. Evanson

I would like to highlight those missionaries who are called to service assignments. They are examples to us.

Elder James E. Evanson teaches that Christlike service—modeled powerfully by service missionaries—opens hearts to the gospel, enables everyone to minister regardless of circumstance, and brings the Savior’s healing power into our lives, inviting us to “go, and do thou likewise.”

23. Adorned with the Virtue of Temperance - Elder Ulisses Soares

I extend an earnest invitation to all of us to adorn our minds and hearts with the Christlike virtue of temperance.

Elder Soares teaches that temperance—a Christlike, Spirit-given self-mastery—reinforces our spiritual foundation in Jesus Christ, harmonizes other virtues, and protects us from the erosion of anger, pride, and excess.

24. The Power of Ministering to the One - Elder Peter M. Johnson

As we minister to the one, we invite the one to come unto Christ and to worship in the house of the Lord.

Elder Johnson teaches that ministering to the one with Christlike love—without judgment—invites individuals to come unto Christ and to the temple, and as we live the doctrine of Christ, remember our spiritual witnesses, and rely on sacred ordinances, we gain power to become His devoted disciples.

25. Look to God and Live - Elder D. Todd Christofferson

It is only by looking to God that individuals, families, and even nations can flourish.

Elder D. Todd Christofferson teaches that true healing, guidance, and flourishing—now and eternally—come as we look to God by turning to Jesus Christ, keeping covenants, heeding prophetic counsel, and making Him our highest priority.

26. Prophets of God - Sister Andrea Muñoz Spannaus

To listen to and obey the voice of the prophet is crucial to holding on to every good thing until the Second Coming.

Sister Andrea Muñoz Spannaus invites youth (and parents) to gain a personal, Spirit-confirmed testimony of living prophets and to heed their unified counsel—like the woman of Shunem welcomed Elisha—so we can hold fast to every good thing as we prepare for the Savior’s Second Coming.

27. Proved and Strengthened in Christ - Elder Henry B. Eyring

Proving moments are not evidence that the Lord has abandoned you. Rather, they are evidence that He loves you enough to refine and strengthen you.

Elder Henry B. Eyring teaches that trials are loving “proving” moments from the Lord that, through faith in Jesus Christ, refine and strengthen us to bear the weight of eternal life.

28. They Are Their Own Judges - Elder David A. Bednar

If we have exercised faith in Jesus Christ, made and kept covenants with God, and repented of our sins, the judgment bar will be pleasing.

Elder Bednar teaches that because of the Father’s plan, our moral agency, and the Savior’s Atonement, the Final Judgment can be “pleasing” as we become righteous through faithful choices, covenants, repentance, and godly fear—ultimately acknowledging what we have chosen to become.

29. The Name by Which Ye Are Called - Elder B. Corey Cuvelier

What does it mean to be called by the name of Christ?

Elder B. Corey Cuvelier teaches that to be called by the name of Christ means centering our identity in Him by making and keeping covenants, remembering Him, standing as His witnesses, and quietly doing good that blesses generations.

30. Forsake Not Your Own Mercy - Elder Matthew S. Holland

You have immediate access to divine help and healing despite your human flaws.

Elder Matthew S. Holland teaches that, like Jonah, we live in a fallen world and often falter, but if we refuse the “lying vanities,” turn to Christ, and honor our covenants (temple and service), we will receive His immediate, personalized mercy and find joy even amid trials.

31. Smiling Faces and Grateful Hearts - Elder Carlos A. Godoy

The greatness of our Saints in Africa becomes even more evident as they face life’s challenges and the demands of a growing Church.

Elder Carlos A. Godoy testifies that Saints in Africa exemplify Christ-centered joy and gratitude amid real hardship, showing that as we focus on Jesus Christ and His Atonement, He succors us and makes our burdens light.

32. Taking on the Name of Jesus Christ - Elder Dale G. Renlund

The more we identify with and remember Jesus Christ, the more we want to be like Him.

Elder Dale G. Renlund teaches that taking upon ourselves the name of Jesus Christ means identifying with Him, remembering Him, becoming like Him, aligning with His work, and thereby receiving empowering covenant blessings.

33. The Good News Recipe - Elder John D. Amos

What might it look like to add more Jesus Christ into your life?

Elder John D. Amos teaches that the “Good News Recipe” for happy living is found in the scriptures, with Jesus Christ as the essential “secret ingredient,” and invites us to add more of Him through charity, small and simple actions, and personal revelation.

34. The Book of Mormon—an Immeasurable Treasure on Our Journey - Elder Ozani Farias

As we feast upon the words of Christ found in the Book of Mormon, the Spirit will help us understand eternal truths.

Elder Ozani Farias testifies that the Book of Mormon is an immeasurable treasure that deepens our conversion to Jesus Christ as we study it diligently, feast upon its words, and bear testimony by sharing it with others.

35. The Family-Centered Gospel of Jesus Christ - President Dallin H. Oaks

Our doctrine and our belief in eternal families strengthen and bond us.

President Oaks teaches that the gospel of Jesus Christ is family-centered and temple-anchored, calling Latter-day Saints to resist societal declines in marriage and childbearing by teaching, bonding, and serving within their families, while noting a pause in new temple announcements to focus on providing ordinances worldwide.
