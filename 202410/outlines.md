[https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/10/11eyring?lang=eng](https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org//study/general-conference/2024/10/11eyring?lang=eng)

### Summary

President Henry B. Eyring presented and received the sustaining vote for General Authorities, Area Seventies, and General Officers of the Church, reaffirming the support and recognition of their roles by the members.

### Haiku

Sustain leaders high,  
With faith and unity strong,  
Guide through inspired eyes.

### Key Points

1. **Sustaining Leadership**: Members were invited to sustain the First Presidency and the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles as prophets, seers, and revelators, reaffirming their essential roles in the Church.
2. **New Area Seventies**: The presentation included the sustaining of six new Area Seventies, highlighting the Church's ongoing efforts to extend its leadership and support across different regions.
3. **Unified Support**: The process of sustaining Church leaders acts as a collective expression of faith, confidence, and prayerful support from Church members worldwide.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- *Doctrine and Covenants 107:22* - "Of the Melchizedek Priesthood, three Presiding High Priests, chosen by the body, appointed and ordained to that office, and upheld by the confidence, faith, and prayer of the church, form a quorum of the Presidency of the Church."
- *Doctrine and Covenants 124:39* - "Therefore, see that ye do all things according to the pattern which I have given you..."
- The principle of sustaining and supporting Church leadership as taught within the Gospel.

### Questions to Ponder

- Why is it important for members to sustain the leaders of the Church?
- How can expressing our support for Church leaders strengthen our own faith and commitment?
- In what ways can we actively demonstrate our sustaining vote in our daily lives?

Elder Neil L. Andersen explored the enduring gift of hope that grows as our faith in Jesus Christ increases, emphasizing how this divine hope can sustain us through life's trials and lead us towards eternal joy.

### Haiku

In trials, joy blooms.

1. **Hope as a Divine Gift**

3. **Temples as Houses of Hope**  
   - Temples fortify our hope and testify of life beyond this world, offering eternal blessings and confirming the significance of our covenants with God, especially during times of hardship.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Faith, Hope, and Charity**: 1 Corinthians 13:13
- **Hope in Christ**: Moroni 7:41 
- **Patient in Tribulation**: Romans 5:3-5 
- **Comfort from the Holy Ghost**: John 14:27

### Questions to Ponder

- How does hope influence the way you handle trials and challenges in life?
- In what ways have you experienced an increase in hope through strengthening your faith in Jesus Christ?
- How does attending the temple enhance your perspective on hope and eternal life?
- What can you do to ensure that hope remains a living gift in your life, despite worldly challenges and discouragements?

## 4. God’s Favourite - Elder Karl D. Hirst

Elder Karl D. Hirst emphasizes that God's love is constant and limitless, urging individuals to recognize their own worthiness of divine love amidst life's challenges and joys.

### Haiku

God’s warmth fills our hearts.

3. **Expressing and Receiving Love:** Elder Hirst encourages experimenting with different ways to feel and share God’s love, suggesting that acts of service, music, nature, and church covenants can enhance our connection to the divine.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **John 13:23**: Reference to "the disciple whom Jesus loved," affirming personal connection with Christ.
- **2 Nephi 4:30**: Nephi's declaration, "I glory in my Jesus," reflecting an intimate relationship.
- **Romans 8:35-39**: Assurance that nothing can separate us from the love of God.
- **1 John 4:7-8**: Encouragement to love one another as God loves us.

### Questions to Ponder

- How do you personally relate to the idea of being God’s "favourite" in terms of divine love?
- In what ways have you felt God's love in your life, and how can you share that love with others?
- What are the barriers that sometimes prevent you from feeling loved by God, and how can you overcome them?
- How can you use your own experiences to help others feel the love of God in their lives?
