### Summary

Elder Gary E. Stevenson discusses the significance of past and future decades in the history of the Church, emphasizing upcoming opportunities for Latter-day Saints to share the gospel through unprecedented experiences and service.

### Haiku

Glad tidings we share,  
A decade to be remembered,  
Serve in joy and light.

### Key Points

1. **Historical Reflection**: Elder Stevenson highlights the miraculous events from 1820 to 1830, such as Joseph Smith's vision and the restoration of the priesthood, as foundational and never to be forgotten moments in Church history.
   
2. **Future Opportunities**: Looking towards 2024 to 2034, he describes it as a decade of extraordinary opportunities for Church members to serve, unite, and share the gospel globally through events like temple rededications, the Church's bicentennial, and the Winter Olympics in Salt Lake City.
   
3. **Call to Action for Youth**: He encourages the rising generation to embrace four key responsibilities—living the gospel, caring for others, sharing the gospel, and uniting families through temple work—as a way to create memorable and uplifting experiences.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Doctrine and Covenants 6:9** - Discusses the importance of bringing forth the light by proclaiming the gospel.
- **Isaiah 2:2** - Reference to the prophecy of the Lord’s house being established as the center of gathering.
- **2 Nephi 2:25** - "Men are that they might have joy" relates to the principle of joy through the gospel.
- **Plan of Salvation** - Discussion of the Atonement as central to God’s plan and achieving eternal joy.
  
### Questions to Ponder

- How can reflecting on the Church's foundational decade (1820-1830) inspire our actions today?
- In what ways can we prepare individually and collectively for the opportunities the next decade will bring?
- How might living the four divinely appointed responsibilities influence your life and those around you?
- What steps can you take now to ensure that the upcoming decade becomes one of service and sharing for you?
