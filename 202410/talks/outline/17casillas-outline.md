### Summary

Elder Gregorio E. Casillas emphasizes that Jesus Christ constantly calls and uses us to assist in bringing His children closer to Him as we act as His disciples and minister to others.

### Haiku

Shepherd knows His flock,  
Calls us to serve with His love,  
Hearts touched, burdens light.

### Key Points

1. **Divine Preparation for Discipleship**: We are reserved for these latter days and must learn to be faithful disciples of Jesus Christ, recognizing our divine potential and spiritual uniqueness.
2. **Ministering with Love**: Through ministering as Christ's humble servants, we help others feel Heavenly Father's love, encouraging discipleship and covenant faithfulness.
3. **Endurance Through Service**: By serving others, we gather testimonies that bolster our faith, and in this process, we also receive the Savior's grace and strength in our challenges.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **John 10:14** - "I am the good shepherd, and know my sheep, and am known of mine."
- **Doctrine and Covenants 58:42** - "As oft as they repent and seek forgiveness, with real intent, they are forgiven."
- **Alma 7:11-13** - Christ understands our pains and challenges, encouraging us to follow His path.
- **Elder Dieter F. Uchtdorf, "Lift Where You Stand"** - Discusses the importance of serving and supporting those around us.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I cultivate a personal relationship with the Savior, understanding that He knows me by name?
- In what ways can I better minister to those around me, particularly in silent, meaningful acts?
- How do we overcome feelings of doubt or the adversary’s traps, and find hope through service and discipleship?
