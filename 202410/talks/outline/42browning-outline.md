### Summary

Sister Tracy Y. Browning explains that our sincere gospel questions can provide opportunities for growth through obedience and trust in God.

### Haiku

Questions guide our path,  
Line by line, truth is revealed,  
Trust leads to insight.

### Key Points

1. **Value of Questions**: Questions are a natural part of our spiritual journey and encourage growth as we seek answers with real intent and obedience.
2. **Line Upon Line**: Understanding often comes gradually; obedience and patience prepare us for further light and insight from God, beyond our current understanding.
3. **Profound Trust**: Deep trust in the Lord, akin to Naaman's obedience, is essential for spiritual healing and growth, as God’s plan often surpasses our understanding.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- *Matthew 7:7-8*: "Ask, and it shall be given you; seek, and ye shall find; knock, and it shall be opened unto you."
- *Isaiah 40:31*: "They that wait upon the Lord shall renew their strength."
- Moses 5:5-6: Adam’s obedience to God’s commandments before understanding their purpose.
- Elder Richard G. Scott’s teachings on faith and trust in the Lord.

### Questions to Ponder

- What gospel questions have you felt prompted to seek answers to recently, and how are you approaching that process?
- In what ways can you be more patient and trust in the Lord’s timetable for revealing His wisdom to you?
- How does obedience to gospel principles influence your ability to receive answers to spiritual questions?
- How do you exercise trust in the Lord when answers to prayers or questions seem delayed?
