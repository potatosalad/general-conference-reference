### Summary

President Dallin H. Oaks encourages followers of Christ to embrace a lifestyle defined by continuous commitment to His teachings, emphasizing the importance of love, avoiding contention, and being peacemakers in all interactions.

### Haiku

Follow Christ always,  
Love binds us to God above,  
Choose peace, cease dispute.

### Key Points

1. **Continuous Commitment**: Following Christ involves consistently living His teachings and commandments, rooted in love for God and others, as a constant guide in every aspect of life.
   
2. **Doctrine of Christ**: Essential elements such as faith, repentance, and enduring to the end are permanent commandments to be followed, while temporary directives may change as circumstances require.
   
3. **Peacemaking and Avoiding Contention**: Disciples should strive to be peacemakers by avoiding contention, following the teachings of Jesus, and demonstrating love even towards adversaries.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Matthew 22:37-39 - The great commandment to love God and neighbors.
- 3 Nephi 11:29-30 - Avoiding the spirit of contention.
- Matthew 5:9 - "Blessed are the peacemakers."
- President Russell M. Nelson's teachings on compassion and peacemaking.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I strengthen my continuous commitment to follow Christ in everyday situations?
- In what ways can I actively choose to be a peacemaker in my family and community?
- What steps can I take to replace contention with love and understanding in my interactions?
- How can I better demonstrate love for God and my fellow men?
