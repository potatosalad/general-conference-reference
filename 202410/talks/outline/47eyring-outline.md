### Summary

President Henry B. Eyring emphasizes the power and simplicity of teaching the doctrine of Jesus Christ, emphasizing repentance, faith, baptism, the gift of the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end as essential principles for spiritual safety and family strength.

### Haiku

Simple teachings pure,  
Heavenly strength through His word,  
Hearts and minds are turned.

### Key Points

1. **Simplified Doctrine**: Emphasizes the importance of teaching the simple principles of the gospel—faith in Jesus Christ, repentance, baptism, receiving the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end—as fundamental to drawing closer to God and protecting families from worldly influences.
   
2. **Power of the Word**: Illustrates the effectiveness and necessity of the word of God, as taught by Jesus and prophets like Alma, in fortifying the faithful against adversity, often more powerful than other means.
   
3. **Teaching by Example**: Encourages living examples of gospel teachings as Mary Bommeli did, asserting that even small teachings can be profoundly influential over time, with an emphasis on prayer and reliance on God for support and patience in teaching efforts.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Alma 31:5**: The word of God has a powerful effect on people.
- **Doctrine and Covenants 18**: Outlines the foundational principles of faith, repentance, baptism, receiving the Holy Ghost, and enduring to the end.
- **Isaiah 40:31**: Strength and resilience come from waiting on the Lord.
- **Alma 37:36-37**: Importance of daily prayer and reliance on God.
- **Stories of Alma the Younger, sons of Mosiah, and Enos**: The impact of remembered teachings in times of spiritual crisis.
