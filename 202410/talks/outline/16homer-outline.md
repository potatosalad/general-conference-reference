### Summary

Elder David P. Homer emphasizes the importance of trusting God in all decisions and acknowledges that God’s eternal truths are absolute, reminding us to submit our will to His for ultimate growth and peace.

### Haiku

Trust Him, make the leap,  
To God's truth we must hold firm,  
Faith in Him brings peace.

### Key Points

1. **Trust in Divine Guidance**: Elder Homer highlights the importance of trusting in God's will even when it contradicts our personal desires or societal norms, using Louisa Pratt's journey as an example of faith and divine trust.

2. **Divine Truths Are Absolute**: He explains that God's commandments and doctrines are not subjective to personal or societal preferences but are based on eternal truths that bring blessings when adhered to.

3. **Submitting to God's Will**: The act of submitting our will to God’s is a demonstration of faith, allowing the Holy Ghost to guide us towards joy, peace, and hope, as we align ourselves with His divine plan.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Alma 37:37**: Encouragement to counsel with the Lord in all dealings to receive divine guidance.
- **John 6:67-69**: Peter’s declaration of faith in Christ, underscoring the importance of trusting Christ as the source of eternal life.
- **D&C 6:36**: "Look unto me in every thought; doubt not, fear not.”
- **Teachings of Neal A. Maxwell**: The importance of submitting our will to God's as the ultimate offering.
