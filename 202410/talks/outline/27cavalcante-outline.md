### Summary

Elder Aroldo B. Cavalcante discusses the importance of being aware of and serving those who may feel forgotten, urging us to act as winds of support and guidance, similar to the winds that guided the Jaredites to the promised land.

### Haiku

Gentle winds of love,  
Guide the weary and the lost,  
God’s hand never stops.

### Key Points

1. **Service in Forgotten Places**: Elder Cavalcante shares experiences of serving elderly residents in Brazil and encourages us to recognize and serve those who may be marginalized or forgotten in our communities.
   
2. **The Power of Simple Acts**: He illustrates how small acts of service can bring hope and facilitate others’ spiritual journeys, drawing a parallel with the Jaredites’ journey to the promised land.

3. **Missionary Work and Individual Potential**: Elder Cavalcante emphasizes the impact young people can have by serving missions and reassures them of their divine potential despite personal insecurities.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Mosiah 2:17**: "When ye are in the service of your fellow beings ye are only in the service of your God."
  
- **Ether 6:8**: The Jaredites were driven forth by the wind towards the promised land.
  
- **Jeremiah 1:7**: "Say not, I am a child: for thou shalt go to all that I shall send thee."

- The importance of recognizing the individual worth and potential of each person.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can we become more aware of those who feel forgotten in our own communities or congregations?
  
- In what ways can we be guided by the Holy Spirit to serve those in need, even if our initial resources or capabilities seem insufficient?

- How can we gain confidence in our own spiritual journey and realize our divine potential, especially in times of doubt or insecurity?
