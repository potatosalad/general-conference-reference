### Summary

Elder D. Todd Christofferson invites us to bury any forms of rebellion against God by cultivating willing hearts and minds, leading us to deeper conversion and joy.

### Haiku

Lay down the rebellion,  
Hearts and swords buried so deep,  
Choose His path, find peace.

### Key Points

1. **Burying Rebellion**: Elder Christofferson emphasizes the importance of spiritually burying our "weapons of rebellion" just as the converted Lamanites physically buried their swords, signifying a deep transformation and commitment to God.

2. **Active and Passive Rebellion**: The talk differentiates between active rebellion like Lucifer's open defiance and more passive forms seen in everyday neglect of divine guidance, urging us to choose God's way over our own.

3. **Sacred Devotion**: By prioritizing God's commandments and replacing forms of rebellion with dedicated obedience and service, we align with divine will, ensuring that our actions reflect His love and intentions.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Alma 23:7 - Laying down the weapons of rebellion.
- Ether 12:27 - Through Christ, weakeness becomes strength.
- Mosiah 5:2 - The change of heart through Christ.
- Doctrine & Covenants 58:42: - Repentance and forgiveness.

### Questions to Ponder

- What are the 'weapons of rebellion' in my own life that need to be buried?
- How can active vs. passive rebellion manifest in our daily actions and decisions?
- In what ways can I strengthen my resolve to follow God's commandments above personal desires?
- How does transforming our 'weapons of rebellion' bring us closer to experiencing divine joy and peace?
