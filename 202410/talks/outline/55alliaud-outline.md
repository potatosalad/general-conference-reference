### Summary 

Elder Rubén V. Alliaud emphasizes the powerful and joyful doctrine that we are literally children of God with the potential to become like Him, a belief fundamental to the teachings of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints.

### Haiku

Children of God's light,  
Potential divine within,  
Glorious our call.

### Key Points 

1. **Divine Nature and Potential**: Elder Alliaud teaches that understanding our literal identity as children of God grants us the potential to become like Him, which is fundamental to the teachings of the Restoration.
   
2. **Identity in Temptation and Adversity**: Experiences from Moses and Jesus Christ's life show that Satan often tempts us to question our divine nature, emphasizing the importance of understanding who we truly are.

3. **Celestial Aspirations**: By embracing our divine heritage and potential, we align with God's work and glory, aiming toward eternal life and perfection through Jesus Christ's atonement.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Moses 1:4-6** - God's declaration to Moses as His son, emphasizing divine identity.
- **Matthew 4:1-11** - The temptations of Christ, illustrating Satan's attempts to cast doubt on divine sonship.
- **Romans 8:16-17** - Affirmation that we are children and heirs of God.
- **Gospel Principle**: "Be ye therefore perfect" - A call to strive for divine perfection (Matthew 5:48).
- **President Nelson’s Talk**: "Think Celestial" - Encouragement to focus on our divine potential and eternal destiny.
