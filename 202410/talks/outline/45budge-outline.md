### Summary

Bishop L. Todd Budge encourages believers to regularly commune with God through quiet, intentional worship, akin to savoring each sacred opportunity in our spiritual lives.

### Haiku

Seek quiet moments,  
Nourish spirit, heart at peace,  
In stillness, find Him.

### Key Points

1. **Need for Spiritual Nourishment**: Emphasizes that just as we need physical nourishment, our spirits require feeding through communion with God, especially amidst our busy lives.
   
2. **Being Still**: Advocates the importance of quiet time for reflection and meditation focused on God, a practice that requires mental exertion and faith.

3. **Intentional Worship**: Urges participants to transform routine spiritual practices into mindful and engaging experiences, thus strengthening their bond with the Savior.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- Isaiah 30:15 – "In quietness and confidence shall be your strength."
- Jeremiah 29:13 – "And ye shall seek me, and find me, when ye shall search for me with all your heart."
- Encouragement from President Russell M. Nelson on savoring scriptures and maintaining quiet sacred time.
- Gospel Principle: Importance of spiritual nourishment and personal revelation.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can you create more meaningful quiet time in your life for spiritual reflection?
- In what ways have you found strength through spiritual stillness and meditation?
- How can routine practices like prayer and scripture study become more intentional and heartfelt in your daily life?
- What changes can you make to ensure you seek and find the Lord with all your heart?
