### Summary

Sister Kristin M. Yee emphasizes the Savior's power to redeem individuals from mistakes and weaknesses, guiding them toward greater potential through His love and atonement.

### Haiku

Canvas of mistakes,  
Jesus' love repairs our flaws,  
We are made anew.

### Key Points

1. **The Redeeming Power of Christ**: Sister Yee shares how through her painting experience, she learned that the Savior can make us whole and better than we were before our mistakes.

2. **Repentance as a Journey**: Emphasizing the process of repentance as ongoing, she highlights the daily opportunity to change and improve with divine assistance.

3. **Covenant Relationship**: Sister Yee stresses the importance of maintaining a covenant relationship with Christ to access His redeeming power and find hope in trials.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Luke 7:47** - "Her sins, which are many, are forgiven; for she loved much."
- **Doctrine and Covenants 84:88** - "My Spirit shall be in your hearts, and mine angels round about you."
- **Alma 5:33-34** - "Behold, he sendeth an invitation unto all men, for the arms of mercy are extended towards them."

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I more fully embrace the Savior's atonement in my life and allow it to change me?
- What steps can I take to repent sincerely and consistently, seeking to align my heart with the Savior?
- In what ways might I be unintentionally defending myself from receiving the fullness of God's love and blessings?
