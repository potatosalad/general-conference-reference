### Summary

In his talk "Ye Are My Friends," Elder David L. Buckner emphasizes that Jesus Christ calls us His friends and urges us to build higher and holier relationships with others to achieve unity and belonging among God's children.

### Haiku

Friends, not just a name,  
In Christ, unity we claim,  
Love bridges our frame.

### Key Points

1. **Christ's Friendship**: Elder Buckner reminds us that Jesus Christ repeatedly calls His followers "friends," symbolizing a sacred bond that elevates and unites us with Him and with each other.
   
2. **Unity in Diversity**: He shares examples demonstrating that despite our diverse backgrounds and differences, unity can be achieved by focusing on common spiritual goals and serving together.

3. **Belonging to Christ**: Emphasizing the importance of belonging, Buckner describes how the Savior invites all to come unto Him, providing a sense of purpose and unity, urging us to embrace everyone within the Gospel's inclusive reach.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- John 15:13 - "Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends."
- Doctrine and Covenants references on friendship, notably emphasizing that Christ calls us His friends.
- Elder Quentin L. Cook's talks on belonging and unity.
- Matthew 11:28 - "Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."

### Questions to Ponder

- How can we better embody Christ’s definition of friendship in our daily interactions?
- In what ways can we invite others into a sense of belonging, both at church and in our communities?
- How does recognizing our individual divine attributes contribute to the unity in our congregations?
