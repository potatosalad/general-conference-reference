### Summary

Elder Brook P. Hales contends that despite life's challenges, Heavenly Father's plan for us is designed for success and growth through mortality.

### Haiku

Mortality's test,  
With grace and strength, we prevail—  
Trust in God's design.

### Key Points

1. **Life's Trials**: Experiencing life's challenges, such as grief, despair, and difficult circumstances, are part of God's plan, designed to refine us and help us grow.
   
2. **God's Assurance**: Despite the trials, we should take comfort in knowing that our Heavenly Father and Savior are aware of us, and through covenants, we can overcome challenges.

3. **Personal Witness**: Elder Hales shares personal experiences, affirming that through the Atonement of Jesus Christ, we can find worth, healing, and understanding in God's plan for us.

### Scriptures and Gospel Principles

- **Moses 1:39**: Heavenly Father's work is to bring about immortality and eternal life.
- **2 Corinthians 12:7–9**: Paul’s experience with a "thorn in the flesh,” and the Lord's assurance that His grace is sufficient.
- **Mosiah 23:22**: Trust in the Lord during difficult times, and you'll be lifted up.
- **2 Nephi 2:2**: Afflictions can be consecrated for our gain.

### Questions to Ponder

- How can I better understand and support others who are experiencing difficult trials?
- In what ways can my personal challenges be seen as part of God's design to refine and bless me?
- How does the Atonement of Jesus Christ provide healing and worth in my own life experiences?
